Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)

🔴 Very Important for Exam
GAAP refers to the rules or guidelines adopted for recording and reporting business transactions, to bring uniformity in preparation and presentation of financial statements. AICPA: 'Principle' = a general law or rule adopted as a guide to action

Fundamental Accounting Equation

🔴 Basis of the entire Double Entry System
Assets = Liabilities + CapitalExpressed from the Dual Aspect (Duality) Concept · Detailed in Chapter 3

Every transaction has a two-fold effect and touches at least two accounts — this equality never breaks, whatever the transaction.

2.5

Accounting Standards

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Accounting Standard: a written policy document covering recognition, measurement, treatment, presentation & disclosure of accounting transactions — issued as an authoritative statement by ICAI.
Accounting Standards
BENEFITS
  • Eliminates variation in treatment
  • Forces disclosure beyond law
  • Enables comparability (inter/intra firm)
LIMITATIONS
  • Difficult choice between alternatives
  • Rigid — little flexibility
  • Cannot override statute/law
APPLICABLE TO
  • Sole proprietorship, Partnership
  • Societies, Trusts, HUF, AOP
  • Co-operative societies, Companies
  • Not purely charitable orgs
📝 Probable Exam QuestionState any three benefits and two limitations of Accounting Standards.

Why IFRS / Global Convergence?

  • Prevents manipulation of figures via consistent recognition/measurement rules
  • Promotes global harmonisation of accounting rules across countries
  • Facilitates global investment & comparability for international investors
  • India's converged standard = Ind AS, notified by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs

Goods and Services Tax (GST)

🟠 Frequently Asked — "One Nation One Tax"
GST is a destination-based tax on consumption of goods & services, levied at every stage of value addition, with credit of tax paid at previous stages available as set-off. Burden ultimately borne by the final consumer.
Q: Is the transaction Intra-State or Inter-State?
INTRA-STATE → CGST (to Centre) + SGST (to State)
e.g. Punjab dealer → Punjab buyer
INTER-STATE → IGST (shared Centre + State)
e.g. MP dealer → Rajasthan buyer

Worked Example

Ramesh (Punjab) sells goods worth ₹10,000 to Seema (Punjab). GST rate 18% = 9% CGST + 9% SGST → ₹900 to Centre, ₹900 to Punjab Govt.

Ramesh (MP) sells goods worth ₹10,00,000 to Anand (Rajasthan) — inter-state → entire 18% (₹1,80,000) charged as IGST, going to the Centre for apportionment.

Key Characteristics

  • Common law & procedure nationwide, single administration
  • Destination-based, levied at point of consumption
  • Comprehensive levy on both goods & services with input tax credit
  • Maximum of two tax rate slabs; no cess/resale/turnover tax
  • No multiple levies (sales tax, entry tax, octroi, entertainment tax abolished)

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
"Big Money Goes And Comes, Daily Reports Matter For Consistent, Conservative, Material, Objective books"
Business entity, Money measurement, Going concern, Accounting period, Cost, Dual aspect, Revenue recognition, Matching, Full disclosure, Consistency, Conservatism, Materiality, Objectivity — all 13 concepts of 2.2, in order.
A = L + C
The accounting equation itself is the best mnemonic — Assets always equal Liabilities plus Capital. Say "A firm owns what it Owes + what the Owner put in."
"No profit till sold, no loss left unrecorded"
Conservatism (Prudence): anticipate all losses, but never anticipate a gain until it is realised.
CGST + SGST = Same State; IGST = Interstate
Both start with "S" sound reminders — SGST/CGST for a Single (same) state transaction; IGST for Inter-state.
Cash = "When Money Moves"; Accrual = "When it's Due"
Cash basis records only on actual receipt/payment; Accrual basis records the moment the right/obligation arises.

Last-Day Revision Notes

  3. 03Dual Aspect → Accounting Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Capital.
  4. 04Going Concern justifies spreading asset cost over its useful life (depreciation basis).
  5. 05Conservatism: anticipate all losses, never anticipate unrealised gains.
  6. 06Consistency ≠ never change method — changes are allowed if fully disclosed.
  7. 07Double Entry = both effects recorded; Single Entry = incomplete, not a true "system."
  8. 08Cash Basis = record on payment/receipt; Accrual Basis = record on occurrence (matches revenue-expense).

  10. 10GST: Intra-state = CGST + SGST; Inter-state = IGST. Destination-based consumption tax.
