7.01 Transfer Entries — Purpose & Types 🔴 VI▾

A Transfer Entry (TE) moves an item from one head of account to another. 3 purposes:

(a) Correct classification error (b) Adjust item under Debt/Deposit/Remittance head (c) Adjust inter-departmental txns not involving cash receipt/payment

Also used when it's simpler to book multi-head items under one head initially, then split later.

AAO-in-charge of Book Section ensures recurring/periodical transfers happen regularly; Annual/half-yearly transfers avoided except listed exceptions (Annexure).

Watch of Periodical Adjustments: Broadsheet maintained showing (1) all periodical adjustments (2) responsible Section (3) due dates (4) actual adjustment date — submitted to Higher Officer after March Accounts close, and again after March Supplementary closes.

7.02 Rules for Correcting Errors 🔴 VI▾
Decision Tree — Is the error in Revenue/Expenditure head, or Debt/Deposit/Remittance head?
  • Revenue/Expenditure Head error:
    • Before year's accounts closed → correct any time
    • After year's accounts closed → no correction, only a note against original entry
    • Amount ≤ ₹100 & found before closing → no formal transfer, just a note
    • If affects State Govt/other Dept/Capital head outside Revenue A/c → always correct by transfer, however small
  • Debt/Deposit/Remittance Head error:
    • Must be corrected by transfer however old, however small
    • Year not closed → direct removal from wrong head to correct head
    • Year closed → 4 specific sub-rules apply (see below)

4 sub-rules when year is closed (Debt/Deposit/Remittance errors):

SituationCorrection
Wrong Debt/Deposit/Remittance head instead of anotherTransfer from one to the other
Credited/debited to D/D/R instead of Revenue/ExpenditureTransfer to the head it should've appeared under
Credited to Revenue instead of D/D/RDebit "Refunds," credit proper head
Debited to Expenditure instead of D/D/RDebit proper head, credit "1201-Postal Receipts-800-Other Receipts-02"

🧠 Note: Compensation for lost/damaged insured articles & Write-offs to "Profit and Loss" must always be corrected by TE, however small the amount.

Adjustment Book (Form C.P.W.A.-54) is closed on the 7th of each month (debit & credit totals); Abstract (Form DG(PA)-519) sent to Book Section by the 9th — a "Nil" abstract if no adjustments occurred.

Account Current Section: Uses "Settlement Account Abstract" — split into Receipts & Expenditure parts, separate per Circle.

Government Security Section, Kolkata: Submits Transfer Entry abstracts to other Circle PAOs by the 20th of each month (Form DG(PA)-19), signed by Group A/B Officer.

7.04 Large Transfers & Grant-Affecting Adjustments 🟡 Concept▾

Large transfers between Debt/Deposit/Remittance heads → made by deduct entry against original debit/credit (prevents exaggerating transaction totals).

Where correction affects a head for which grants were obtained, principle applies regardless of amount:

(a) Same-year misclassification → deduct entry against original debit/credit
(b) Previous-year misclassification → plus/minus credit (not affecting the year's debits); if inter-circle, both circles must adjust within the same official year.

7.05 Journal & Ledger — Purpose 🔴 VI▾

Post-CSI, Postal Wing accounts run on accrual accounting basis (previously single-entry). Journal & Ledger scientifically bring out balances where Govt acts as banker/remitter/borrower/lender — verified periodically against double-entry balances.

Journal: Form DG(PA)-561 — first 2-3 pages reserved for opening entries. Ledger: Account Code Form 54.

7 Classes of Ledger Heads:

  • I. Opening & Closing Heads — Government, Balance
  • II. Revenue Heads — 0021, 0037, 0049, 0075, 0235, 1201
  • III. Expenditure Heads — 2016, 2047, 2049, 2071, 2075, 2235, 3201/3465/2552, 5201/4465/4552
  • IV. Debt & Remittance heads closed to Government (Nil for P&T)
  • V. Debt & Remittance heads closed to Balance
  • VI. Personal Accounts of Head Postmasters per Circle (incl. Transfers)
  • VII. Postal Net Profit and Loss

🧠 "OG-Rev-Exp-DrG-DrB-PA-NPL" — the 7 ledger head classes in order (I–VII).

7.06 Opening, Monthly Entries & Ledger Posting 🟠 FAQ▾

Opening Books: Balances transferred from Balance A/c to Debt/Remittance heads & Personal A/cs, per previous year's closing — but this can't be posted until previous year's books actually close (partway through new year).

Monthly Journal entries: Dr. "L-Suspense & Misc (c) Other A/cs 8671-Departmental Balances Posts" → Cr. Total Receipts of month; and Dr. Total Expenditure → Cr. 8671. Personal A/c figures from General Statement of Disbursers' Accounts; Receipts/Expenditure figures from General Abstract.

Ledger Posting: Monthly, from major/minor head entries in General Abstract; true ledger heads = minor heads. Detailed-head figures posted only once a year, after March Supplementary closes (Form AO-54.A).

"Posts – Net Profit and Loss" head operated only after final closure (not monthly) — 1201/3201 figures carried to its credit/debit sides, distributed by Sub-Major Heads (vertical) & Minor Heads (horizontal), per Annexure-II.

7.07 Closing the Books & Trial Balance Sheet 🔴 VI▾

Books closed formally after Finance Accounts are finally printed, with Supervisory Officer (Book Section) approval.

Closed ToHeads
Government0021, 0037, 0049, 2049, 5201; also "Remittance A/c England-India," "Deposits with RBI," "A/c between Posts & Telegraphs and other Depts"
BalanceAll other Debt, Deposit, Remittance heads & Personal Accounts

"Net Profit and Loss" closed to Govt via balancing entries per Department branch (Indian + English transactions + Dividend to General Revenues).

Trial Balance Sheet (Form DG(PA)-562): Prepared annually after March Final + Supplementary closure; tests accuracy of Ledger postings. Grand Totals reconciled with Journal totals for the year. Must complete before Review of Balances (Part B, Finance Accounts) and before final account closure.

🧠 Note: Since TEs are now posted directly in-application by respective sections post-approval, a separate CTLA is not required — but each section keeps its own TE register.
