Definition परिभाषा

NPS (originally "New Pension Scheme", now "National Pension System") is a defined-contribution pension scheme for Govt. servants joining service on or after 01.01.2004.

एनपीएस (मूल रूप से "नई पेंशन योजना", अब "राष्ट्रीय पेंशन प्रणाली") 01.01.2004 या उसके बाद सेवा में शामिल होने वाले सरकारी कर्मचारियों के लिए एक अंशदान-आधारित पेंशन योजना है।

🏛️ Tier-I vs Tier-II टियर-1 बनाम टियर-2

▼

Tier-I 🔴

  • Mandatory for Govt servants joining on/after 01.01.2004
  • Exit allowed at/after 60 years age
  • Min 40% pension wealth → mandatory annuity investment at exit
  • Exit before 60 yrs → 80% mandatory investment
  • Provides lifetime pension to subscriber, then dependant

Tier-II 🟠

  • Optional, at discretion of Govt servant
  • Can continue only if Tier-I is active
  • Auto-closes when Tier-I account closes
  • One-way switch: funds can move Tier-II → Tier-I only 🟠 IP 2023
🧠 Mnemonic: "40-Stay, 80-Early-Pay" → 40% mandatory at normal exit (60 yrs), 80% mandatory if exiting early.
Probable Q: Can Tier-II continue after Tier-I closes? → No, it auto-closes 🔴

💰 Contribution Rules (from 01.04.2019) अंशदान नियम

▼
Employee10% of Pay+DA
Government14% of Pay+DA
🧠 Mnemonic: "10 by You, 14 by Govt" — Govt contributes 4% more than employee.
First Contribution — Time Limit 🔴

Credited within 20 days of application submission, OR by the last date of the month of joining — whichever is later.

"Emolument" for NPS includes: 🟠
  • Basic pay (FR 9(21)(a)(i))
  • Non-Practising Allowance (Medical Officers, in lieu of private practice)
  • Admissible Dearness Allowance
Probable Q: What % does Govt contribute to NPS Tier-I? → 14% of Pay+DA 🔴

⚠️ Contribution during Suspension, Absence & Leave निलंबन, अनुपस्थिति व अवकाश के दौरान अंशदान

▼
During Suspension 🔴
  • Subsistence allowance drawn in a month = counted as emolument
  • Subscriber's contribution: optional, at his discretion
  • If period later regularized as duty/leave: contribution difference credited with interest
  • Govt's contribution: based on subsistence allowance, UNLESS subscriber opted out of his own contribution
During Absence from Duty (no pay/leave salary) 🟠

No contribution by subscriber during such absence.

During Leave 🔴

Subscriber's Contribution

Based on leave salary payable.
Extraordinary Leave → no leave salary → NO contribution

Government's Contribution

If leave is on medical grounds, civil commotion, or higher studies (useful to official duty) and leave salary is nil/less than full pay → Govt contributes 14% of notional Pay+DA
🧠 Mnemonic: "MCH" → Medical, Civil commotion, Higher studies — the 3 leave grounds where Govt still contributes 14%.
Probable Q: Is Govt contribution made during Extraordinary Leave? → No employee contribution since no leave salary is payable 🔴

⚰️ Death & Invalidation — Option Facility मृत्यु व अशक्तता — विकल्प सुविधा

▼
Important Rule 🔴

Under Rule 10, CCS (Implementation of NPS) Rules, 2021, NPS-covered officials must exercise an option in Form 1 to choose benefits under NPS OR CCS (Pension) Rules, 1972, in case of death/invalidation.

  • One copy kept in Service Book
  • Another copy forwarded to CRA by Head of Office
Family Entitlement on Death 🔴

(A) Opted for Old Pension Scheme

Family Pension, Death Gratuity, Leave encashment, CGEGIS — same as Old Pension Scheme.
Govt's NPS contribution + returns → transferred to Govt account.
Employee's NPS contribution + returns → paid to family.

(B) NPS Corpus Benefits

PRAN closed; family gets:
• Lumpsum up to 20% of accumulated pension wealth
• Annuity from remaining wealth via ASP (per PFRDA Exit & Withdrawal Regs 2015)
Death gratuity/leave encashment/CGEGIS — same as Old Pension Scheme.
Probable Q: Max lump sum % payable to family under NPS corpus on death? → 20% of accumulated pension wealth 🔴

🚪 Exit, Deferment & Annuity Rules निकास, स्थगन एवं वार्षिकी नियम

▼
Deferment of Balance Annuity Amount 🔴
  • Can be deferred up to age 75 years
  • Must intimate Authority 15 days before superannuation retirement date
  • If subscriber dies before due date after deferment → annuity mandatorily purchased by spouse (life annuity for spouse)
Decision: Mandatory Annuity % at Exit
At/after Superannuation (60 yrs)?
YES → 40% mandatory annuity purchase. If corpus ≤ ₹5 lakh, full withdrawal allowed without annuity 🔴
NO (Before superannuation / voluntary retirement) → 80% mandatory annuity purchase. If corpus ≤ ₹2.5 lakh, full withdrawal allowed without annuity 🔴
🧠 Mnemonic: "40-5, 80-2.5" → Superannuation: 40% annuity, ₹5L exemption. Early exit: 80% annuity, ₹2.5L exemption.
Annuity Options Available 🟡
  • Default Scheme: Annuity for subscriber + spouse life, purchase price returned to family in order — dependent father → dependent mother → surviving children/legal heirs
  • Annuity for life + return of purchase price to nominee
  • Annuity for life only — no return of purchase price

सभी ASP सभी विकल्प प्रदान नहीं करते; मूल्य निर्धारण भी ASP अनुसार भिन्न होता है।

Probable Q: Deadline to intimate deferment before superannuation? → 15 days prior 🔴

💸 NPS Withdrawal Conditions एनपीएस निकासी शर्तें

▼
Important Rules 🔴
  • Withdrawal allowed only after 3 years from date of joining
  • Max withdrawal = 25% of subscriber's own contribution 🟠 IP 2023
  • Purposes allowed: children's higher education, children's marriage, construction/purchase of flat (no prior house), medical treatment (self/spouse/children/dependent parents)
  • Max 3 withdrawals allowed during entire tenure 🟠 IP 2023
  • Not allowed for: Foreign travel 🟠 IP 2023
🧠 Mnemonic: "3-25-3" → 3 years wait, 25% max withdrawal, 3 max withdrawals lifetime.
Probable Q (Actual IP 2023): For what purpose is NPS withdrawal NOT allowed? → Foreign travel 🔴

🏢 PFRDA, CRA & NSDL पीएफआरडीए, सीआरए एवं एनएसडीएल

▼
Composition of PFRDA 🟠 IP 2023

1 Chairperson + 3 whole-time members + 3 part-time members

Empanelled Annuity Service Providers (ASP) 🟡
  • Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC)
  • SBI Life Insurance Co. Ltd
  • ICICI Prudential Life Insurance Co. Ltd
  • HDFC Standard Life Insurance Co. Ltd
  • Star Union Dai-ichi Life Insurance Co. Ltd

(सूची समय-समय पर बदलती रहती है)

CRA vs NSDL — Functions 🔴

CRA

Part of Postal Accounts Office (like Accounts Branch in Head Office); compulsory unit.
Forwards all data electronically to NSDL, keeps documents itself.
Receives subscriber grievances (via Divisional Head) and forwards to NSDL.

NSDL

Private organisation, based in Mumbai.
Handles NPS-related work for all Postal Accounts Offices in India.
Communicates only PRAN number to CRA; all else (annuity payment, claim settlement) direct to subscriber via Head Office.
Subscribers cannot contact NSDL directly.
🧠 Mnemonic: "CRA keeps, NSDL speaks (only PRAN)" — CRA retains documents; NSDL only communicates PRAN back to CRA.
Probable Q: Where is NSDL located, and what does it communicate to CRA? → Mumbai; only the PRAN number 🔴
