★ One-Page Revision Sheet

Definition (Batliboi)

  • "A statement prepared with debit and credit balances of ledger accounts to test the arithmetical accuracy of the books."

Accounting Process — Step 3

  • Journal → Ledger → Trial Balance

3 Methods of Preparation

  • Balance method (most used)
  • Total method (rarely used)
  • Total & Balance method (not in practice)

Errors NOT Disclosed

  • Error of principle
  • Compensating errors
  • Complete omission of a transaction

Suspense Account Rule

  • Debit total short → debit Suspense A/c
  • Credit total short → credit Suspense A/c
  • Temporary account, closed once errors are located

Format Title

  • "Trial Balance as on ___" — the date always appears in the title

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Rules

  • Trial balance possible only if double entry system is followed.
  • Total Debit column = Total Credit column when arithmetically accurate (not conclusive proof).

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Trial balance can tally even with errors — omission, compensating errors, and errors of principle stay hidden.
  • Under Balance method: an account with zero balance is excluded from the trial balance.

📅 Important Dates

No historical date — but every trial balance itself must carry a specific "as on" date in its title.

🏛️ Important Authorities

  • J.R. Batliboi — standard definition of trial balance quoted in the syllabus.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Can be prepared on any day (month-end, quarter-end) but normally at the end of the accounting period.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — a mismatched trial balance simply signals errors to be located, not a punishable lapse.

Trial Balance
Trial balance = a statement of debit & credit balances of all ledger accounts on a particular date; checks arithmetical accuracy and facilitates preparing financial statements.
🟡 Conceptual Understanding In computerised accounting, ledger posting, trial balance and final accounts are generated simultaneously once the journal entry is recorded — so posting/arithmetic errors don't occur.
📝 Probable Question: What is trial balance? Is it a conclusive proof of accuracy? (3 marks)
Trial balance acts as a "lubricant" for the accounting cycle — it helps detect discrepancies so the cycle can be completed properly.
📝 Probable Question: Why is trial balance necessary before preparing final accounts? (2 marks)
J.R. Batliboi: "A trial balance is a statement, prepared with the debit and credit balances of the ledger accounts to test the arithmetical accuracy of the books."

📝 Probable Question: "Agreement of trial balance is not conclusive proof of accuracy." Explain. (5 marks — very frequently asked)
Balance Method
  • Records only the balance (Dr/Cr) of each account
  • Widely used — helps prepare financial statements
Total Method
  • Records total of debit side & total of credit side
  • Not commonly used
Total & Balance Method
  • 4 columns: Dr total, Cr total, Dr balance, Cr balance
  • Not in practice
Balance Method — 4 Steps
1. Calculate balances of all ledger accounts (incl. cash book)
2. Record account names + debit/credit balance in respective columns
3. Enter L.F. (ledger page number)
4. Total both columns — must be equal, else locate errors
Account TypeColumn
Sundry debtors, Cash in hand, Salary, Discount allowed, Plant & Machinery, FurnitureDebit
Sundry creditors, Bank overdraft, Discount received, Capital, SalesCredit
📝 Probable Question: Explain the balance method of preparing trial balance with its steps. (5 marks)
When errors can't be located immediately but the trial balance must still be tallied for timely financial statements, the difference is transferred to a temporary Suspense Account.
Decision Tree: Suspense Account Treatment
Debit total is LESS than credit total? → Debit the Suspense A/c with the shortfall
Credit total is LESS than debit total? → Credit the Suspense A/c with the shortfall
Errors later located and rectified? → Suspense A/c is closed / eliminated
🟡 Conceptual Understanding Suspense account is a stopgap, not a permanent account — it must eventually be cleared to zero once every error is traced and corrected (detailed rectification is Unit 9).
📝 Probable Question: What is a suspense account? When and how is it used? (5 marks)

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Trial balance is prepared on a particular date — the date must appear in the title.
  • Prepared with the help of ledger account balances.
  • Checks arithmetical accuracy of entries made in the books.
  • Under balance method, accounts with nil balance are excluded.
  • If it doesn't tally, the difference goes to a temporary Suspense Account for timely financial statement preparation.
