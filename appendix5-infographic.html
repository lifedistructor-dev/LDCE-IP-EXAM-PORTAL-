1. Classification Structure — Why & How Accounts Are Coded▶

📖 Definition | परिभाषा

🔴 Classification structure is devised to depict the total expenditure of Government on each function/programme/activity — it aids budgetary analysis, decision-making, and performance evaluation. वर्गीकरण संरचना सरकार के प्रत्येक कार्य/कार्यक्रम पर कुल व्यय को दर्शाने हेतु बनाई गई है।

🎯 Why Classification (Purpose)

  • To ensure Uniformity & standardization
  • To present objectives/purposes of govt. expenditure in terms of functions, programmes, activities
  • To bring expenditure of a function/programme together irrespective of department
  • To help management with timely accounts data for monitoring & analysis
  • To secure itemized control over expenditure

🌳 6-Tier Classification Structure (15-digit CGA code)

  • Sector (A, B, C, D... — letter of alphabet)
    • Major Head (4-digit — indicates Government function, e.g. 1201, 8446)
      • Minor Head (scheme/programme under the function)
        • Sub-Head (identifies specific scheme/organisation)
          • Detailed Head (sub-ordinate admin level / sub-scheme)
            • Object Head (nature of expenditure — salary, DTE etc. = unit of appropriation)
🧠 Mnemonic: "Scoring Marks Means Super Dedicated Objective" → Sector → Major Head → Minor Head → Sub Head → Detailed Head → Object Head.

🔢 GL Code vs CGA Head

TermDigitsPurpose
CGA Head of Account15-digitGovernment-wide classification (Sector→Object Head)
DOP ERP GL Code10-digitSystem-level code introduced post-ERP implementation, mapped 1:1 to the 15-digit CGA head

🟠 Classification to the correct Head of Account is essential — where linked to Grant, it also enforces Budget Discipline.

📌 Head-Level Definitions (Micro-Topics)

HeadWhat it Represents
Major HeadGovernment function (corresponds to Scheme classification / non-scheme sector)
Minor HeadScheme/non-scheme programme under the function — classification is by purpose, not by department incurring it
Sub-HeadIdentifies the specific scheme in pursuance of the programme (e.g. organisation)
Detailed HeadSub-ordinate administrative level / sub-scheme
Object HeadItemised control — object/nature of expenditure (salary, DTE) = unit of appropriation

🏛️ Authority

  • Errors/omissions reported to Postal Directorate (PA Wing) for amendments
  • Corrections/additions approved by Secretary & Chief Accounting Authority, DoP
  • Consistent with Appendix-2 to the Account Code, Volume-I

2. Sectoral Classification & Government Accounts Parts▶

📖 Definition

Government Accounts follow sectoral classification into 4 broad sectors, distinguished by a letter of the alphabet:

SectorName
AGeneral Services
BSocial & Community Services
CEconomic Services
DGrant-in-aid and Contributions

📚 Three Parts of Government Accounts

PartDescription
Part I – Consolidated FundGrouped into Sectors (General/Social/Economic Services) → Major Heads (sub-sectors where needed)
Part II – Contingency FundSingle Major Head only; all Contingency Fund transactions recorded under it
Part III – Public AccountGrouped into Sectors/Sub-Sectors (lettered) → Major Heads
🧠 Mnemonic: "Consolidated has Categories, Contingency is Concise (1 head), Public has Parts too" — remember Contingency Fund = only 1 Major Head, unlike the other two Parts.

6. Major Head 8661 — Suspense Accounts (Postal) & GST Sub-Heads▶

📖 Definition

8661 is the temporary parking head for transactions awaiting final classification — most prominently GST collections (CGST/SGST/UTGST/IGST + penalties) service-category-wise, pending remittance to tax authorities.

📋 GST Sub-Head Pattern (Minor Head 101, Sub-Head by Service)

Sub-HeadService Category
60Courier Services
61Banking & Financial Services
62Postal Life Insurance (PLI)
63 (pattern)Rural PLI (RPLI)

Within each service, Detailed Heads follow a consistent 13-step numbering pattern: 01 CGST · 02 Penalty-CGST · 03 SGST · 04 Penalty-SGST · 05 UTGST · 06 Penalty-UTGST · 07 IGST · 08 Penalty-IGST · 09 Ded. Input Tax Credit · 10-13 Ded. Remit to CGST/SGST/UTGST/IGST.

🧠 Mnemonic: "C-P-S-P-U-P-I-P" (CGST, Penalty, SGST, Penalty, UTGST, Penalty, IGST, Penalty) — the repeating 8-step tax+penalty pattern before ITC deduction & remittance steps.

📝 One-Page Revision Sheet | अंतिम दिन के लिए रिवीज़न

Structure: Sector → Major Head (4-digit) → Minor Head → Sub-Head → Detailed Head → Object Head = 15-digit CGA code; 10-digit DOP ERP GL code maps to it.
Sectors: A-General Services · B-Social Services · C-Economic Services · D-Grant-in-aid. Contingency Fund (Part-II) = only 1 Major Head.
Key Receipt Heads: 0037-Customs · 0049-Interest Receipts · 1201-Postal Receipts (🔴 most important)
Postal Function Head: 3201 (Economic Services – Postal Operations), sub-divided 01-08 & 60
Small Savings: 8001-National Savings Deposits (POSB/RD/TD/MIS/SCSS/NSS/Sukanya) · 8002-NSC · 8006-PPF
Deposits vs Advances vs Suspense: 8446-Postal Deposits (liability, no interest) · 8553-Postal Advances (recoverable) · 8661-Suspense (temporary, incl. GST)
Remittances: 8781-Money Orders · 8783-Railway Ticket Sale (PRS) · 8787-Railways Adjustment · 8789-Defence Adjustment
Cash Balance: 8999 — Cash in Treasury / RBI Deposits / Remittance in Transit (Local & Foreign)

🔑 Master Mnemonic Chain

"0037 सीमा शुल्क, 1201 डाक प्राप्ति, 3201 डाक कार्य, 8001 बचत, 8446 जमा, 8553 अग्रिम, 8661 निलंबन, 8781 मनीऑर्डर, 8999 नकद शेष" — यह वाक्य क्रमांक के क्रम में प्रमुख Major Heads को याद दिलाता है: Customs (0037) → Postal Receipts (1201) → Postal Operations (3201) → Small Savings (8001) → Deposits (8446) → Advances (8553) → Suspense (8661) → Money Orders (8781) → Cash Balance (8999).
