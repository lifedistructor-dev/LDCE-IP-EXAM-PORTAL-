24.01 Introduction — PLI History 🔴 VI▾

PLI introduced: 1st February 1884 — India's oldest life insurer. Started for postal employees; extended to Telegraph Dept employees in 1888; extended to female employees in 1894 (when no other insurer covered women). Grew to 46+ lakh policies by 31.03.2017.

Covers: Central/State Govt employees, PSUs, Universities, aided institutions, Nationalised Banks, local/autonomous bodies, JVs with ≥10% Govt/PSU stake, credit cooperative societies, Defence & Paramilitary personnel.

24.02 RPLI Introduction — Malhotra Committee 🔴 VI▾

RPLI introduced: 24.03.1995, following Malhotra Committee (1993) observation that only 22% of India's insurable population was insured, and life insurance funds were just 10% of gross household savings.

Objective: insure rural public, especially weaker sections & women workers, leveraging DoP's vast rural network. Grew to 146+ lakh policies by 31.03.2017.

Likely Q: Which committee's recommendation led to RPLI's introduction, and in which year was RPLI launched? → Malhotra Committee (1993); RPLI launched 24.03.1995.

24.05 Core Insurance Solutions (CIS) & CPC 🟠 FAQ▾

IT modernisation approved by CCEA, Nov 2012, outlay ₹4,909 crore. McCamish application implemented for CIS — PLI/RPLI data migrated from NIC software.

Post-CIS, PLI/RPLI work decentralised from Divisional/Regional/Circle level to Head Post Office level via Central Processing Centre (CPC) — per PLI Directorate order dt. 24.09.2015.

24.06 Role of PAOs in PLI/RPLI Accounting 🔴 VI▾

Schedules/certified lists of PLI premia + payment vouchers received with monthly DDO accounts at Circle PAO. Checked against CSI/booked figures, consolidated to Circle Total = Circle Detail Book.

🔴 Key date: Consolidated statement sent to PLI Section/Director PLI by 28th of the following month. GL check done via T-Code Fagll03.

24.07 Investment Board & Fund Managers 🟠 FAQ▾

Union Cabinet (13 Dec 2007) approved Investment Board for POLIF (Post Office Life Insurance Fund) & RPOLIF (Rural POLIF), managed by 2 Public Sector Fund Managers: UTI AMC & SBI Funds Management Pvt Ltd, investing in passive/non-discretionary mode via Custodian Bank HDFC.

Investment Board composition: Member(PLI) Postal Services Board (Chairman); 3 external Financial Experts (MoF-consulted); 1 Joint Secretary, MoF/DoD; Joint Secretary & FA, DoP; CGM PLI Directorate; Consulting Actuary; Chief Investment Officer (Convenor).

Frozen corpus (as on 31.10.2009) converted to GOISSFRB (Govt of India Special Security Floating Rate Bond) effective 01.11.2009.
