Chapter X — Capital Accounts
अध्याय 10 — पूंजी लेखा

▼
10.01 Capital vs Revenue Expenditure 🔴 VI▾

Capital Expenditure: Incurred to acquire tangible assets of a permanent nature, or enhance utility of existing assets. Recorded under Major Head 5201 – Capital Outlay on Postal Services.

Revenue Expenditure: Maintenance, repair, upkeep, day-to-day working expenses (incl. establishment & administrative costs).

🧠 "Capital = Build it. Revenue = Run it."

10.02 Asset Records & Responsibility 🟠 FAQ▾

Administrative authorities delegated with procurement must maintain capital asset records. Head of PAO + Sr. DDG (PAF) assist AS&FA for comprehensive assets/liabilities records; Head of Circles build/update them.

Registers of Land & Buildings: Maintained by Heads of Circles per Rule 484, P&T Financial Handbook Vol-I — shows date of construction, floor area, ownership, original value, additions.

Internal Audit verifies: correctness of asset register maintenance/updation, GST Input Tax Credit availing (cross-checked with invoices), and that all Head-5201-eligible charges are correctly captured.

PAO ensures 5201 bookings (scheme & non-scheme) are against proper budget allotment, correctly classified, and assets entered in the register via the procurement application. Budget Section of PAO maintains a watch-register for 5201 expenditure vs allotment — monthly reconciliation responsibility rests with Head of Circle + Head of PAO per GFR Rule 57.

Chapter XI — Expense Coverage Ratio (ECR)
अध्याय 11 — व्यय कवरेज अनुपात

▲
11.01 Concept & Formula 🔴 VI▾

ECR = Revenue Receipts (RR) earned per ₹100 of Working Expenses (WE). Higher ECR = better financial performance. Ideal Operating Ratio for DoP = 1:1.

ECR = (Net Revenue Receipts ÷ Net Working Expenses) × 100

Net Revenue Receipts = Gross Revenue Receipts − Refunds
Net Working Expenditure = Gross Working Expenditure − Depreciation − Recoveries

ECR ValueMeaning
< 100Revenue < Expenditure → Loss
> 100Revenue > Expenditure → Profit
Operating Ratio = Net Expenditure ÷ Net Revenue

Operating Ratio < 1 = profitable state of business (mirror image of ECR > 100).

🧠 "ECR high is a High-five 🖐️ (good); Operating Ratio low is Low and lovely (good)."

11.02 Purpose & Ways to Improve ECR 🟠 FAQ▾

ECR shows how much expenditure a unit's own revenue covers — a unit-level profitability measure, sensitising staff to each office's earning potential.

Control/minimise revenue expenditure Achieve revenue targets, prevent pilferage Maximise recoveries from other organisations Exploit new schemes/services Optimum HR utilisation Performance audit 6 months post-launch of new product Vigilance against fraud Correct Head-of-Account booking

Establishment charges (average cost) Pension element = 12% of average pensionable-post cost Leave salary element = 11% of average pensionable-post cost DA, HRA, TPA on average cost Fixed % addition for admin & audit cost Fixed/assessed house rent Fixed stationery charges 2/3rd of Line Establishment charges (unit-dedicated) 5% of total postage-stamp sale value (printing/Direction/Audit cost)

🧠 "12 for Pension, 11 for Leave, 5 for Stamps, 2/3rd for Line" — the 4 numbers to memorise for ECR cost components.
