1.01 Key Definitions (a–t) 🔴 VI▾

🧠 Mnemonic (order of first letters): "A-B-A-B-B-B-C-C-C-C-D-D-G-G-H-R-S-T" — Accounting Authority, Bank, Branch Audit/PAO, CAG, Chief Acctg Authority, Controller Genl of Accounts, Director Genl/Audit, Department, Debt Heads, Govt/Govt A/c, HQ Office, Remittance, State, Treasury.

Accounting Authority (लेखा प्राधिकारी): GM(Finance) Postal Accounts incl. DA(Postal) & Dy.DA(Postal) exercising accounting functions.
Accounts Officer: Any officer subordinate to CAA/Accounting Authority who exercises accounting functions.
Audit Officer: Officer subordinate to C&AG exercising audit functions.
Bank: RBI or its offices/branches; SBI branch acting as RBI's agent; subsidiary bank u/s 2 of SBI(Subsidiary Banks) Act 1959; or any RBI-appointed agency.
Branch Audit Office: Office of Director of Audit / Audit officer in-charge of a Postal & Telecom Audit Office.
Branch Postal Accounts Office (PAO): Office of GM(Finance) Postal Accounts / DA(Postal) / Dy.DA(Postal) in-charge of the Circle's Postal Accounts Office.
Constitution: The Constitution of India.
Chief Accounting Authority (CAA) 🔴: Secretary of the Department/Ministry with overall control of accounting functions — i.e., Secretary, Department of Posts.
Comptroller and Auditor General (C&AG): The Comptroller and Auditor General of India.
Controller General of Accounts (CGA) 🟠: Controller of Accounts, Ministry of Finance (Dept. of Expenditure) — responsible for maintaining a sound Payment-cum-Accounting system across departmentalised accounts offices and prescribing rules for custody/payment/withdrawal of Govt money.
Director General: Director General, Postal Services.
Director of Audit: Director of Audit, P&T.
Department: Department of Posts.
Debt Heads: Heads of Account recording Debt, Deposits, Sinking Funds, Advances & Suspense transactions.
Government: Central or State Government, or both, per context.
Government Account 🟠: Relates to the Consolidated Fund of India (Art. 266(1)) or Public Account of India (Art. 266(2)). Contingency Fund = established under Contingency Fund of India Act 1950, per Art. 267(1).
Head Quarter Office: Office of the Director General, Postal Services.
Remittance Heads: Heads of account for transactions relating to remittance business.
State: Any state specified in the First Schedule of the Constitution.
Treasury: Includes a sub-treasury.
परिभाषाएँ: मुख्य लेखा प्राधिकारी (CAA) = डाक विभाग के सचिव; नियंत्रक महालेखाकार (CGA) = वित्त मंत्रालय में भुगतान-सह-लेखा प्रणाली के प्रभारी
Likely Q: Who is the Chief Accounting Authority for Department of Posts? → Secretary, Department of Posts.
1.02 General Outlines of System of Accounts 🟡 Concept▾

General outlines of the Central Govt accounts system (Chapter III, Account Code Vol. I) apply mutatis mutandis to DoP accounts. CAA ensures suitable accounts are maintained by subordinate officers in prescribed forms.

Postal accounts follow, as far as practicable, Commercial Book Keeping practice. Two key features:

(i) A Capital Account is maintained to exhibit value of assets
↓
(ii) A Govt ledger head shows the true/commercial Profit or Loss on Department's working — Dept bears charges to other Depts & gets credit for services rendered

Revenue realisations booked under Major Head 1201-Postal Receipts; working expenses under 3201-Postal Services — divided into sub-major, minor, sub & detailed heads.

1.03 Profit & Loss A/c, Initial Accounts, RMS 🟠 FAQ▾

Profit & Loss Account: Dept meets all liabilities from own revenue; surplus/deficit appropriated to/from Postal Capital Reserve Fund. It is a real account within Govt accounts, prepared annually from net P&L in the Ledger after final closure — includes inter-branch adjustments. Shown in Finance & Revenue Accounts and DG's financial review for Appropriation Accounts.

Initial Accounts: Each Head Post Office is the primary accounting unit — renders an e-account of receipts/disbursements to Circle Accounts Office. Transactions classified at point of origin via Daily Transaction Report, Treasurer's Cash Book. 🔴 Month's account is freezed on 5th of following month.

Railway Mail Service: Head Record Office is the RMS accounting unit — renders monthly e-account return to Circle Accounts Office (same rules as HPO).

Likely Q: By which date is a month's account freezed? → 5th of the following month.
1.04 Compilation Flow: HPO → PAO → Directorate → CGA 🔴 VI▾
HPOs push DDO-wise accounts to PFMS on T+3 basis (transaction date + 3 days)
↓
Monthly CGA report generated → cross-checked with PFMS data → variations adjusted GL-wise via Transfer Entries
↓
PAO consolidates in PFMS after Transfer Entries → flows to e-Lekha
↓
Postal Accounts Wing (HQ) submits monthly accounts in PFMS (digitally signed) + e-Lekha for DoP as a whole
↓
HQ compiles Annual Accounts → incorporated into Combined Finance & Revenue Accounts of Central Govt + Appropriation Accounts

Finance Accounts: Prepared annually by Ministry of Finance — receipts/payments, financial results of Revenue & Capital accounts, public debt, liabilities & assets. Secretary, DoP (as CAA) is responsible for accuracy/timeliness of Postal material furnished.

Duties of an Accounting Authority (3 main): (a) compile e-accounts of Circle's receipts/disbursements (b) transfer inter-Circle items via system & adjust items transferred by others (c) generate monthly detailed e-account, forward to PA Wing HQ.

DG's duty: prepare monthly detailed account (major-head-wise) of Dept & submit extract to Ministry of Finance and CGA.

2.04 Staff Strength, Statistics & Establishment Norms 🟠 FAQ▾

Staff requirement basis 🔴: average work of 5 continuous months, January–May of the proposal year. Budget staff proposals due to Circle Head & HQ by 30th June each year. (For GPF/NPS/SDBS/Book: 1st-of-month figures used.)

Responsibility for accurate statistics: AAO of the section personally; independently rechecked by another AAO; Branch Officer also checks; both sign the statement.

Central Inspection/Internal Check must verify registers of statistics for ≥10% of sections per group (min. 1 section like PA, Admin, Account Current, Pension) during independent recheck.

Routine sections (Money Order, Cash Certificates) = flexible strength, varies with transaction volume. Non-routine sections (Postal Accounts, Pension, A/c Current, GPF, Coordination, Admin) = not flexible, changed only on manifest workload shift. Concept of man-days/outturn per day is abolished — work must be completed day-to-day without arrears (invoking FR 11 for internal staff redistribution).

Likely Q: On what basis are staff requirements computed? → Average work of Jan–May (5 months) of the proposal year; proposals submitted by 30th June.
2.05 Calendar of Returns (COR) & State of Work Report (SWR) 🔴 VI▾

Each section maintains a Calendar of Returns – Form Sy.264 showing due/actual dates for: (a) dispatch of returns due from section (b) submission of accounts/objections/statements/registers to Branch Officer (c) receipt of returns due to section (d) special watch items. Subdivided by periodicity: Bi-weekly, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, Bi-monthly, Quarterly, Half-yearly, Yearly.

2.07 Record Room Procedure – Preservation & Destruction 🟠 FAQ▾
Files/registers/voucher bundles bound after closure; outer cover shows year of destruction (Form Sy.322C, dated signature of AO)
↓
Kept in section for not more than 3 calendar years
↓
Transferred to Departmental Record Room with duplicate list (File No./Classification, Year, Date of Review/Destruction, Record Room Index No.)
↓
Record Room verifies, retains 1 copy, returns 1 signed copy with Index No. to section

No file issued without a requisition signed by Sectional Head (showing Index No.); requisition slip kept in file's place until returned. Non-receipt within 1 month → Record Keeper reminds the section. Detailed preservation periods → Appendix-III of this volume.
