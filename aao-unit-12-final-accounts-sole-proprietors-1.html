Final Accounts = 2 Parts

  • Income Statement (Trading + P&L A/c)
  • Position Statement (Balance Sheet)

Cost of Goods Sold

  • = Opening Stock + Net Purchases + Direct Expenses − Closing Stock

Gross Profit / Loss

  • Sales − Cost of Goods Sold = Gross Profit (or Loss if negative)

Net Profit / Loss

  • Gross Profit + Indirect Incomes − Indirect Expenses = Net Profit

Balance Sheet Equation

  • Assets = Capital + Liabilities (Dual Aspect Concept)

Marshalling — 2 Orders

  • Liquidity order: most liquid asset first, current liability first
  • Permanence order: least liquid asset first (companies use this — Companies Act)

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Sections / Rules

  • Closing stock valued at cost or net realisable value, whichever is lower — per Accounting Standard 2 (Revised).
  • Schedule III, Companies Act 2013 — prescribes balance sheet format for Joint Stock Companies (no prescribed format for sole proprietors/partnerships).

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Contingent liabilities are NOT shown in the balance sheet — only as a footnote below it.
  • Balance sheet is a statement, not an account — no "To"/"By" prefixes, despite being part of final accounts.

📅 Important Dates

No historical date — balance sheet is prepared "as on" a specific date (a snapshot), not for a period like the P&L account.

🏛️ Important Authorities

  • J.R. Batliboi — definitions of Trading Account and Balance Sheet.
  • Prof. Carter — definition of Profit and Loss Account.
  • Finney & Miller — definition of Fixed Assets.
  • Hovard & Upton — definition of Current Assets.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Current assets/liabilities = realisable/payable within one year (normal operating cycle).
  • Long-term = one year or more.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — balance sheet preparation is "compulsory in certain cases" (e.g. companies under law) rather than penalty-linked in this unit.

Marshalling: 2 Orders of Presentation
Order of LIQUIDITY: most-liquid asset first (cash), goodwill last; current liabilities first, capital last
Order of PERMANENCE: reverse — goodwill first, cash last; capital first, current liabilities last. Mandatory for Joint Stock Companies under Companies Act.

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Final accounts consist of Trading & Profit and Loss Account (income statement) and Balance Sheet (position statement).
  • Cost of Goods Sold = Opening Stock + Net Purchases + Direct Expenses − Closing Stock.
  • Closing stock is valued at cost or net realisable value, whichever is lower.
  • Balance sheet must satisfy Assets = Capital + Liabilities.
  • Contingent liabilities are shown only as a footnote, never on the balance sheet itself.
