🗂 Chapter 1: Background & Overview (पृष्ठभूमि)

Topic: Office Memorandum F.No.6-1/2025-FA(P) dated 28th March 2025, issued by Integrated Finance Wing, Department of Posts — "Delegation of Financial Powers to Heads of Circles (HOC), Heads of Department (HOD), Independent Directors and Divisional Heads of Department of Posts."

विषय: डाक विभाग में विभिन्न प्राधिकारियों (HOC, HOD, स्वतंत्र निदेशक, मंडल प्रधान) को नई DFPR 2024 के अंतर्गत वित्तीय शक्तियाँ पुनः प्रत्यायोजित करने वाला परिपत्र।

🔴 HIGH🟠 FREQUENT
Exam Q: OM delegating revised DFPR 2024 powers to DoP authorities — dated? Ans: 28th March 2025 (F.No.6-1/2025-FA(P))
🕰 Timeline — Evolution of Financial Powers in Dept. of Posts
Pre-1985: P&T Department — Schedule of Financial Powers of Posts & Telegraphs officers followed.
January 1985: Bifurcation into Department of Posts (DoP) & Department of Telecommunications (DoT); separate Schedule of Financial Powers drawn up for DoP.
1st April 1988: DoP brought under the erstwhile Delegation of Financial Powers Rules (DFPR), 1978.
30th July 2021: Last revision of DoP's Schedules of Financial Powers vide OM No.6-1/2021-Fin.Coord.
1st April 2024: Ministry of Finance replaces DFPR 1978 with DFPR 2024 — many old Schedule items lose relevance. 🔴
28th March 2025: Secretary (Posts), in consultation with AS&FA, delegates revised financial powers under Rule 12(2) of DFPR 2024 via Schedule II-A, II-B, II-C and Schedule III. 🔴
1st April 2025: Revised delegated powers come into force, replacing earlier Schedule II-A/II-B/II-C/III. 🔴
Exam Q: DFPR 1978 replaced by DFPR 2024 — effective from? Ans: 1st April 2024 🔴
Exam Q: Revised Schedules for DoP officers came into force from? Ans: 1st April 2025 🔴 (Don't confuse with DFPR 2024's own effective date!)
🧠 Mnemonic: "1985-Split, 1988-DFPR78, 2021-Revised, 2024-DFPR-New, 2025-DoP-Applied"

🔴 Who is "Head of Department" (HOD)? — Note 1▶

For this Schedule, "Head of Department" includes: Secretary Postal Services Board, Chief Postmasters General (CPMG), Chief General Managers (CGM), Director RAKNPA, Postmasters General (PMG), Sr. DDG (PAF), General Managers (PAF), General Manager (CEPT), Chief Engineers, and Director (Foreign Post).

Exam Q: Name any 3 officers classified as "Head of Department" under DFPR-2024 DoP Schedule. Ans: CPMG, CGM, Director RAKNPA (also PMG, GM-CEPT, Chief Engineers) 🔴
🟠 Notes 2–5 — Special Provisions▶
  • Note 2: Sr. DDG (PAF) & General Managers (Finance) exercise only powers in Schedule II-C relevant to their operational role. Director-level officers heading PAOs are treated as "Head of Office" with specific powers under Schedule II-C for uniformity.
  • Note 3: In-charge of Foreign Post exercises powers equivalent to HoD (Sch. II-A) / Independent Director (Sch. II-B) / Divisional Head (Sch. III), as applicable.
  • Note 4: Certain listed items (e.g. a(ii), a(iii), a(iv), 11(xix)(a), 15(i)/(ii)/(iv), 18(iv), 22, 24(vii), 25(i)/(ii), 27(iii)/(iv)/(v), 28(i), 31, 34(i)-(v) etc.) of Schedule II-A can be exercised ONLY by HOC (not by HOD generally).
  • Note 5: Certain HOC-only items (11(xix)(a), 15(i)/(ii)/(iv), 18(iv), 22, 24(vii), 27(iii)/(iv), 28(i)) are also exercisable by Director (RAKNPA) and CGM (PD/BD/PLI).
Exam Q: Which officers besides HOC can exercise the HOC-only items listed in Note 5? Ans: Director (RAKNPA) and CGM (PD/BD/PLI) 🟠
🔴 General Conditions Applicable to All Delegated Powers (Para 4)▶
  1. No work requiring sanction of higher authority may be sanctioned by splitting it into two or more distinct parts.
  2. If a contract extends over a period of time, the total value over its entire currency is taken for applying the monetary limit.
  3. Limits for sanctioning estimates/passing excess apply not just to individual estimates but to projects comprising multiple constituent estimates — competency is based on total project cost.
  4. Where financial powers are authorized to Gazetted Officers (Rule 12(3), DFPR 2024), the Head of Department remains responsible for correctness, regularity & propriety of their decisions.
  5. All conditions/instructions/remarks against each item of expenditure in the Schedules must be complied with.
Exam Q: Can a Rs.15 lakh work be split into two Rs.7.5 lakh sanctions to avoid higher approval? Ans: No — splitting to avoid sanction of higher authority is expressly prohibited (Condition (i)) 🔴

🟡 Object Head 09 (Training Expenses)▶
  • Domestic training expenses: monetary limits fixed for HOC/HOD (subject to Nodal Dept. rules); IFA consultation applies as per limits.
  • Foreign training expenses: Full Powers, but subject to prior approval of competent authority [Secretary (Posts) / Hon'ble MOC / SCoSI].

Exam Q: Who can sanction outsourced training services up to Rs.2 crore/annum? Ans: Director, RAKNPA only 🟡
🧾 Chapter 4: Schedule II-A — Office Expenses & Contingent Expenditure (Object Head 13)

🟢 Threshold rule: Office equipment/furniture not exceeding Rs.1 lakh or 3 years useful life (whichever) is booked as "Office Expenses" (revenue); beyond that → classified as capital expenditure under "Machinery & Equipment" / "Furniture & Fixtures". Vehicles are always capital ("Motor Vehicles"), regardless of usage.

🏤 Chapter 7: Schedule III — Divisional Heads (मंडल प्रधान) 🔴 MOST IMPORTANT FOR IP EXAM

🔴 Divisional Heads = Superintendents of Post Offices/RMS Divisions etc. — the level most directly relevant to Inspector Posts (IP) functioning. Salaries, Wages, Pensionary Charges, Allowances, LTC, Domestic & Foreign Travel Expenses: all Full Powers (subject to FR/SR, FHB) — but if rules explicitly mandate IFA consultation, the matter is referred up to HOD/HOC (Divisional Heads don't consult IFA directly).

🔴 Other Financial Powers of Divisional Heads — Refunds▶

All the following carry Full Powers for Divisional Heads:

  • (a) Refund of earnest money to tenderers — within the divisional head's tender competency
  • (b) Refund of air surcharge/fee difference for surcharged air mail delayed & sent by surface route due to service fault
  • (c) Refund of postage on articles maliciously sent unpaid to annoy the addressee
  • (d) Refund of coins, currency notes, unused stamps/postcards/envelopes/stamp booklets found in letter boxes or counters & credited to office accounts
  • (e) Refund of amounts over-paid or short-paid by/to a member of public, found in excess in office accounts
Exam Q: Divisional Head's power to refund postage on maliciously-sent unpaid articles? Ans: Full Powers 🔴 — classic IP exam trick question (candidates assume monetary cap exists)!
👥 Chapter 8: Schedule II-B & II-C — Independent Directors and Accounts Officers
🟡 Schedule II-B: Directors of Postal Services (Independent Charge)▶

Covers officers who are Directors of Postal Services having Independent Charge. Generally mirrors HOD's item structure but with its own (often lower) monetary ceilings, e.g.:

ItemLimit
Ad-hoc payment of arrears of pay/allowancesRs. 10,000
Honorarium & Rewards to Govt. servantRs. 2,500 (IFA not required at this level)
Honorarium — SB control workRs. 2,500
Exam Q: Independent Director's ad-hoc arrears payment limit (Sch. II-B)? Ans: Rs. 10,000 🟡
🟡 Schedule II-C: Sr. DDG (PAF), GM (Finance) & Directors of Accounts (Postal)▶

Uniquely structured as a 3-column table — Sr. DDG (PAF), GM (Finance), and DAP each get separately listed powers for the same item (unlike other Schedules with a single "authority" column).

Example: Salaries → Full Powers for all three (Sr.DDG-PAF / GM-Finance / DAP alike).

🟢 Sr. DDG (PAF) heads the Postal Accounts Wing in the Directorate; GM (Finance) exercises only powers relevant to operational requirements; Director-level PAO heads are treated as "Head of Office" for uniformity in large establishments.

🧠 Master Mnemonic — Hierarchy of Powers (highest to lowest):
"HOC → HOD → Independent Director / GM-Finance/DAP → Divisional Head"
(शक्ति क्रम: सर्कल प्रधान > विभाग प्रधान > स्वतंत्र निदेशक/GM-वित्त > मंडल प्रधान)
🧠 Mnemonic — Schedule Letters:
"II-A = All (HOC & HOD), II-B = Bosses with independent charge (Directors), II-C = Cash/Accounts people (Sr.DDG-PAF, GM-Finance, DAP), III = Third-tier field (Divisional Heads)"
