💵 Definition of Salary वेतन की परिभाषा Sec 15-16

▼
Section 15 — What is chargeable as "Salaries" 🔴
  • Any salary due from employer, whether paid or not
  • Any salary paid/allowed though not yet due
  • Any arrears of salary paid/allowed, if not taxed earlier
  • "Employer" includes former employer
  • Salary/bonus/commission paid to a partner of a firm is NOT salary under this head 🟠
Section 16 — "Salary" includes 🔴
Wages
Annuity or Pension
Gratuity
Fees or Commission
Perquisites
Profits in lieu of salary
Advance of salary
Leave encashment received
Recognised PF accretion (taxable portion)
Employer's pension scheme contribution (Sec 124)
Agniveer Corpus Fund contribution (Sec 125)
🧠 Mnemonic: "WAG-FPAAL" → Wages, Annuity/pension, Gratuity, Fees/commission, Perquisites, Advance, Leave encashment — key items under Sec 16.
Probable Q: Is a partner's salary from a firm taxed under "Salaries"? → No — Sec 15(4) excludes it 🔴

🎁 Perquisites अनुलाभ Sec 17

▼
Includes (Sec 17(1)) 🟠
  • Rent-free / concessional accommodation
  • Free/concessional benefit or amenity (director / high-income employee)
  • Specified security / sweat equity shares
  • Employer's payment of employee's obligation
  • Employer's payment to effect life assurance/annuity (except recognised PF, approved superannuation fund, or Deposit-linked Insurance Fund)
  • Employer's contribution > ₹7,50,000/year to RPF + NPS(124) + superannuation fund combined 🔴
  • Annual accretion (interest/dividend) on above excess contribution
Exemptions from Perquisite (Sec 17(2)) 🟡
  • Medical treatment in employer-maintained hospital
  • Medical treatment in Govt/local authority/approved hospital, or for prescribed diseases
  • Employer-paid health insurance premium (approved scheme)
  • Conveyance for office commute
  • Medical treatment/travel abroad (subject to RBI permission & income limits)
🧠 Mnemonic: "7.5 Lakh Cap" → Combined employer contribution to RPF+NPS+Superannuation above ₹7,50,000/yr is a taxable perquisite.
Probable Q: What is the annual threshold for employer's combined RPF+NPS+Superannuation contribution to become a taxable perquisite? → ₹7,50,000 🔴

🧾 TDS on Salary वेतन पर टीडीएस Sec 392

▼
Person responsible for paying "Salaries" shall deduct income-tax at time of payment
↓
Deduction at the average rate of income-tax, computed on rates in force for the tax year
↓
Based on estimated income of employee for that year
Employer's Option — Non-Monetary Perquisite 🟠

Employer may, at his own option, pay tax on whole/part of non-monetary perquisite (Sec 17(1)) himself instead of deducting from employee.

Particulars DDO Must Consider (Sec 392(4)) 🔴
  • Salary from any other employer during the year
  • Relief under Sec 157 (Govt servant/company/co-op/local authority employee)
  • Loss under "Income from house property"
  • Income under any other head (except loss, other than house property loss)
  • Tax already deducted/collected at source for the year

Restriction: Tax deductible from Salary can be reduced only for house-property loss or other TDS/TCS already made — nothing else 🔴

DDO's Sec 392(5) Duties
  • Furnish statement of perquisites/profits in lieu of salary with value, to employee
  • Obtain evidence/proof of claims from employee before allowing deductions
  • Adjust TDS for any excess/deficiency of earlier deduction
Special Cases
  • Recognised PF trustees — deduct tax on accumulated balance paid to employee (per Schedule XI)
  • EPF accumulated balance ≥ ₹50,000 — TDS at 10% if includible in total income 🟠
  • Salary paid in foreign currency — converted to ₹ at prescribed exchange rate for TDS
🧠 Mnemonic: "Average Rate, Every Payment" — TDS on salary always uses the average rate method, unlike flat-rate TDS on other incomes.
Probable Q: At what rate is TDS on EPF withdrawal ≥₹50,000 deducted (if taxable)? → 10% 🔴

📅 DDO Statements, Returns & Time Limits डीडीओ विवरण, रिटर्न व समय सीमा Sec 397, 399

▼
TAN — Tax Deduction & Collection Account Number 🔴
  • Every deductor must apply for TAN within prescribed time (Sec 397(1)(a))
  • TAN must be quoted in all challans/statements/certificates
PAN Requirement & Higher TDS 🔴

If deductee fails to furnish valid PAN → higher of:

  • Rate specified in the Act, OR
  • Rate(s) in force, OR
  • 20% (5% for specified virtual digital asset/other cases)
Payment & Statement Cycle — Sec 397(3) 🔴
Step 1: Deduct TDS at time of salary payment
↓
Step 2: Pay TDS to credit of Central Government within prescribed time
↓
Step 3: Deliver a statement (TDS return) for the period, in prescribed form & time to Income-tax authority
↓
Step 4 (if error): Deliver a correction statement within 2 years from end of tax year of original statement 🔴
Government DDO — Special Provision (Sec 397(3)(e)) 🟠

Where TDS paid to Govt credit without a challan, the Pay & Accounts Officer / Treasury Officer / Cheque Drawing & Disbursing Officer must deliver the statement.

Processing of Statements — Sec 399 🟡

All TDS/TCS statements (incl. correction statements) are processed by adjusting for arithmetical errors or incorrect claims apparent from the statement; interest computed on amounts reflected.

🧠 Mnemonic: "TAN-Pay-Statement-Correct" (T-P-S-C) → 4-step DDO compliance cycle under Sec 397.
Probable Q: Within how long must a correction statement be filed? → 2 years from end of the relevant tax year 🔴

⚠️ Default, Interest & Penalty चूक, ब्याज एवं शास्ति Sec 398, 412

▼
"Assessee in Default" — Sec 398(1) 🔴

DDO who fails to deduct/pay TDS (wholly or partly) is deemed an "assessee in default".

Exception — Sec 398(2) 🟠

Not treated as default if payee has: filed return of income, included the amount in that return, paid tax due — AND a CA's certificate to this effect is furnished.

Interest for Default — Sec 398(3) 🔴

Failure to Deduct

1% per month (or part) — from date deductible to date actually deducted

Deducted but Not Paid

1.5% per month (or part) — from date deducted to date actually paid
🧠 Mnemonic: "1 to Deduct, 1.5 to Deposit" → 1% for delay in deducting, 1.5% for delay in depositing after deduction.
Time Limit for "Default" Order — Sec 398(5) 🔴

No order can be made deeming default after 6 years from end of tax year in which TDS was deductible, OR 2 years from end of tax year in which correction statement was filed — whichever is later.

Penalty — Sec 412 🔴
  • Penalty amount as Assessing Officer directs (continuing default → further amounts)
  • Total penalty shall not exceed the amount of tax in arrears
  • No penalty without giving reasonable opportunity of being heard
  • No penalty if default was for "good and sufficient reasons"
  • Penalty not waived merely because tax was later paid
  • If tax demand later fully reduced on appeal → penalty cancelled & refunded
Probable Q: What is the maximum penalty leviable under Sec 412 for TDS default? → Cannot exceed the amount of tax in arrears 🔴

⚡ Last-Day Revision Notes अंतिम-दिन पुनरीक्षण नोट्स

▼
  • Standard Deduction = ₹75,000 (new regime) / ₹50,000 (other)
  • Govt employee commuted pension = fully exempt (Sl.7)
  • Non-Govt commuted pension: ⅓ exempt if gratuity received, else ½ exempt
  • VRS exemption = min(compensation, ₹5,00,000)
  • TDS on Salary (Sec 392) = average rate method, monthly
  • PAN not furnished → TDS at 20% (or specified rate)
  • Correction statement deadline = 2 years
  • Interest: 1%/month non-deduction, 1.5%/month non-deposit
  • Default order limit = 6 years / 2 years from correction, later
  • Sec 412 penalty ≤ tax in arrears
  • Sec 123 (80C-equiv) cap = ₹1,50,000
  • Sec 124 NPS: employer 14%/10%; self extra ₹50,000
  • Sec 126 health insurance: ₹25,000+₹25,000(parents)+₹50,000+₹50,000(medical exp)
  • EPF TDS threshold = ₹50,000 at 10%

🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics याद रखने की तरकीबें

▼
"75-New, 50-Old" → Standard deduction ₹75,000 under new regime (202), ₹50,000 in old regime.
"⅓ with Gratuity, ½ without" → Non-Govt employee commuted pension exemption rule.
"1 to Deduct, 1.5 to Deposit" → interest rates for TDS default under Sec 398(3).
"6-2 Later Rule" → default order time limit = later of 6 yrs / 2 yrs from correction statement.
"7.5 Lakh Combo Cap" → RPF+NPS+Superannuation employer contribution ceiling for perquisite taxability.
"123-124-125-126" → sequential deduction sections: savings, NPS, Agnipath, health insurance.
"TAN-Pay-Statement-Correct" → DDO's 4-step compliance cycle under Sec 397.
"Penalty ≤ Arrears" → Sec 412 penalty ceiling rule, easy one-liner.
