What are Adjustment Entries

  • Journal entries at year-end for items omitted from trial balance — closing stock, O/S & prepaid items, accruals

The Golden Rule of Adjustments

  • EVERY adjustment appears in TWO places: once in Trading/P&L A/c, once in Balance Sheet

4 Needs for Adjustments

  • Record trial-balance omissions
  • Bring in O/S & prepaid expenses
  • Bring in accrued/advance income
  • Create reserves/provisions

Closing Stock Valuation

  • Lower of Cost or Net Realisable Value (AS-2 Revised) — Conservatism convention

Add vs Deduct Pattern

  • Outstanding/Accrued items → ADDED to the concerned expense/income
  • Prepaid/Advance items → DEDUCTED from the concerned expense/income

Bad Debts Special Rule

  • No existing provision → debit P&L A/c directly
  • Provision exists → transfer to Provision for Bad Debts A/c instead

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Sections / Rules

  • AS-2 (Revised): Net Realisable Value = estimated selling price − estimated cost of completion − estimated cost to make the sale.
  • Matching principle underlies every adjustment: this period's revenue matched with this period's expense.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Income tax paid is treated as a personal drawing of the proprietor — deducted from Capital, NOT shown as a business expense in P&L A/c.
  • Bad debts treatment splits into two paths depending on whether a Provision for Doubtful Debts already exists.

📅 Important Dates

No historical dates — adjustments are tied to the close of each accounting period, not fixed calendar dates.

🏛️ Important Authorities

Indian Accounting Standards (AS-2 Revised) governs closing stock valuation — the only named standard in this unit.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

No statutory time-limit — adjustments are made "at the time of preparing final accounts," i.e., at each accounting period's close.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision in this unit — adjustments are corrective/completing entries, not punitive.

Some expenses are incurred-but-unpaid, some incomes are earned-but-unreceived, and some items (like closing stock) are only known after the trial balance is drawn. These must be brought into the final accounts via adjustment entries so the accounts show a true and fair view.
📝 Probable Question: Why are adjustments necessary while preparing final accounts? (2 marks)
Adjustment entries = journal entries passed at the end of the accounting period to bring in trial-balance omissions and to correctly match revenues with expenses of the period.
4 Reasons Adjustments Are Needed
Record TB omissions (closing stock, interest on capital/drawings)
Bring in outstanding & prepaid expenses
Bring in accrued income & income received in advance
Create reserves & provisions

Decision Tree: Add or Deduct?
Item is OUTSTANDING (expense) or ACCRUED (income) — i.e. earned/incurred but not yet recorded → ADD to the concerned figure
Item is PREPAID (expense) or RECEIVED IN ADVANCE (income) — i.e. recorded but belongs to next period → DEDUCT from the concerned figure
🟢 Quick Revision Direct expenses (wages, carriage inward) → adjust in Trading A/c. Indirect expenses (salaries, rent, office costs) → adjust in P&L A/c. Same add/deduct rule applies either way.
📝 Probable Question: Show the adjustment entry, and treatment in final accounts, for outstanding expenses and prepaid expenses. (5 marks — extremely high repeat rate; also frequently combined into 8-mark "prepare final accounts with the following adjustments" questions)
Applying the master table above, a full Trading A/c, P&L A/c and Balance Sheet is prepared incorporating every adjustment simultaneously — each affecting BOTH the income statement AND the balance sheet.
Worked pattern: Trial balance figure (e.g., Salaries ₹10,000) + Adjustment (Outstanding Salaries ₹1,000, given outside TB) → P&L A/c debit side shows Salaries ₹11,000 → Balance Sheet liabilities side shows Outstanding Salaries ₹1,000.
🟡 Conceptual Understanding The exam trick: adjustment data given OUTSIDE the trial balance (usually in a note below it) needs BOTH a Trading/P&L entry AND a Balance Sheet entry. Adjustment data already INSIDE the trial balance needs only ONE entry (typically Balance Sheet only, since it's already reflected in the ledger balance).
📝 Probable Question: From the given trial balance and adjustments, prepare Trading Account, Profit and Loss Account, and Balance Sheet. (12–15 marks — the single biggest numerical question in Paper I)

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Adjustment entries bring trial-balance omissions and accrual items into the final accounts.
  • Every adjustment has a DUAL effect — once in Trading/P&L A/c, once in the Balance Sheet.
  • Outstanding/accrued items are ADDED; prepaid/advance items are DEDUCTED.
  • Closing stock is valued at the lower of cost or net realisable value (AS-2 Revised).
  • Income tax paid by the business is treated as the proprietor's drawings, not a business expense.
