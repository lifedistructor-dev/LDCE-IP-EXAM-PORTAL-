📚 Rule-wise Detailed Notes नियमवार विस्तृत नोट्स
🟠 Rule 1 — Short Title, Commencement & Application R.1▶

Definition / परिभाषा

Chapter → Preliminary. Sub-Chapter → Applicability. Micro Topic → Exemptions. Nano Topic → Rs.500/month exemption clause.

Rules commence "at once" (1964) and apply to every person appointed to a civil service/post connected with Union affairs, including civilians in Defence Service.

Important Exceptions 🔴

(a) Railway servants under Railway Services (Conduct) Rules; (b) Members of All India Services (IAS, IPS, IFoS); (c) Posts exempted by President's special order.

Second proviso: Rules 4, 6, 7, 12, 14, 15(3), 16, 18(1)(2)(3), 19, 20, 21 don't apply to non-gazetted staff earning ≤ Rs.500/month in ports, defence installations, mines, factories, etc.

Probable Q: Name three categories of persons excluded from CCS Conduct Rules 1964.
MCQ Trap: All India Service members ARE excluded (they have separate Conduct Rules).

🔴 Rule 3 — General (Code of Ethics) R.3▶
Rule 3(1): Every Govt Servant shall...
• Maintain absolute integrity & devotion to duty
• Uphold supremacy of Constitution & democratic values
• Defend sovereignty, integrity, security, public order
• Maintain political neutrality
• Promote merit, fairness, impartiality
• Maintain accountability & transparency
• Not misuse position for financial/material benefit
• Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information

Rule 3(2) — Supervisory & Oral Directions 🔴

Supervisory officer must ensure integrity/devotion of subordinates. Directions should ordinarily be in writing; if oral, official superior must confirm in writing "immediately thereafter"; subordinate must seek written confirmation "as early as possible".

Mnemonic (English): "I DID PMAC" → Integrity, Devotion, Impartial, Democratic values, Political neutrality, Merit, Accountability, Confidentiality.
याद रखने की तरकीब: "ईमानदारी सर्वोपरि" — ई(मानदारी)-सत्यनिष्ठा, कर्तव्यनिष्ठा, राजनीतिक तटस्थता, जवाबदेही।
Probable Q: Oral instruction from superior must be confirmed in writing — who initiates confirmation if superior doesn't?
Answer: The subordinate must seek written confirmation "as early as possible" (Explanation II safeguards this).
🔴 Rules 3-A, 3-B, 3-C R.3A-C▶

3-A: Promptness and Courtesy

No discourteous conduct; no dilatory tactics or wilful delay in disposing work.

3-B: Observance of Government's Policies

Must act per policies on: age of marriage, environment preservation, wildlife/heritage protection, prevention of crime against women.

3-C: Prohibition of Sexual Harassment 🔴 (Very Exam-Important)

Sexual harassment includes: physical contact/advances; demand for sexual favours; sexually coloured remarks; showing pornography; unwelcome conduct of sexual nature.

Workplace includes: Govt-funded establishments, hospitals, sports venues, transport during employment, even a dwelling place/house.

Probable Q: Does "workplace" under Rule 3-C include a dwelling place?
Answer: Yes — explicitly included in the Explanation.
🟠 Rule 4 — Employment of Near Relatives in Companies/Firms R.4▶

No Govt servant may use position/influence to secure employment for family in any company/firm. Class I officers need prior sanction if son/daughter/dependant joins a firm with official dealings; if urgent, employment may be accepted provisionally with post-facto report.

Govt servant must not deal with matters/contracts involving a firm where a family member is employed/interested — refer to superior instead.

🔴 Rule 5 — Taking Part in Politics & Elections R.5▶
Govt Servant + Political Party/Movement
↓
PROHIBITED: membership, association, subscribing, assisting

Voting Rights

May vote (right preserved) but must NOT reveal how they voted or intend to vote. Displaying electoral symbol on person/vehicle/residence = "using influence" (deemed violation).

Family Members

Must try to prevent family from political activity subversive of Government; if unable, must report to Government.

Probable Q: Displaying an electoral symbol on one's residence amounts to what?
Answer: Using influence in connection with election — a violation of Rule 5(4).
🟠 Rules 6 & 7 — Associations, Demonstrations & Strikes R.6-7▶

Rule 6: No joining associations prejudicial to sovereignty, integrity, public order, or morality.

Rule 7: No participation in demonstrations prejudicial to sovereignty/security/foreign relations/public order/decency, or involving contempt of court/defamation. No strikes, coercion, or physical duress related to service matters.

Probable Q: Is participation in a strike allowed under any condition?
Answer: No — absolutely prohibited under Rule 7(ii), including "abetting" a strike.
🔴 Rules 8, 9, 10 — Media, Criticism, Evidence R.8-10▶

Rule 8: Connection with Press/Media

Cannot own/edit/manage newspaper or electronic media without sanction. Publishing a book/participating in media in official discharge is exempt. Must clarify views are personal, not Government's.

Rule 9: Criticism of Government 🔴

No adverse criticism of Central/State Govt policy via broadcast, publication, or press that could embarrass Centre–State or Centre–foreign relations. Exception: bona fide trade union office-bearer views on service conditions (for eligible categories); official-capacity statements exempt.

Rule 10: Evidence before Committee

Sanction needed to give evidence before any enquiry (except: statutory enquiry authorities, judicial enquiry, departmental enquiry by subordinate authority). Cannot criticise Govt policy while giving sanctioned evidence.

Probable Q: In which 3 situations is prior sanction NOT required to give evidence?
Answer: Enquiry by Parliament/State Legislature-appointed authority; judicial enquiry; departmental enquiry by subordinate authority.
🟡 Rules 11 & 12 — Official Info & Subscriptions R.11-12▶

Rule 11: Info must be shared per RTI Act 2005 in good faith. Unauthorized communication of official/classified documents is prohibited unless by general/special order.

Rule 12: Cannot solicit or accept contributions/collections for any purpose without prior sanction.

🔴 Rule 13 — Gifts (HIGH-WEIGHTAGE) R.13▶

"Gift" includes free transport, boarding, lodging, or pecuniary advantage from anyone other than a near relative/personal friend with no official dealings. Casual meal/lift = NOT a gift.

Occasion / GroupReport Threshold
Weddings/functions from relatives/friends (no official dealing) — Group A₹25,000
Same — Group B₹15,000
Same — Group C₹7,500
Any other case — Group A/B (sanction needed above)₹1,500
Any other case — Group C/D (sanction needed above)₹500
From foreign dignitary, one occasion₹1,000

Foreign Firm Gifts 🔴

Cannot accept gift from a foreign firm contracting with/dealing with Govt of India; from other firms, subject to normal sub-rule (3) limits.

Mnemonic: "A-25, B-15, C-7.5 (thousand, on occasions) | A/B-1.5k, C/D-0.5k (otherwise) | Foreign VIP-1k".
याद रखें: ग्रुप घटता है, सीमा भी घटती है (Group A→D, limit decreases)।
Probable Q: A Group C employee receives a wedding gift worth ₹8,000 from a relative — must it be reported?
Answer: Yes, since it exceeds the ₹7,500 threshold for Group C.
🟠 Rule 13-A — Dowry R.13A▶

Total prohibition: giving, taking, abetting, or demanding dowry (directly/indirectly). "Dowry" meaning per Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961.

🟡 Rule 14 — Public Demonstrations in Honour R.14▶

Sanction needed to accept complimentary/valedictory address or testimonial. Exceptions: private/informal farewell on retirement/transfer; simple inexpensive entertainment by public bodies. Coercing Class III/IV staff for subscriptions is forbidden.

Probable Q: Does amateur sports participation need sanction?
Answer: No — expressly exempted under Rule 15(2)(c).
🟢 Rule 15-A — Sub-letting Govt Accommodation R.15A▶

No sub-letting/leasing of allotted Govt accommodation to others. Must vacate within time-limit set by allotting authority once allotment is cancelled.

🔴 Rule 16 — Investment, Lending & Borrowing R.16▶

No speculation in stock/shares (frequent buy-sell = speculation). Occasional investment through licensed brokers is allowed.

Cannot invest in ways that embarrass official duty (e.g., Director-quota shares). Officers deciding IPO/FPO pricing of a CPSE cannot apply for shares in that IPO/FPO (self or family).

No lending/borrowing/depositing money with persons/firms having official dealings, except: small interest-free loans to/from relatives/friends, bona fide tradesman credit accounts, or pay advance to private employee.

Probable Q: Can an officer deciding CPSE share pricing apply for shares in that company's IPO?
Answer: No — explicitly barred under Rule 16(2)(ii), even through family members.
🟡 Rule 17 — Insolvency & Habitual Indebtedness R.17▶

Must manage private affairs to avoid habitual debt/insolvency. Must report legal proceedings for debt recovery/insolvency to Government. Burden of proof that insolvency was due to unforeseeable circumstances lies on the Government servant.

🔴 Rule 18 — Movable, Immovable & Valuable Property (MOST AMENDED) R.18▶
On First Appointment
↓
Submit Return of Assets & Liabilities (immovable property, shares/cash, other movables, debts)

Annual Return 🔴 (Group A & B only)

Must submit annual return of immovable property details — every year.

Prescribed AuthorityApplicable Group
GovernmentGroup A
Head of DepartmentGroup B
Head of OfficeGroup C / D

Key Thresholds ⏱️

• Movable property transaction: report within 1 month if value exceeds 2 months' basic pay.
• Immovable property: previous knowledge of prescribed authority needed always; previous sanction needed if transacting with a person having official dealings.
• Group D: sub-rule (1) not ordinarily applicable unless directed.
• Small movable items under ₹10,000 can be lumped as one figure; daily-use articles excluded.

Movable Property Definition includes

Jewellery, insurance policies (premium > 2 months' pay), shares/securities/debentures, loans given/taken, vehicles, refrigerators/radios/TVs.

Mnemonic: "A-Govt, B-HOD, C/D-HOO" for Prescribed Authority.
याद रखें: "जितनी बड़ी ग्रुप, उतनी ऊँची अथॉरिटी।"
Probable Q: Who is the Prescribed Authority for a Group B officer's property return?
Answer: Head of Department.
🟠 Rule 18-A — Property Outside India / Transactions with Foreigners R.18A▶

Prior sanction of prescribed authority required to: acquire/dispose immovable property outside India, or enter any property transaction (acquisition/disposal) with a foreigner, foreign Government, or foreign organisation — for self or family.

🔴 Rule 19 — Vindication of Acts & Character R.19▶
Official act criticised/defamed
↓
Apply for sanction to approach Court/Press
↓
No reply within 3 months → Deemed GRANTED 🔴

Vindicating private character/acts done in private capacity does NOT require sanction, but action taken must be reported to prescribed authority.

Probable Q: If no reply to a sanction request under Rule 19 is received within how many months, is it deemed granted?
Answer: 3 months.
🟢 Rule 20 — Canvassing Non-official/Outside Influence R.20▶

Prohibits bringing political or outside influence to bear on superior authority to further one's own service interests.

🟠 Rule 21 — Restriction Regarding Marriage R.21▶

No marriage with a person who has a living spouse, nor marrying again while own spouse is living — EXCEPT with Central Government permission (if personal law allows AND other valid grounds exist).

Marrying a non-Indian national → must intimate the Government forthwith.

Probable Q: Under what two conditions may Government permit a bigamous marriage under Rule 21?
Answer: (a) permissible under personal law, AND (b) other valid grounds exist — both conditions needed.
🟠 Rules 22 & 22-A — Intoxication & Child Labour R.22-22A▶

Rule 22: Must abide by local intoxication laws; must NOT be under influence during duty; must not consume in a "public place" (incl. conveyances accessible to public); must not appear intoxicated in public; no excessive use.

Rule 22-A: No employing any child below 14 years of age to work.

🟢 Rules 23, 24, 25 — Interpretation, Delegation, Repeal R.23-25▶

Rule 23: Interpretation disputes go to Government — decision final.

Rule 24: Government may delegate powers (except Rules 23 & 24 itself) to any officer/authority.

Rule 25: Old corresponding rules repealed; prior actions deemed taken under new rules; contravention of old rules still punishable as if under new rules.

Probable Q: In which year was the sexual harassment prohibition (Rule 3-C) first inserted?
Answer: 1998 (GSR 49, dated 7th March 1998).

"3 महीने" (3-month) rule: Rule 19 vindication sanction — silence for 3 months = deemed granted. Only 3-month rule in this Act — easy trap in MCQs vs "1 month" (Rule 18(3) property report) and "14 years" (child labour age, Rule 22-A) and "500/1500/7500/15000/25000" (gift ladder).
