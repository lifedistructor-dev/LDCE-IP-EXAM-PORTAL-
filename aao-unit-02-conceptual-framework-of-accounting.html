📜 Important Rules

  • GAAP — Generally Accepted Accounting Principles: universal norms adopted by accountants for uniformity.
  • Accounting principles = Concepts (basic assumptions) + Conventions (practice-based guidelines).

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Materiality convention is an exception to the Full Disclosure principle — immaterial items need not be disclosed.
  • Conservatism restricts realisation concept further: anticipate no income, but provide for all possible losses.

📅 Important Dates

  • 21 April 1977: ICAI Council constituted the Accounting Standards Board (ASB).

🏛️ Important Authorities

  • ICAI — issues Accounting Standards in India.
  • ASB — formulates AS under ICAI Council.
  • IASB — International Accounting Standards Board, issues IFRS.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

No statutory time-limit in this unit — Periodicity concept fixes the reporting period, generally one year.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision specified in this conceptual unit.

Batliboi: "Book-keeping is an art of recording business dealings in a set of books."
Carter: "Book-keeping is the science and art of recording correctly in the books of account all business transactions of money or money's worth."
Micro TopicKey Points
FeaturesRecording process · monetary transactions only · primary stage · includes journalising & ledger posting
ObjectivesPermanent record · ascertain profit/loss · ascertain financial position · know progress · find tax liability · fulfil legal requirements
AdvantagesSystematic record · financial info · control · legal evidence · comparison · tax liability
LimitationsOnly monetary txns · ignores price-level changes · historical data only
🟡 Conceptual Understanding Book-keeping is routine and clerical; accounting is analytical. Book-keeping feeds accounting, not the other way round.

Is it a basic assumption behind the accounting system (i–xi)? → Concept
Is it a usage/practice adopted for realistic reporting (xii–xiv)? → Convention

📝 Probable Question: Why are Accounting Standards needed? State any three reasons. (3 marks)
IFRS is issued by the IASB (International Accounting Standards Board) — a set of international standards on how transactions/events should be reported, aimed at worldwide acceptability and improved global financial reporting.
📝 Probable Question: Who issues IFRS and what is its purpose? (2 marks)
21 April 1977

ICAI Council constitutes the Accounting Standards Board (ASB), recognising the need for AS in India.

Ongoing / present

Globalisation drives convergence of Indian AS with IFRS → results in Ind AS.

Current practice

Big companies follow Ind AS; smaller units may continue with AS. Full migration to Ind AS expected in future.

Ind AS = International Accounting Standards modified to suit Indian accounting practices, customs & traditions.
🟡 Conceptual Understanding ASB formulates AS considering Indian law, custom, business environment AND the International Accounting Standards — it is not a blind copy of IFRS.
