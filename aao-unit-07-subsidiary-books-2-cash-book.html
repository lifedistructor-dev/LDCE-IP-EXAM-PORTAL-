★ One-Page Revision Sheet

What Makes Cash Book Special

  • Both a subsidiary book AND a principal book (ledger) — no separate Cash A/c needed in ledger

3 Types of Cash Book

  • Single column (cash only)
  • Double column (cash + discount)
  • Three column (cash + discount + bank)

Cash Discount vs Trade Discount

  • Cash discount → recorded in books, given for prompt payment
  • Trade discount → never recorded, given on bulk purchase

Contra Entry

  • Cash ↔ Bank transactions — marked "C" in L.F., no further ledger posting needed

Imprest System

  • Fixed advance to petty cashier; reimbursed exactly what was spent to restore the fixed float

Petty Cash Book Types

  • Simple petty cash book
  • Analytical petty cash book (columnar, by expense head)

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Rules

  • Cash book receipts = Debit side; Payments = Credit side, exactly like a ledger account.
  • Cash column is always balanced; Discount columns are only totalled, never balanced.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Cash column can NEVER show a credit balance — total receipts always exceed total payments.
  • Bank column CAN show a credit balance (overdraft) — unlike the cash column.

📅 Important Dates

No historical dates — imprest is topped up periodically (weekly/fortnightly/monthly) per business policy.

🏛️ Important Authorities

Internal bookkeeping procedure — no external regulatory authority cited in this unit.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Petty cashier reimbursed at end of each period so the float returns to the original imprest amount before the next period begins.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — petty cashier authority is simply capped at a specified payment limit; larger payments route through the main cashier.

Cash columns → post to the OTHER account named on each side, with Cash A/c as the counter-entry (already in cash book)
Discount Allowed total (Dr side) → Debit Discount Allowed A/c "To Sundry Accounts as per Cash Book"
Discount Received total (Cr side) → Credit Discount Received A/c "By Sundry Accounts as per Cash Book"
📝 Probable Question: Why is the discount column of the cash book not balanced? (2 marks)
Adds a bank column alongside cash & discount. Cash balance is always Debit; Bank balance can be Debit (favourable) or Credit (overdraft/unfavourable).
Contra entry = a transaction where BOTH aspects (debit & credit) are recorded within the cash book itself, because both Cash A/c and Bank A/c live there. Marked "C" in the L.F. column — no further ledger posting needed.

📝 Probable Question: What is a contra entry? Give two examples with their treatment in the cash book. (4 marks — very frequently asked)
Petty cash book records small, repetitive expenses (postage, stationery, carriage, refreshments) kept separate from the main cash book. Maintained by the petty cashier.
Imprest System: a fixed advance ("Imprest Money") is given to the petty cashier at the start of each period. At period end, the head cashier reimburses exactly the amount spent — restoring the float to the original fixed amount.
Simple Petty Cash Book
  • Resembles single column cash book
  • One column for all payments
Analytical Petty Cash Book
  • Separate column per expense head + total column
  • Postage, Stationery, Carriage, Travelling, Refreshments, Sundries, Personal A/cs
4 Advantages of Petty Cash Book
Better control over petty payments
Saves main cashier's time
Main cash book not overloaded
Easier posting
🟢 Quick Revision Petty cashier can only PAY (never receive, except reimbursement) and only within a specified payment limit — larger payments go through the main cashier.

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Cash book records only cash (and bank) transactions, in chronological order.
  • Cash book is both a subsidiary book and a principal (ledger) book.
  • Cash discount is recorded in books; trade discount is never recorded.
  • Contra entries (cash ↔ bank) are marked "C" and need no further ledger posting.
  • Petty cash is managed under the imprest system for effective control.
