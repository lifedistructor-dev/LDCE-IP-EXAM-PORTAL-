🗂 Chapter 1: Topic Overview (विषय परिचय)

Topic: Delegation of Financial Powers Rules, 1978 (DFPR) — issued by Finance Department, Govt. of Orissa vide No. 12000-Codes-12/78-F, superseding Finance Dept. Resolution No. 14028-F dated 1 April 1967.

विषय: यह नियम राज्य सरकार के विभिन्न प्राधिकारियों (Departments, Heads of Departments, Collectors आदि) को वित्तीय व्यय स्वीकृत करने की शक्तियाँ प्रत्यायोजित (delegate) करता है।

💰 Chapter 3: Budgeting, Funds Provision & Allotment — Rules 3–5
🟠 Rule 3: Budgeting Process▶

Finance Dept. indicates ceilings of committed expenditure in advance. Schemes continuing >2 years admitted based on past actuals.

1️⃣ Finance Dept. sets expenditure ceilings by Major Heads
2️⃣ Controlling Officers report past actuals (Accountant-General too)
3️⃣ Files for continuing non-plan schemes (<2 yrs) → sent to Finance Dept. for pre-Budget scrutiny
4️⃣ Finance Dept. prepares consolidated Budget slips by Major Heads
5️⃣ Planning & Co-ordination Dept. indicates Plan ceilings
6️⃣ Committee scrutiny (Secretary + HoD + Financial Adviser + Finance + Planning reps)
7️⃣ New Demand Schedules furnished to Finance Dept.

🟢 Quick Point: No lump sum Budget provision except works < Rs.10 lakh; splitting estimates to stay under Rs.10 lakh is prohibited.

Exam Q: Lump sum Budget provision permissible for works costing less than?
Ans: Rs. 10 lakhs (Rule 3(6))
🟡 Rule 4: Provision of Funds by Legislature▶

Demands for grants/appropriation for Charged Expenditure presented to Legislature. After Demands voted & Appropriation Act passed → funds become available for appropriation/re-appropriation.

🔴 Rule 5: Allotment of Funds▶
  • Administrative Depts allot provision among Controlling Officers before start of financial year.
  • Schemes reserved for scrutiny → allotment restricted to first 4 months (till end of June) for continuing posts, contingencies & TA.
  • Expenditure can be incurred only after appropriation authorised by law.
Exam Q: Restricted allotment period for schemes under scrutiny = ?
Ans: Till end of June (4 months) 🔴
⚖️ Chapter 4: General Limitations & Residuary Powers — Rules 6–7
🔴 Rule 6: General Limitations on Power to Sanction Expenditure▶
  • Public revenue spent only on legitimate objects of public expenditure.
  • Recurring sanction operative once funds for 1st year appropriated; continues in subsequent years subject to appropriation & sanction terms.
  • Authority can sanction expenditure only if authorised by: (a) these/other rules (b) provisions of law (c) general/special order of Governor.
  • New principle/practice likely to increase future expenditure → needs prior Finance Dept. concurrence.
  • No expenditure without funds available via appropriation/re-appropriation.
Can authority sanction expenditure?
✅ Yes — if funds available + authorised by rules/law/order
❌ No — if involves new principle without Finance Dept. concurrence
❌ No — if funds not appropriated/re-appropriated
Exam Q: Sanctioning new principle likely to increase future expenditure requires?
Ans: Prior concurrence of Finance Department (Rule 6(4)) 🔴
🟢 Rule 7: Residuary Financial Powers▶

Any financial power not specifically delegated to any authority vests in the Finance Department.

Exam Q: Powers not delegated under DFPR vest in? Ans: Finance Department 🟢 (very easy recall question)
👥 Chapter 5: Creation of Posts & Re-appropriation — Rules 8–9
🔴 Rule 8: Creation, Extension & Permanency of Temporary Posts▶
  • Old practice: create/extend temp. posts 1 year at a time → avoidable paperwork. New rule: posts continue until terminated by competent authority (Finance Dept. Resolution No. 10806-F dated 2 March 1978).
  • Conditions for creating temp. posts: (i) specific Budget provision with Finance Dept. concurrence (ii) Rules of Business observed (iii) similar posts must exist on approved pay scale.
  • Permanency of temp. posts: full powers with Admin Dept., BUT NOT for posts to be made permanent before 3 years or linked to time-bound schemes.
  • Post in Govt. Company/undertaking with max pay ≥ Rs. 4,100 → needs Govt. approval + Finance Dept. concurrence + Chief Minister's orders via Minister.
Exam Q: Post in Govt. Company requiring CM's approval — pay threshold? Ans: Rs. 4,100 or more 🔴
Exam Q: Permanency of temp. post barred if made permanent before? Ans: 3 years
🔴 Rule 9: Re-appropriation — 12 Key Restrictions▶

Administrative Depts have full powers to sanction re-appropriation subject to:

#Restriction
iNo transfer between Major Heads of account
iiNo re-appropriation from Plan grants to Non-Plan expenditure
iiiNo transfer across State/Central/Centrally Sponsored Plan groups
ivNo transfer from special non-plan scheme grants
vNo funds for expenditure not sanctioned by competent authority
viCharged ↔ Voted expenditure: no inter-transfer
viiNo transfer to/from Secret Service expenditure
viiiNo transfer between Primary Units augmenting provision by >25%
ixOffice Exp./TA/Contingent — no augmentation between primary units for special purposes (e.g. vehicle purchase)
x"Other allowances" cannot be augmented
xiNo re-appropriation from savings met via Reserve Funds/Statutory Bodies
xiiWorks expenditure — needs admin approval + technical sanction; excess needs revised sanction

🟠 Rule 11 & 11A: Grants, Loans, Stipends & Share Capital Investment▶
  • Admin Depts: full powers for grant-in-aid (incl. Stipends/Scholarships) & loans, subject to Finance Dept. concurrence on rules, interest rate & repayment period.
  • Heads of Depts competent to sanction grants-in-aid if allotment under sub-head not exceeded; utilisation certificate must be furnished for earlier grants.
  • Rule 11A (Investment of share capital): Admin Depts may release funds NOT exceeding Rs. 10 lakh/annum as equity in wholly State-owned Statutory Corporation/Company; Rs. 1 lakh/annum for other corporate bodies.
Exam Q: Max equity investment/annum in State-owned Statutory Corporation? Ans: Rs. 10 lakh (Rule 11A) 🟠
Exam Q: Limit for "other corporate bodies" under 11A? Ans: Rs. 1 lakh/annum

🧮 Chapter 8: Refund of Revenue, Write-off & Stationery — Rules 14–16
🟡 Rule 14: Power to Refund Revenue▶

Authority in whose favour revenue credited may refund it for: (a) wrong credits (b) over-payments (c) abandoned scheme where fee realised in advance. Refund procedure per Orissa GFR Rules 166–169 & Orissa Treasury Code SR 345–349-A.

🟢 Rule 16: Local Purchase of Stationery▶

Local purchase generally prohibited. Exceptionally, Head of Office may sanction up to Rs.400 at a time, subject to max Rs.1,000 in a financial year.

Exam Q: Max local stationery purchase per financial year? Ans: Rs. 1,000 (single instance cap Rs.400) 🟢

✅ Chapter 9: Expenditure Sanction, Insurance, Trading, Subordinate Powers — Rules 17–20
🔴 Rule 17: Expenditure Sanction▶

Required only for schemes/services with financial provision via New Demand Schedules.

  • Works Projects (PWD Code): no separate sanction needed if Budget provision available, but admin approval must precede commencement.
  • If work begins before admin approval (exceptional): Govt. authorisation needed; admin approval must follow within 3 months of commencement, else no further expenditure allowed.
  • Unresolved pre-Budget schemes: Admin Dept. may sanction expenditure for first 4 months (till end June) — restricted to continuing posts, contingent/TA, grants-in-aid for continuing schemes.
  • Prior Finance Dept. concurrence needed for: time-bound post continuance, purchase of tools/plant/vehicles/heavy machinery, and contingent expenses exceeding Rs.1 lakh.
Day 0: Work commences on Govt. authorisation (without admin approval)
Within 3 months: Administrative approval MUST be obtained
After 3 months (if not obtained): No further expenditure can be incurred 🔴
Exam Q: Time limit to obtain admin approval after work commences on authorisation? Ans: 3 months 🔴 Very important time-limit question!
🟡 Rule 18: Insurance of Government Property▶

Govt. property ordinarily NOT insured. No subordinate authority may undertake insurance expenditure without prior Finance Dept. concurrence — except where Admin Dept./HoD has full discretion to sanction (their own decision to insure).

🟡 Rule 19: Trading Operations▶

All proposals for: (a) purchase of commodities for sale/issue to public (b) fixing prices of direct trading operations (c) Govt. companies' price fixation — must go to Finance Dept. for concurrence.

🟢 Exception: Proposals under (a)/(b) need NOT go to Finance Dept. if transaction value is below Rs. 2 lakh.

Exam Q: Threshold below which trading proposals skip Finance Dept. concurrence? Ans: Rs. 2 lakh 🟡

🧑‍💼 Chapter 10: Miscellaneous & Financial Adviser — Rules 21–22
🟢 Rule 21: Miscellaneous▶
  • Unspecified matters → governed by general/special orders with Finance Dept. concurrence.
  • Authority already exercising higher powers on an item shall continue to do so.
  • Powers delegated apply to past cases too (pending on date rules came into force); expenditure already incurred beyond powers = irregular, needs ex-post-facto sanction by authority competent when incurred (not later).
  • Delegated powers subject to any general/special economy ban by State Govt.
Exam Q: Ex-post-facto sanction for irregular expenditure — by whom? Ans: Authority competent to incur expenditure AT THE TIME it was incurred, not the authority who later became competent. 🟢 Tricky conceptual Q!
🔴 Rule 22: Financial Adviser — Duties & Responsibilities▶

Financial Advisers/Assistant FAs assist Admin Depts in Budget formulation, project scrutiny, and post-Budget vigilance.

Key duties (Annexure E, 21 points) include:

  • Ensure Budget drawn per Finance Dept. instructions; scrutinise Budget proposals
  • Ensure complete departmental accounts maintained per Orissa GFR
  • Watch/review expenditure progress vs sanctioned grants; issue timely warnings
  • Maintain Register of liabilities & commitments
  • Screen supplementary demand proposals & further delegation proposals
  • Associate with scheme formulation from initial stage (cardinal principle)
  • Ensure compliance of audit objections/inspection reports/Draft Audit Paras
  • Screen expenditure proposals referred to Finance Dept.
  • Reconcile departmental figures with Accountant-General monthly; finalise accounts before end of June
  • Control/supervise Internal Auditors; ensure prompt revenue collection

🟡 Important carve-out: FA should NOT be burdened with routine service matters — Subsistence Allowance, Special Pay, Pay Fixation, Pension, TA pre-audit, disciplinary proceedings — as these detract from core duties.

🔴 Where Secretary proposes to overrule FA's advice → reference must be made to Finance Department.

Exam Q: If Secretary wants to overrule Financial Adviser's advice, what must happen? Ans: Matter referred to Finance Department 🔴
Exam Q: Reconciliation of final accounts of year must be completed before? Ans: End of June

🔴 MUST-KNOW NUMBERS (DFPR 1978)

  • Rules effective from: 1 April 1978
  • Superseded Resolution: No.14028-F dated 1 April 1967
  • Lump sum Budget provision cap: Rs. 10 lakh works
  • Restricted allotment period: till end June (4 months)
  • Re-appropriation augmentation cap between Primary Units: 25%
  • Post in Govt. Company needing CM approval: pay ≥ Rs.4,100
  • Temp. post permanency bar: before 3 years
  • Contracts — HoD limit: Rs.12.5 lakh; Admin Dept: Full powers
  • Committee (HoD office) scrutiny band: Rs.10,000 – Rs.25 lakh
  • Admin approval after unauthorised work start: within 3 months
  • Contingent expenditure needing Finance concurrence: > Rs.1 lakh
  • Trading proposal exemption threshold: below Rs.2 lakh
  • 11A Equity investment: Rs.10 lakh (Statutory Corp) / Rs.1 lakh (other corporate)
  • Local stationery purchase: Rs.400/time, Rs.1,000/year
  • Write-off powers: Admin Dept 15,000 | Member BoR 10,000 | HoD 7,500 | Collector 3,000 | Subordinate Office 1,500
  • Annual establishment review — Secretaries report to Finance Dept. by 15 April
  • Reconciliation of final accounts before: end of June
