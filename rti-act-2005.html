Act No.: 22 of 2005 | Assented: 15 June 2005 | Extends to whole of India
Commencement: Ss.4(1), 5(1)(2), 12, 13, 15, 16, 24, 27, 28 — at once; rest on 120th day of enactment (i.e. 12 Oct 2005)
Total Chapters: 6 | Total Sections: 31 | 2 Schedules (Oath form; Excluded intelligence/security orgs)
Reply time (normal): 30 days | Life/liberty matters: 48 hours
Via Assistant PIO: +5 extra days added to the 30/48-hr clock
Transfer to other public authority: within 5 days
Third party notice: within 5 days; third party representation: 10 days; PIO decision: within 40 days
First Appeal: 30 days (to senior officer) | Second Appeal: 90 days (to CIC/SIC)
Appeal disposal: 30 days, extendable to max 45 days (with reasons)
Penalty: ₹250/day, max ₹25,000 per PIO, per request
20-year rule (S.8(3)): Info older than 20 years must be given (except clauses a, c, i of S.8(1))
CIC/SIC composition: 1 Chief + up to 10 Commissioners
2019 Amendment: Fixed 5-year term replaced by "term prescribed by Central Govt" (Ss.13, 16)
Repealed Act: Freedom of Information Act, 2002 (S.31)

📚 Section-wise Detailed Notes धारावार विस्तृत नोट्स
🟠 Section 1 — Short Title, Extent & Commencement S.1▶

Act extends to whole of India (J&K exclusion removed by Act 34 of 2019, effective 31-10-2019 — J&K reorganisation).

Ss.4(1), 5(1)&(2), 12, 13, 15, 16, 24, 27, 28 → in force at once. Remaining provisions → 120th day after enactment (12 October 2005).

Probable Q: Which sections came into force immediately (at once) on enactment?
Answer: Ss.4(1), 5(1)&(2), 12, 13, 15, 16, 24, 27, 28.
🔴 Section 2 — Definitions S.2▶
TermMeaning

Competent AuthoritySpeaker (LS/Assembly), Chairman (RS/Council), CJI (SC), Chief Justice (HC), President/Governor (other constitutional bodies), Administrator (Art. 239)
InformationAny material in any form — records, documents, memos, e-mails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, samples, models, electronic data; incl. info re: private body accessible under any other law

Mnemonic: "PARC-3T" → Public authority, Appropriate govt, Record, Competent authority, Third party, Information, RTI.
Probable Q: Does the definition of "third party" include a public authority?
Answer: Yes — explicitly included in S.2(n).
🟡 Section 3 — Right to Information S.3▶

Subject to the Act's provisions, all citizens shall have the right to information. (Note: right is for "citizens" only, not all persons.)

Probable Q: Is the right to information under RTI Act available to foreign nationals?
Answer: No — the Act confers the right specifically on "citizens".
🔴 Section 4 — Obligations of Public Authorities (Proactive Disclosure) S.4▶

S.4(1)(a): Record-keeping

Maintain records catalogued/indexed; computerise appropriate records within reasonable time, subject to resources.

S.4(1)(b): Suo-motu Publication (17 items) 🔴

Within 120 days of enactment, publish: org particulars/functions/duties; officer powers/duties; decision-making procedure; norms; rules/manuals; document categories; public consultation arrangements; boards/committees info; officer directory; monthly remuneration; budget; subsidy programme details; concessions/permits recipients; e-form info; facilities for citizens; PIO details; other prescribed info — update annually.

S.4(1)(c)&(d)

Publish relevant facts while formulating policies affecting public; provide reasons for administrative/quasi-judicial decisions to affected persons.

S.4(2): Suo Motu Spirit

Constant endeavour to provide info suo motu so public needs minimal recourse to formal RTI requests.

Mnemonic: "120 days to publish, then update every year" — Same 120-day figure as commencement, easy to confuse; remember: commencement 120 days is for the Act, S.4(1)(b) 120 days is for publication after enactment.
Probable Q: Within how many days of enactment must a public authority publish the 17 categories under S.4(1)(b)?
Answer: 120 days, updated every year thereafter.
🔴 Section 5 — Designation of Public Information Officers S.5▶
Within 100 days of enactment
↓
Designate CPIO/SPIO at all administrative units [S.5(1)]
Designate Assistant PIO at sub-divisional level [S.5(2)]

Assistant PIO Role 🔴

Receives applications/appeals & forwards to PIO/senior officer/Commission. Adds 5 days to the S.7(1) response period when application routed via APIO.

PIO may seek assistance of any other officer [S.5(4)]; that officer must render assistance and is treated as PIO for contravention purposes [S.5(5)].

Probable Q: If an RTI application is submitted to an Assistant PIO, how many extra days are added to the response period?
Answer: 5 days.
🟠 Section 6 — Request for Obtaining Information S.6▶

Request in writing/electronic, in English, Hindi, or the local official language, with prescribed fee, to PIO or Assistant PIO.

No reason needed — applicant not required to disclose why info is sought (S.6(2)), only contact details.

Transfer of Misdirected Applications 🔴

If info held by/more closely connected with another public authority, application transferred within 5 days, and applicant informed immediately.

Probable Q: Is an applicant required to state reasons for seeking information?
Answer: No — S.6(2) explicitly exempts this.
🔴 Section 7 — Disposal of Request (HIGH-WEIGHTAGE) S.7▶
SituationTime Limit
Normal request30 days
Life or liberty concerned48 hours
Via Assistant PIO+5 days added
No decision given in timeDeemed refusal (S.7(2))
Info free if authority misses deadlineS.7(6)

Fee & Free Categories

Fee must be reasonable; no fee for persons below poverty line (BPL). If time limit breached, info provided free of charge.

Rejection Communication (S.7(8))

Must state: (i) reasons for rejection, (ii) appeal period, (iii) appellate authority particulars.

Info provided in form sought unless it would disproportionately divert resources or harm record preservation (S.7(9)). Sensorily disabled persons get special assistance (S.7(4)).

Mnemonic: "30-48-5" → 30 days normal, 48 hours life/liberty, +5 days via APIO.
Probable Q: If a PIO fails to decide within the specified period, what is the legal consequence?
Answer: Deemed refusal of the request (S.7(2)).
🔴 Section 8 — Exemptions from Disclosure (MOST IMPORTANT) S.8▶
S.8(1): No obligation to disclose...
a) Prejudicial to sovereignty/integrity/security/strategic/economic interests/foreign relations/incitement to offence
b) Expressly forbidden by court/tribunal; contempt of court
c) Breach of privilege of Parliament/State Legislature
d) Commercial confidence/trade secrets/IP — unless larger public interest
e) Fiduciary relationship — unless larger public interest
f) Received in confidence from foreign Government
g) Endangers life/physical safety; reveals source of law-enforcement info
h) Impedes investigation/apprehension/prosecution
i) Cabinet papers incl. Council of Ministers deliberations (but disclosed after decision + matter complete)
j) Personal info with no public activity nexus / unwarranted privacy invasion — unless larger public interest

Overriding Proviso 🔴

Info that cannot be denied to Parliament/State Legislature cannot be denied to any person (S.8(1)(j) proviso).

S.8(2): Public Interest Override

Notwithstanding Official Secrets Act 1923 or any S.8(1) exemption, disclosure allowed if public interest in disclosure outweighs harm to protected interests.

S.8(3): 20-Year Rule 🔴

Info relating to any occurrence >20 years old must be provided, EXCEPT clauses (a), (c), (i) of S.8(1) — i.e. sovereignty/security, parliamentary privilege, and cabinet papers remain exempt even after 20 years.

Mnemonic: "20-year survivors = a, c, i" → sovereigntY, breach of PrivilegE (Parliament), CabineT papers. Remember "ACI stays exempt."
Probable Q: Which exemption clauses survive even after the 20-year rule under S.8(3)?
Answer: Clauses (a), (c), and (i) of S.8(1).
🟡 Sections 9 & 10 — Copyright Rejection & Severability S.9-10▶

S.9: PIO may reject a request if it would infringe copyright subsisting in a person other than the State.

S.10: If part of a record is exempt, the non-exempt part must be severed and provided; applicant must be notified of the severance, reasons, officer's name, fee, and appeal rights.

🟠 Section 11 — Third Party Information S.11▶
PIO intends to disclose third-party info treated as confidential
↓ within 5 days
Written notice to third party
↓ within 10 days
Third party makes representation
↓ within 40 days of original request
PIO's final decision + written notice to third party

Except trade/commercial secrets, disclosure allowed if public interest outweighs harm to third party. Third party can appeal under S.19 within 30 days.

Mnemonic: "5-10-40" for third party: notice in 5, representation window 10, decision by 40.
Probable Q: Within how many days must the PIO decide on third-party disclosure after receiving the original request?
Answer: 40 days (notwithstanding the normal S.7 time limit).
🔴 Sections 12-14 — Central Information Commission S.12-14▶

Constitution (S.12)

By notification in Official Gazette. Consists of: Chief Information Commissioner (CIC) + up to 10 Information Commissioners.

Appointment Committee 🔴

President appoints on recommendation of: (i) PM (Chairperson), (ii) Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha, (iii) a Union Cabinet Minister nominated by PM.

Eligibility & Restrictions

Persons of eminence in law, science, tech, social service, management, journalism, mass media, administration/governance. Cannot be MP/MLA, hold office of profit, be connected with political party, or carry on business/profession.

Headquarters

Delhi; may set up offices elsewhere with Central Govt approval.

Term (S.13) — 2019 Amendment 🔴

Originally fixed 5-year term; now term "as prescribed by Central Government" — but age cap remains 65 years. Not eligible for reappointment. IC vacating office is eligible for appointment as CIC (max 5 years aggregate in both roles).

Removal (S.14)

By President on proved misbehaviour/incapacity, after Supreme Court inquiry on Presidential reference. Also removable for: insolvency, moral turpitude conviction, paid employment outside office, unfitness (mind/body), or prejudicial financial interest.

Mnemonic: "PM-LOP-Minister" for CIC appointment committee; "P-M-L" easy recall.
Probable Q: What change did the RTI (Amendment) Act, 2019 bring to the Chief Information Commissioner's term?
Answer: Replaced the fixed 5-year term with a term "as prescribed by the Central Government," while retaining the 65-year age cap.
🟠 Sections 15-17 — State Information Commission S.15-17▶

Mirrors CIC structure at State level. State Chief Information Commissioner + up to 10 State Information Commissioners, appointed by Governor on recommendation of committee: CM (Chairperson), Leader of Opposition in Assembly, a Cabinet Minister nominated by CM.

Removal by Governor (same grounds as S.14, via Supreme Court reference). Headquarters at place specified by State Govt notification.

Probable Q: Who appoints the State Chief Information Commissioner?
Answer: The Governor, on the recommendation of a committee headed by the Chief Minister.
🟠 Section 18 — Powers & Functions of Information Commissions S.18▶

Receives & inquires into complaints re: PIO not appointed, refused access, no timely response, unreasonable fee, incomplete/misleading/false info, or any other RTI-related matter.

Civil Court Powers (S.18(3)) 🔴

Summoning/enforcing attendance; requiring discovery/inspection of documents; receiving evidence on affidavit; requisitioning public records; issuing summons for witnesses/documents; any other prescribed matter.

Commission may examine any record under public authority's control during inquiry — no record can be withheld on any grounds (S.18(4)).

Probable Q: Under which law's provisions does the Commission derive its civil-court-like powers?
Answer: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (S.18(3)).
🔴 Section 19 — Appeal (HIGH-WEIGHTAGE) S.19▶
AppealToTime Limit
First AppealSenior officer (in same public authority)30 days (extendable if sufficient cause)
Third-party appeal against S.11 disclosure orderSenior officer30 days from date of order
Second AppealCIC / SIC90 days (extendable if sufficient cause)
Appeal disposal—30 days, extendable to max 45 days (reasons recorded)

Onus of Proof 🔴

Burden to prove denial was justified lies on the PIO who denied the request (S.19(5)).

Commission's Powers on Decision (S.19(8))

Require public authority to: provide access; appoint PIO; publish info; change record-management practices; enhance staff training; provide annual report — plus compensate complainant for loss/detriment, impose penalties, or reject application.

CIC/SIC decisions are binding (S.19(7)). Third party gets reasonable hearing opportunity if decision relates to their info (S.19(4)).

Mnemonic: "30-30-90-45" → First appeal 30 days, third-party appeal 30 days, second appeal 90 days, max disposal 45 days.
Probable Q: On whom does the burden of proof lie in appeal proceedings under RTI?
Answer: On the PIO who denied the request (S.19(5)).
🔴 Section 20 — Penalties (HIGH-WEIGHTAGE) S.20▶
PIO — without reasonable cause — refuses application / misses deadline / malafide denial / incorrect-incomplete-misleading info / destroys info / obstructs
↓
Penalty: ₹250/day (till info furnished), MAX ₹25,000 🔴

PIO gets reasonable opportunity of hearing before penalty imposed; burden to prove reasonable & diligent conduct lies on the PIO (S.20(1) proviso).

S.20(2): Disciplinary Action

If failure is persistent/without reasonable cause, Commission recommends disciplinary action against the PIO under applicable service rules (separate from monetary penalty).

Mnemonic: "250 x 100 = 25,000" → ₹250/day penalty caps at ₹25,000 (i.e., after 100 days of delay).
Probable Q: What is the maximum penalty a PIO can face under Section 20 for one complaint?
Answer: ₹25,000 (at ₹250/day).
🔴 Sections 21-24 — Good Faith, Overriding Effect, Court Bar, Exclusions S.21-24▶

S.21: No suit/prosecution/legal proceeding against anyone for good-faith action under the Act.

S.22: RTI Act has overriding effect over Official Secrets Act 1923 and any inconsistent law.

S.23: Courts barred from entertaining suits re: orders under this Act — only remedy is appeal under the Act.

S.24: Intelligence & Security Organisations Excluded 🔴

Act does not apply to organisations in the Second Schedule (e.g., IB, R&AW, CBI, NIA, Enforcement Directorate, NTRO, etc.) — EXCEPT:

• Info on corruption and human rights violations allegations is NOT excluded.
• Human rights violation info: only after CIC/SIC approval, provided within 45 days.

Probable Q: Even for excluded intelligence organisations, what two categories of information must still be disclosed?
Answer: Allegations of corruption, and allegations of human rights violations.
🟡 Sections 25-30 — Monitoring, Programmes & Rule-Making S.25-30▶

S.25: CIC/SIC prepares annual report on Act's implementation; laid before Parliament/State Legislature.

S.26: Appropriate Govt develops educational programmes; must compile a public guide within 18 months of commencement.

S.27 & 28: Rule-making power of appropriate Government and competent authority respectively (fees, term/salary of Commissioners post-2019 amendment, appeal procedure, etc.)

S.29: Rules laid before Parliament/State Legislature for 30 days (modification/annulment possible).

S.30: Power to remove difficulties — Central Govt order, but not after 2 years from commencement.

Probable Q: Within how many months of commencement must the appropriate Government publish a public guide under S.26?
Answer: 18 months.
🟠 Section 31 & Schedules S.31 + Sch.▶

S.31 — Repeal: Freedom of Information Act, 2002 is repealed by RTI Act, 2005.

First Schedule

Form of Oath/Affirmation for CIC/IC/State CIC/SIC — swear/affirm allegiance to Constitution, uphold sovereignty & integrity of India, perform duties without fear/favour.

Second Schedule — Excluded Organisations (partial list)

Intelligence Bureau, R&AW, Directorate of Revenue Intelligence, CBI, NIA, Enforcement Directorate, Narcotics Control Bureau, BSF, CRPF, CISF, NSG, Assam Rifles, Special Protection Group, DRDO, National Security Council Secretariat, etc. (26 organisations, amended periodically).

Probable Q: Which Act was repealed by the RTI Act, 2005?
Answer: The Freedom of Information Act, 2002.
