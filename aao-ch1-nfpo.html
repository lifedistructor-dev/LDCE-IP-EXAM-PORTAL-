🏛️ 1.1 Meaning & Characteristics 🔴

Definition: Not-for-Profit Organisations (NPOs) are entities set up for the welfare of society — clubs, charitable institutions, schools, hospitals, religious bodies, trade unions — that function without profit motive. Main aim = service, not profit.

Key Characteristics

  • Formed to serve a specific group / public at large (education, health, sports, culture)
  • Organised as trusts / societies; contributors called members
  • Managed by an elected managing/executive committee
  • Income sources: subscriptions, donations, legacies, grant-in-aid, investment income
  • Funds credited to Capital Fund / General Fund (not "Capital A/c")
  • Surplus (Income − Expenditure) is NOT distributed — added to Capital Fund
  • Reputation built on service to society, not profit
  • Accounting info meant for contributors + statutory compliance (Registrar of Societies)
🧠 Mnemonic — "SOMCAR": Service motive, Organised as trust, Managed by committee, Capital fund (not capital a/c), Accountable to Registrar
📝 Probable Q: "Distinguish between Profit-seeking entities and Not-for-Profit entities." (Very common 3-4 mark question)

📚 1.2 Accounting Records 🟡

NPOs maintain 3 core records:

  1. Cash Book — all receipts & payments
  2. Ledger — income, expense, asset, liability accounts
  3. Stock Register — fixed assets & consumables
Note: NPOs do NOT maintain a "Capital Account" — instead they maintain Capital Fund / General Fund, accumulated from surpluses, life membership fees, donations, legacies.

Final Accounts / Financial Statements (3 statements)

Final Accounts of NPO
Receipt & Payment A/c
Income & Expenditure A/c
Balance Sheet
📝 Probable Q: "What are the financial statements prepared by a Not-for-Profit Organisation?" — always list all 3, in order.

💰 1.3 Receipt & Payment Account 🔴

Definition: Summary of the Cash Book — records ALL cash & bank receipts/payments during the year, regardless of period (past/current/future) or nature (capital/revenue).

Salient Features (🟠 frequently asked as "features/characteristics")

  • It's a summarised Cash Book (Dr = Receipts, Cr = Payments)
  • Shows total amounts irrespective of period — includes arrears & advances
  • Includes BOTH capital and revenue receipts/payments
  • No cash/bank distinction — single total per item
  • NO non-cash items (no depreciation, no outstanding/accrued items)
  • Opens with opening cash/bank balance (or overdraft); closes with year-end balance

Format (Fig 1.1)

Receipts (Dr)Payments (Cr)
Balance b/d (Cash/Bank)Insurance, Salaries, Rent, Rates
Subscriptions, Entrance FeesPrinting & Stationery, Postage

Steps to Prepare

1. Enter opening cash/bank balance (Dr side)
↓
2. Enter total of ALL receipts (any period)
↓
3. Enter total of ALL payments (any period)
↓
4. Balancing figure = Closing cash/bank
📝 Probable Q: Numerical — "Prepare Receipt and Payment Account from a given Cash Book." Also theory: "State the features of Receipt and Payment Account."

📊 1.4 Income & Expenditure Account 🔴

Definition: Equivalent of Profit & Loss A/c. Prepared on accrual basis. Records ONLY revenue items of the current year. Balance = Surplus (income>expenditure) or Deficit (expenditure>income), transferred to Capital Fund.

Steps to Convert R&P A/c → I&E A/c

1. Study R&P Account thoroughly
↓
2. Exclude opening/closing cash-bank balances
↓
3. Exclude capital receipts & payments
↓
4. Adjust revenue items for current year only (remove arrears/advances, add outstanding)
↓
5. Add non-cash items: depreciation, provisions
↓
6. Balance = Surplus/Deficit
🧠 Mnemonic — "SECAB": Study R&P → Exclude balances → Capital items excluded → Adjust for current year → Balance (Surplus/Deficit)

🧾 1.5 Balance Sheet 🟡

Prepared like a normal Balance Sheet, BUT:

  • Liabilities side has Capital Fund / General Fund instead of Capital
  • Surplus/Deficit from I&E A/c is added/deducted from Capital Fund
  • Capitalised items (legacies, entrance fees, life membership fees) directly added to Capital Fund
  • Special/specific purpose funds (building fund, sports fund) shown separately on liabilities side
Capital Fund (Opening) + Surplus (or − Deficit) + Legacies + Entrance Fees + Life Membership Fees = Capital Fund (Closing)

⭐ 1.6 Peculiar Items 🔴 HIGHEST WEIGHTAGE

These items get special treatment — this is the single most-tested part of Chapter 1.

Main recurring income. R&P A/c shows cash received; I&E A/c shows amount related to current year only (accrual basis).

Subscription received (R&P) + Outstanding at end (current yr) + Advance received last yr (for this yr) − Outstanding at start (last yr, now received) − Advance received this yr (for next yr) = Income from Subscription (I&E A/c)
📝 Most repeated numerical type in board/AAO exams — practice the subscription account format too.
TypeTreatment
Specific DonationCapitalised → Liabilities side of Balance Sheet (any amount)
General DonationRevenue receipt → Income side of I&E A/c

Amount received as per a deceased person's will. Non-recurring → added directly to Capital/General Fund. (Small legacies may be treated as income.)

Lump sum instead of periodic subscription → Capital receipt → credited directly to Capital/General Fund.

Paid once at admission. Default treatment: Capital receipt → Capital Fund. Exception: for institutions like schools where it's regular/small — may be treated as revenue income. Follow question's specific instruction if given.

Full sale proceeds → Receipts side of R&P A/c. Gain/Loss on sale → I&E A/c. Book value of asset deducted from asset in Balance Sheet.

Recurring in nature → shown as income in I&E Account.

Payment to a non-employee (e.g., guest artist) → Expenditure side of I&E Account.

Fund from a bequest/gift; income devoted to a specific purpose → Capital receipt → shown as a specific purpose fund on Liabilities side.

🎯 Special / Specific Purpose Funds 🟠

E.g., Prize Fund, Match Fund, Sports Fund. Created for a defined purpose.

Is there a Special Fund (e.g., Prize Fund)?
YES → Income from fund investments ADDED to fund (not I&E). Expenses related to fund DEDUCTED from fund. Shown in Balance Sheet.
Balance goes negative? → Debit balance transferred to I&E Account (expenditure side).
Fund (Opening) + Related Income + Donations for Fund − Related Expenses = Net Fund Balance (Balance Sheet)
📝 Probable Q: "Show how Prize Fund, Prize Fund Investments and related income/expenses will appear in the financial statements."

🏓 1.8 Incidental Trading Activity 🟡

Definition: Some NPOs conduct a small trading activity alongside their main service (e.g., a club running a canteen, or billiard table). Result of such activity is worked out separately in a mini Trading Account and only the net profit/loss is shown in I&E Account (not gross figures).

1.7 I&E Account from Trial Balance: Where a Trial Balance (not R&P A/c) is given, prepare I&E Account and Balance Sheet directly using standard adjustments (outstanding, prepaid, depreciation) — same principles as normal final accounts preparation.

✅ 3 Final Statements: R&P A/c → I&E A/c → Balance Sheet
✅ R&P A/c = Cash Book summary; ALL periods; no depreciation
✅ I&E A/c = P&L equivalent; current year only; accrual basis
✅ Capital Fund replaces "Capital A/c"
✅ Specific Donation = Capitalised; General Donation = Income
✅ Legacy, Life Membership Fee, Entrance Fee (default) = Capital Fund
✅ Sale of asset: proceeds → R&P; gain/loss → I&E
✅ Honorarium = expenditure (paid to non-employee)
✅ Special funds: income added, expenses deducted from the fund itself, NOT I&E
✅ Stationery consumed = Purchases + Op. Stock − Cl. Stock

⏱️ Last-Minute Memory Tricks

📌 "Capital items → Capital Fund": Legacy, Life Membership, Entrance Fee (default), Specific Donation, Endowment Fund, Building Grant — all go to Capital Fund/Liabilities, NOT to I&E Account income.
📌 "Recurring = Revenue": Subscription, General Donation, Sale of periodicals/sports material, Maintenance Grant, Cash Subsidy — all go to I&E Account.
