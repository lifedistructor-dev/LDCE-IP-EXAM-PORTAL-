Definition परिभाषा

USOF — a fund established to ensure universal, affordable telecom access in rural/remote India, financed by a levy on telecom operators' revenue.

यूएसओएफ — ग्रामीण/दूरस्थ भारत में सार्वभौमिक, किफायती टेलीकॉम पहुंच सुनिश्चित करने हेतु स्थापित निधि, जो ऑपरेटरों के राजस्व पर लेवी से वित्तपोषित होती है।

Important Dates महत्वपूर्ण तिथियां
EventDate
USOF established2002 🔴
Legal basisIndian Telegraph (Amendment) Act, 2003 🔴
Important Authorities महत्वपूर्ण प्राधिकरण
  • Administered by Department of Telecommunications (DoT)
  • Under the Ministry of Communications
Funding Mechanism वित्तपोषण तंत्र
Financed via Universal Service Levy (USL)
↓
USL = 5% of Adjusted Gross Revenue (AGR) of telecom licensees 🔴

🎯 Key Objectives of USOF Schemes मुख्य उद्देश्य

▼
1. Promoting Rural Telecom Access — equitable access in underserved/rural areas 🟠
2. Enhancing Digital Connectivity — BharatNet, public Wi-Fi, satellite tech
3. Security & Connectivity in Remote Areas — LWE-affected & border regions
4. Bridging the Digital Divide — urban-rural gap
5. Supporting Women & Social Development — empowering rural women & marginalized communities
🧠 Mnemonic: "RED-BS" → Rural access, Enhancing connectivity, Digital divide, Border/security, Support women — 5 objectives in order.

💰 Funding & Financials वित्तपोषण एवं वित्तीय

▼
Important Rules 🔴
  • Fund source: USL = 5% of AGR from all telecom licensees
  • Budget: Over ₹55,000 crore allocated since inception 🔴
  • Disbursement: to telecom operators for rural/remote infrastructure deployment & maintenance
Functions of USOF Administration प्रशासन के कार्य
  • Development of schemes across streams
  • Implementation agreements with ISPs/TSPs & organizations
  • Overseeing execution of schemes; disbursing subsidies
  • Post-implementation reviews
  • Budgeting & auditing of USOF activities
  • Engaging with international organizations

🌊 Streams of USOF Activities यूएसओएफ गतिविधि की धाराएं

▼

यह छह "धाराएं" (Streams) समय-समय पर अपडेट होती हैं — याद रखने के लिए क्रम महत्वपूर्ण है।

Stream I: Public Access Service (VPTs & FRCPs)
Stream II: Household Telephones in Rural Areas (RDELs)
Stream III: Mobile Infrastructure Phase I (Rural/Remote)
Stream IV: Broadband Connectivity — phased (Wire Line + Rural Public Service Terminals)
Stream V: General Infrastructure — Optical Fiber Cable for Assam
Stream VI: New Technology Induction — e.g. Solar Mobile Charging pilot
🧠 Mnemonic: "PHM-B GN" → Public access, Household phone, Mobile infra, Broadband, General infra, New tech (Streams I–VI in order) 🔴
Probable Q: Which Stream covers Mobile Infrastructure Phase I? → Stream III

📑 Financial Conditions in USOF Agreements वित्तीय शर्तें

▼
Purpose 🟡
  • Ensure financial discipline
  • Regulate disbursements for telecom infra projects
  • Promote transparency & accountability
  • Ensure timely completion with clear financial guidelines
1. Funding & Contributions 🔴

Telecom license fee contribution = ~5% of AGR towards USOF. Fund usage: enhancing rural infra in commercially non-viable areas.

2. Subsidy Disbursement Conditions 🟠
Milestone-based payments — tied to predefined infra milestones
↓
Phased structure: Initial (project start) → Intermediate (mid-milestones) → Final (after verified completion)
3. Eligibility & Selection Criteria
  • Competitive Bidding: lowest bidder for an area gets the subsidy
  • Compliance: non-compliance → reduction/cancellation of subsidy
4. Performance-Based Incentives
  • QoS standards linked to higher subsidies/bonuses
  • Early completion → bonus; delays → penalty
5. Penalty Clauses 🔴
  • Financial penalties for delays — deducted from future subsidy or reclaimed
  • Revocation of Contracts — severe non-compliance → govt revokes contract & reclaims disbursed subsidy
Probable Q: On what basis is a service provider selected for USOF subsidy? → Competitive bidding (lowest bidder) 🔴

🌐 BharatNet (formerly NOFN) भारतनेट (पूर्व एनओएफएन)

▼
Introduction 🔴
  • Aims to connect all 2,50,000 Gram Panchayats with broadband
  • Approved by Union Cabinet: 25 Oct 2011
  • Implemented by SPV Bharat Broadband Network Limited (BBNL), incorporated 25 Feb 2012 under Companies Act 1956
  • Non-discriminatory access to all telecom service providers (mobile, ISPs, cable TV, content)
  • Supports e-health, e-education, e-governance
Three Phases तीन चरण 🟠
Phase I
1,00,000 GPs — by BSNL, RailTel, PGCIL (CPSUs). Target: March 2017
Phase II
Remaining GPs — underground/aerial OFC + radio + satellite, via CPSUs & state Discoms. Target: Dec 2018
Phase III
Futuristic ring-topology network linking districts/blocks/GPs. Target: 2023
🧠 Mnemonic: "1-1-1" → Phase I: 1 lakh GPs by 2017; Phase II: remaining GPs by 2018; Phase III: ring network by 2023.
Financial Agreement (USOF ↔ BBNL) 🔴
  • USOF covers full Capex + Net Opex (excluding revenue) for 5 years from 25 Feb 2012
  • Approval of total Capex+Opex needed from Telecom Commission
  • Total funding capped at ₹20,100 crore 🔴 (revisable on Net Cost approval)
Net Cost = Capex + (Opex − Revenue)
Probable Q: Total USOF funding cap for BBNL under the agreement? → ₹20,100 crore 🔴
Fund Release depends on: निधि जारी निर्भर करती है
  • (a) Requirement of Fund
  • (b) Physical Progress of project
  • (c) Approval of Telecom Commission / DoT

BBNL submits monthly physical & financial progress reports to CCAs.

Schedule of Disbursement 🔴
Fund ReleaseCondition
Initial 10%Mobilisation advance on requisition
25% of total fundingBased on demand + utilisation certificate
Beyond 75% of approved amountOnly after 55% physical progress achieved
Every +5% fund releaseFor every +5% physical progress, up to max 95%
Balance 5%Released 1 year after commissioning, on 100% completion certificate
🧠 Mnemonic: "10-25-55-95-5" → 10% mobilisation, 25% on UC, 55% progress unlocks >75% release, 95% cap via 5% steps, final 5% after 1 yr.
Material Procurement Funds

Up to 95% of material cost released on request; final 5% after utilization/commissioning by BBNL.

Mode of Payment & No-Interest Rule 🔴
  • Payment via cheque/electronic transfer through Cash Section, DoT HQ, Sanchar Bhawan
  • No interest paid by USOF Administrator for short/late payments to BBNL
Annual Audit 🟠
  • Auditors appointed under Section 225 / Section 619, Companies Act 1956
  • Audit report to USOF Administrator: within 7 calendar days of signing, but not later than 30 September of following year 🔴
Probable Q: Deadline for BBNL's audit report submission? → Within 7 days of signing, latest by 30th September 🔴

🧮 Numerical Question — FBG Calculation संख्यात्मक प्रश्न — FBG गणना

▼
Method / Formula 🔴
Step 1: Average LF = (Q1+Q2+Q3+Q4 License Fees) ÷ 4 Step 2: Double it → 2 × Average LF Step 3: Add 10% buffer → Value + (Value × 10%) Step 4: Round off to nearest higher thousand = Required FBG
Solved Example (FY 2019-20) 🟠
QuarterAGR (₹)License Fee Payable (₹)
Q1 (Apr-Jun)15,71,8751,25,750
Q2 (Jul-Sep)11,94,50095,560
Q3 (Oct-Dec)12,12,30096,984
Q4 (Jan-Mar)30,29,2002,42,336
Average LF = ¼(125750+95560+96984+242336) = ₹1,41,657.50 Double = 2 × 1,41,657.50 = ₹2,83,315 +10% buffer = 2,83,315 + 28,331.50 = ₹3,11,646.50 Rounded up = ₹3,12,000 (Required FBG)

Existing FBG

₹1,16,000

Required FBG

₹3,12,000
🧠 Mnemonic: "Avg-Double-Ten-Round" (A-D-T-R) → Average LF → Double it → +10% → Round up to nearest thousand.
Probable Q: If average quarterly LF is ₹1,00,000, what is the FBG requirement (before rounding)? → 2×1,00,000 = 2,00,000 + 10% = ₹2,20,000 🔴
