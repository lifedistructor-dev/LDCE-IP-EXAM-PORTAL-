🏢 2.1 Nature of Partnership 🔴

Section 4, Indian Partnership Act 1932: Partnership is the "relation between persons who have agreed to share the profits of a business carried on by all or any of them acting for all."

Individuals = Partners; collectively = Firm; business name = Firm's Name. Firm has no separate legal entity from its partners.

6 Essential Features

  1. Two or more persons — min 2 partners. Max: 50 partners (Companies Act 2013, Sec 464 + Rule 10 of Companies (Misc.) Rules 2014)
  2. Agreement — oral or written (written preferred to avoid disputes)
  3. Business — mere co-ownership of property ≠ partnership
  4. Mutual Agency — each partner is both principal & agent for others
  5. Sharing of Profit (loss-sharing implied); charitable activity ≠ partnership
  6. Unlimited Liability — joint & several; private assets liable too
🧠 Mnemonic — "TABM-SL": Two persons, Agreement, Business, Mutual agency, Sharing of profit, Liability unlimited

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) 🟡

  • Registered under LLP Act, 2008; separate legal entity, perpetual succession
  • Min 2 partners; min 2 designated partners, at least 1 resident in India
  • Indian Partnership Act 1932 does NOT apply to LLP
  • Central Govt can investigate LLP affairs via a Competent Inspector
📝 Probable Q: "State the maximum number of partners a firm can have as per Companies Act, 2013." / "Distinguish between a Partnership Firm and an LLP."

📄 2.2 Partnership Deed 🔴

Definition: Written document containing the terms of the partnership agreement. Not legally compulsory (oral valid too), but preferred to avoid disputes. Should follow the Stamp Act and preferably be registered with the Registrar of Firms.

Typical Contents (🟠 list-type question)

  • Name/address of firm & partners; nature of business
  • Capital contribution by each partner; profit-sharing ratio
  • Interest on capital, loan, drawings; salary/commission to partners
  • Rules for admission, retirement, death; dissolution settlement
  • Dispute resolution method

2.2.1 Provisions Relevant for Accounting — WHEN DEED IS SILENT 🔴 EXAM FAVOURITE

MatterRule if Deed is Silent
Profit Sharing RatioEqual share (irrespective of capital contributed)
Interest on CapitalNo interest allowed; if allowed by deed, payable only out of profits
Interest on DrawingsNo interest charged
Interest on Partner's Loan/Advance6% p.a. — payable even if firm incurs a loss
Salary/Remuneration to PartnerNot allowed unless deed provides
🧠 Mnemonic — "PEDSR = 6% only for Loan": Profit-Equal, Drawings-No interest, Salary-No, Remuneration-No, but Loan/Advance = 6% always (the ONE exception that's payable even in losses)
📝 Probable Q (very high frequency): "What are the provisions of the Indian Partnership Act 1932 relevant to accounting, applicable when the partnership deed is silent?" — Always mention the 6% loan interest exception.

💼 2.4 Capital Accounts of Partners 🔴

Two methods to maintain partners' capital accounts:

Fixed or Fluctuating Capital Method?
FIXED: 2 accounts per partner (Capital A/c + Current A/c). Capital A/c stays fixed; all adjustments (interest, salary, drawings, share of P&L) go through Current A/c, which can be Dr or Cr.
FLUCTUATING: Only 1 account per partner. All adjustments recorded directly in Capital A/c, so balance fluctuates yearly. Default method if no instruction given.

Distinction Table

BasisFixed CapitalFluctuating Capital
No. of accounts2 (Capital + Current)1 (Capital only)
Adjustments recorded inCurrent A/cCapital A/c itself
BalanceRemains unchanged (unless addition/withdrawal of capital)Fluctuates every year
Balance signAlways CreditMay be Debit sometimes
🧠 Mnemonic — "FIXED = 2 accounts, always Credit; FLUCTUATING = 1 account, can go Negative": Remember "Fixed = Frozen number, Current = Changing mood"
📝 Probable Q (very high frequency): "Distinguish between Fixed and Fluctuating Capital Accounts." — Board favourite, 3-4 marks, always asked as a table.

📈 2.5 Distribution of Profit — P&L Appropriation A/c 🔴 CORE TOPIC

Definition: P&L Appropriation Account is an extension of the P&L Account. It shows how profit is appropriated/distributed among partners — via interest on capital, salary, commission, interest on drawings — before transferring balance to Capital A/cs in the profit-sharing ratio.

Format (Fig 2.3)

Dr. (Appropriations)Cr. (Additions)
P&L A/c (if loss)P&L A/c (if profit)
Interest on CapitalInterest on Drawings
Salary to Partner
Commission to Partner
Interest on Partner's Loan
Partners' Capital A/cs (share of profit)

Journal Entry Sequence 🟠

1. P&L A/c → P&L Appropriation A/c (transfer net profit/loss)
↓
2. Interest on Capital A/c → Partners' Capital/Current A/cs
↓
3. Partners' Capital/Current A/cs → Interest on Drawings A/c
↓
4. Salary/Commission to Partner A/c → Partners' Capital/Current A/cs
↓
5. Appropriation A/c → Partners' Capital A/cs (final share of profit)
📝 Probable Q: Numerical — "Prepare P&L Appropriation Account and Partners' Capital Accounts" — appears almost every year, 6-8 marks.

💹 2.5.2 Interest on Capital 🔴

  • Allowed only if expressly agreed in the deed; NOT a matter of right
  • Given when: (i) unequal capital but equal profit sharing, or (ii) equal capital but unequal profit sharing
  • Payable only out of profits

Calculation with Additions/Withdrawals

1. Interest on Opening Capital → for full year
↓
2. Interest on Additional Capital → from date introduced to year-end
↓
3. Interest on Capital Withdrawn → deduct, from date withdrawn to year-end
Interest on Capital = Capital × Rate/100 × Period(months)/12

💸 2.5.3 Interest on Drawings 🔴 VERY NUMERICAL-HEAVY

Charged only if deed provides. Discourages excessive drawings.

Fixed Amount Withdrawn Monthly (Avg Period Method)

Withdrawal TimingAverage Period
Beginning of every month6½ months
Middle of every month6 months
End of every month5½ months
Avg Period = (Total months ± 1) / 2 (+1 for beginning-of-month, −1 for end-of-month, no adjustment for middle)

Fixed Amount Withdrawn Quarterly

Withdrawal TimingAverage Period
Beginning of every quarter7½ months
End of every quarter4½ months

Varying Amounts at Different Dates → Product Method

For each withdrawal: Product = Amount × Months outstanding (till year-end) Total Products → Interest = ΣProducts × Rate/100 × 1/12
🧠 Mnemonic — "BEM 6½-6-5½, Quarter 7½-4½": Beginning-Middle-End of month = 6½-6-5½; Quarter Beginning-End = 7½-4½. Chant this before the exam!
📝 Probable Q: Numerical using average period / product method — appears almost every year. Practice all 5 scenarios (monthly-begin/mid/end, quarterly-begin/end, varying amounts).

🛡️ 2.6 Guarantee of Profit to a Partner 🟠

Definition: A new/existing partner is assured a minimum guaranteed profit share. If his actual share (as per PSR) is less than guaranteed amount, the shortfall (deficiency) is borne by the guaranteeing partner(s).
Who gave the guarantee?
ALL old partners gave it → Deficiency borne by them in their profit-sharing ratio (unless stated otherwise)
ONE partner alone gave it → That partner bears the entire deficiency
Deficiency = Guaranteed Amount − Actual Share as per PSR Guaranteeing Partner's Final Share = His Original Share − His portion of Deficiency Guaranteed Partner's Final Share = Guaranteed Amount (always)
📝 Probable Q: "A and B are partners... admit C with a guarantee of minimum profit of Rs. X... Prepare P&L Appropriation Account." — Classic recurring numerical.

🔧 2.7 Past Adjustments 🟠

Definition: Errors/omissions (interest on capital/drawings/loan, salary, commission, outstanding expenses) discovered after final accounts are prepared & profit already distributed. Corrected WITHOUT altering old accounts.

Two Methods of Correction

MethodHow
(a) Profit & Loss Adjustment AccountPass entries through a separate adjustment account; net effect distributed to partners in old PSR
(b) Direct in Partners' Capital A/csPrepare a "Statement Showing Net Effect" comparing what SHOULD have been credited vs what WAS credited; adjust the difference directly between partners
Net Effect = (i) Amount that should've been credited/debited − (ii) Amount actually credited/debited (as extra share of profit) = (iii) Excess (Dr partner) / Short (Cr partner) → single adjusting entry
📝 Probable Q: "After final accounts were prepared, it was found that interest on capital was omitted. Give the adjusting journal entry." — Practice the "Statement of Net Effect" table format.

📑 2.8 Final Accounts of a Partnership Firm 🟡

Prepared the same way as a sole proprietorship's final accounts — only difference: after Trading & P&L A/c, net profit/loss is transferred to the P&L Appropriation Account (not directly to Capital A/c) to distribute among partners.

Trading A/c → Gross Profit/Loss
↓
P&L A/c → Net Profit/Loss
↓
P&L Appropriation A/c → Interest, Salary, Commission adjustments
↓
Balance → Partners' Capital A/cs (in PSR)
