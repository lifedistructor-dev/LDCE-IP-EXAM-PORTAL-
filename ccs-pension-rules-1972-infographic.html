Preliminary 🔴 VERY IMP

प्रारंभिक — Rules 1 to 4

Chapter → Sub-Chapter → Micro:
CCS (Pension) Rules 1972 → Preliminary → Short Title/Application/Definitions/Transfer

📖 Definition 🔴

English: The Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972 came into force on 1 June 1972. They apply to Govt. servants appointed on or before 31 December 2003 to civil services/posts of the Union on pensionable establishments (incl. civilian Defence Service Govt. servants appointed substantively), subject to 8 listed exclusions.

हिंदी: केंद्रीय सिविल सेवा (पेंशन) नियम, 1972 1 जून 1972 से लागू हुए। ये 31 दिसंबर 2003 तक नियुक्त सरकारी कर्मचारियों पर लागू होते हैं (नई पेंशन योजना NPS के कारण यह कट-ऑफ तिथि महत्वपूर्ण है), 8 अपवादों के अधीन।

Probable Q: Up to what date of appointment do the CCS (Pension) Rules, 1972 apply? Ans: Appointments on or before 31 December 2003 (after this, NPS applies).

📜 Rule 4 — Transfer From Non-Applicable Service 🟡
  • Servant permanently transferred to a CCS(Pension)-Rules post from one where these rules don't apply → automatically becomes subject to these rules.
  • 🔴 Option: Within 6 months of the transfer order (or 6 months from return from leave, if on leave) → may elect to remain under his earlier pension rules.
  • Option must be in writing, communicated to the transferring authority; once exercised, is final.
Probable Q: Within how many months must a transferred Government servant exercise the option to retain his old pension rules? Ans: 6 months.
🧠 Mnemonic: "1-6-1972 to 31-12-2003" → CCS Pension Rules commencement and cut-off (NPS applies after). "6-Month Option, Final Forever" → Rule 4 transfer option.

General Conditions 🔴 VERY IMP

सामान्य शर्तें — Rules 5, 7-12

📜 Rule 5 — Regulation of Claims 🔴
  • Claim to pension/family pension is regulated by rules in force at the time of retirement/discharge/resignation/death.
  • The day of retirement/discharge/resignation is treated as the last working day; date of death is also a working day.
  • 🟠 Exception: for premature/voluntary retirement under FR 56(j)-(m) or Rule 48/48-A, the retirement date is treated as a non-working day.
Probable Q: Is the date of voluntary retirement treated as a working day or non-working day? Ans: Non-working day (exception under Rule 5).
📜 Rule 7 — Limitation on Number of Pensions 🟠
  • A Govt. servant shall not earn two pensions in the same service/post at the same time or by the same continuous service.
  • Except as provided in Rule 19 (military service), a re-employed pensioner (after superannuation/retiring pension) is not entitled to a separate pension/gratuity for the re-employment period.
📜 Rule 8 & 9 — Pension Subject to Good Conduct / President's Right 🔴
  • Rule 8: Future good conduct is an implied condition. Appointing authority may withhold/withdraw pension (whole/part, permanent/specified period) if pensioner is convicted of a serious crime or found guilty of grave misconduct. Minimum pension after reduction: ₹375/month (historic figure in rule; revised over time).
  • 🔴 Show-cause notice: 15 days to respond (extendable by another 15 days by appointing authority) before action under Rule 8(3).
  • Rule 9: President reserves the right to withhold/withdraw pension/gratuity (full or part) or order recovery of pecuniary loss, if found guilty of grave misconduct/negligence in departmental/judicial proceedings — even for service including re-employment.
  • 🔴 UPSC consultation mandatory before final orders under Rule 9.
  • Appeal against an order under Rule 8(1) (if passed by authority other than President) lies to the President, in consultation with UPSC.
Probable Q: Whose consultation is mandatory before the President passes a final order under Rule 9? Ans: Union Public Service Commission (UPSC).
Probable Q: How many days does a pensioner get to respond to a show-cause notice under Rule 8? Ans: 15 days (extendable by another 15 days).
📜 Rules 10-12 — Post-Retirement Restrictions 🟡
  • Rule 10: Restrictions on commercial employment after retirement.
  • Rule 11: Restriction on practice in income tax and other cases after retirement.
  • Rule 12: Restrictions on employment after retirement under a Government outside India.

Qualifying Service 🔴 VERY IMP

अर्हकारी सेवा — Rules 13-32, 48A, 48B, 48C

📖 Definition 🔴

English: Qualifying Service means service rendered while on duty or otherwise, which is taken into account for pension/gratuity purposes. It commences from the date a Government servant takes charge of the post (Rule 13).

हिंदी: अर्हकारी सेवा का अर्थ है वह सेवा जो ड्यूटी पर या अन्यथा प्रदान की गई हो, जिसे पेंशन/उपदान के प्रयोजन हेतु गिना जाता है। यह उस तारीख से प्रारंभ होती है जब कर्मचारी पद ग्रहण करता है (नियम 13)।

📜 Rule 24 & 26 — Forfeiture of Service 🔴
  • Rule 24: Dismissal or removal from service → entails forfeiture of past service.
  • Rule 26: Resignation → also entails forfeiture of past service (with certain exceptions, e.g., resigning to join another post under Govt. of India through proper channel).
📜 Rules 27-28 — Interruption in Service 🟡
  • Rule 27: Interruption in service (other than by dismissal/removal/resignation) does not automatically forfeit past service, but the interruption period itself does not count as qualifying service unless condoned.
  • Rule 28: Competent authority may condone interruptions in service in certain circumstances (e.g., where interruption is due to reasons beyond the servant's control), treating the total service as continuous for pension.
📜 Rules 48, 48A, 48B, 48C — Retirement & Additions 🔴
  • Rule 48: Retirement on completion of 30 years' qualifying service.
  • Rule 48-A: Retirement on completion of 20 years' qualifying service (voluntary retirement).
  • Rule 48-B: Addition to qualifying service granted on voluntary retirement (weightage).
  • Rule 48-C: Addition to qualifying service for Pioneers in General Reserve Engineers Force.
Probable Q: After how many years of qualifying service can a Government servant seek voluntary retirement under Rule 48-A? Ans: 20 years.
📜 Rule 32 — Verification of Qualifying Service 🔴

Head of Office must verify and determine qualifying service after 18 years of service, and again 5 years before retirement — communicated to the Government servant in Form 24.

Probable Q: At what two points is qualifying service required to be verified under Rule 32? Ans: After 18 years of service, and 5 years before retirement.

Emoluments and Average Emoluments 🔴 VERY IMP

परिलब्धियाँ एवं औसत परिलब्धियाँ — Rules 33-34

📖 Definition — Rule 33 (Emoluments) 🔴

English: "Emoluments" means basic pay (as per FR 9(21)(a)(i)) which a Govt. servant was receiving immediately before retirement or on the date of death; also includes Non-Practising Allowance for medical officers and Stagnation Increment.

हिंदी: "परिलब्धियाँ" का अर्थ है मूल वेतन (FR 9(21)(a)(i) अनुसार) जो सेवानिवृत्ति से ठीक पहले या मृत्यु की तारीख पर प्राप्त हो रहा था; इसमें चिकित्सा अधिकारियों का गैर-व्यवसाय भत्ता एवं स्थिरता वेतन वृद्धि भी शामिल है।

Probable Q: Does "Emoluments" include Special Pay or Personal Pay? Ans: No — only basic pay, Non-Practising Allowance, and Stagnation Increment count.
📜 Rule 33 — Key Notes 🟠
  • If absent on leave (leave-salary payable) or reinstated after suspension without forfeiture → emoluments = what he would have drawn had he not been absent.
  • If on EOL or under suspension (not counting as service) → emoluments = last drawn before such leave/suspension.
  • Increment earned during EL (even if not actually drawn) counts as emoluments, if earned within the first 120 days of EL.
  • Pay drawn on deputation to Armed Forces of India → counted as emoluments; pay on foreign service → NOT counted (notional Govt. pay used instead).
📖 Definition — Rule 34 (Average Emoluments) 🔴

English: Average Emoluments are determined with reference to emoluments drawn during the last 10 months of service.

हिंदी: औसत परिलब्धियाँ सेवा के अंतिम 10 महीनों के दौरान प्राप्त परिलब्धियों के आधार पर निर्धारित की जाती हैं।

  • If absent on leave (with leave salary) or reinstated after suspension → notional emoluments considered.
  • If on EOL/suspension (not counting as service) during last 10 months → that period disregarded; an equal period before the 10 months is included instead.
  • Increment earned during EL in last 10 months (within first 120 days of that EL) → included even if not actually drawn.
Probable Q: Over what period is Average Emoluments calculated? Ans: The last 10 months of service (Rule 34).

Probable Q: What is the minimum percentage of compensation pension that must be granted to a compulsorily-retired (as penalty) Government servant? Ans: Not less than two-thirds (Rule 40).
📜 Rule 38 — Invalid Pension Details 🟠
  • Medical certificate needed from: Medical Board (Gazetted officer, or non-gazetted with pay > specified limit) or Civil Surgeon/District Medical Officer (other cases).
  • A lady doctor must be included in the Medical Board when examining a woman candidate.
  • Medical certificate form: Form 23.
  • If declared fit for less-laborious work → employed on lower post if willing; only if no such post available → admitted to invalid pension.
📜 Rule 39 — Compensation Pension Details 🟡
  • 🔴 3 months' notice required before discharge due to post abolition.
  • If notice < 3 months and no alternative employment given → pay & allowances for the shortfall period may be sanctioned.
  • No compensation pension for period covered by pay-in-lieu-of-notice.
  • If servant accepts new appointment instead of compensation pension, and later gets a pension of any class → that pension shall not be less than the compensation pension he could have claimed.
Probable Q: What is the minimum notice period before discharge due to abolition of post? Ans: 3 months (Rule 39).
📜 Rule 41 — Compassionate Allowance 🔴
  • Dismissed/removed servants normally forfeit pension and gratuity entirely.
  • 🔴 Exception: Competent authority (that dismissed/removed him) may, in deserving cases, sanction a Compassionate Allowance not exceeding two-thirds of the pension/gratuity that would have been admissible on compensation pension.
  • Compassionate Allowance can be commuted, like other classes of pension.
Probable Q: What is the maximum Compassionate Allowance that can be sanctioned to a dismissed employee? Ans: Not exceeding two-thirds of admissible pension/gratuity.

📜 Rule 49 — Amount of Pension / Service Gratuity 🔴
  • Qualifying service < 10 years → Service Gratuity = half month's emoluments per completed 6-monthly period.
  • Qualifying service ≥ 10 years → Pension = 50% of emoluments or average emoluments, whichever is more beneficial.
  • Fraction of qualifying service ≥ 3 months → rounded to next completed half-year.
  • Final pension amount → rounded to next higher rupee.
  • 🟢 Additional pension for very senior citizens (age-linked, on top of basic pension):
Age of PensionerAdditional Pension
80 to <85 years20% of basic pension
85 to <90 years30% of basic pension
90 to <95 years40% of basic pension
95 to <100 years50% of basic pension
100 years or more100% of basic pension
Probable Q: A pensioner's age is 87. What additional pension is he entitled to? Ans: 30% of basic pension.
📜 Rule 50 — Retirement/Death Gratuity 🔴

Retirement Gratuity (min. 5 years' qualifying service): ¼ of emoluments per completed 6-monthly period, max 16½ times emoluments.

Death Gratuity Table (if dies while in service):

Length of Qualifying ServiceRate of Death Gratuity
Less than 1 year2 × emoluments
1 year or more but < 5 years6 × emoluments
5 years or more but < 20 years12 × emoluments
20 years or more½ emoluments per 6-month period, max 33 × emoluments

🔴 Maximum ceiling (retirement or death gratuity): ₹10,00,000 (ten lakh rupees).

"Family" for gratuity (Rule 50(6)) includes: wife/wives, husband, sons (incl. step/adopted), unmarried & widowed daughters, father, mother, brothers <18 yrs, unmarried/widowed sisters, married daughters, children of pre-deceased son.

Probable Q: What is the death gratuity rate for a Government servant who dies with 8 years of qualifying service? Ans: 12 times emoluments (5 yrs to <20 yrs slab).
📜 Rule 54 — Family Pension 1964 🔴
  • Normal Family Pension = 30% of basic pay (uniform rate), subject to min/max as prescribed.
  • 🔴 Enhanced Family Pension (50% of last pay) — applicable if death occurred: (a) in service after 7+ years' continuous service, or (b) after retirement while in receipt of pension.
  • Enhanced rate payable for: 10 years (if died in service) OR 7 years / until age 67, whichever is earlier (if died after retirement) — then reverts to normal 30% rate.
  • Additional family pension for pensioners 80+ years — same age-slab % structure as Rule 49 pension.
  • Family pension period: widow/widower — until death/remarriage; unmarried son — till 25 yrs/marriage/earning; unmarried/widowed/divorced daughter — till marriage/remarriage/earning; disabled child — for life (with Medical Board certification); dependent parents — for life.
Probable Q: For how long is Enhanced Family Pension payable if the Government servant dies while in service? Ans: 10 years from the date following death.
Probable Q: What is the normal rate of Family Pension? Ans: 30% of last pay drawn (uniform rate).

Determination & Authorization of Pension and Gratuity 🔴 VERY IMP (Timeline-heavy)

पेंशन एवं उपदान की राशि का निर्धारण एवं प्राधिकरण — Rules 56-74

📜 Rule 56 — List of Retiring Government Servants 🟠
  • Head of Department prepares a list every 3 months (1 Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct) of servants due to retire in the next 12-15 months.
  • Copy to Accounts Officer by end of that quarter month (31 Jan/30 Apr/31 Jul/31 Oct).
📜 Rule 57 — No Demand Certificate 🟡

Head of Office writes to Directorate of Estates at least 1 year before anticipated retirement (for allottees of Govt. accommodation), for issue of "No Demand Certificate" covering the period up to 8 months before retirement.

📜 Rule 65 — Authorization by Accounts Officer 🔴

  • Other retirement cases: PPO issued within 3 months of receiving pension papers from Head of Office.

📜 Rule 68 — Interest on Delayed Gratuity Payment 🟠

  • Responsibility fixed and disciplinary action taken against the official(s) responsible for the delay.

Determination & Authorization of Family Pension & Death Gratuity 🔴 VERY IMP

सेवाकाल में मृत्यु पर पारिवारिक पेंशन एवं मृत्यु उपदान का निर्धारण — Rules 77-80D

📜 Rule 77 — Obtaining Claims 🔴
  • On intimation of death while in service, Head of Office ascertains death gratuity/family pension eligibility.
  • Addresses nominee(s) in Form 10/11 for gratuity claim (Form 12); eligible family member/guardian in Form 13 for family pension claim (Form 14).
  • Child who has attained 18 years may claim directly without guardian.
📜 Rule 78 — Completion of Form 18 🔴
  • Form 18 completed within 1 month of intimation of death.
  • Emoluments verified for max 1 year preceding death (or before EOL commencement if servant was on EOL at death).
  • Entire process (qualifying service + emoluments determination + family pension/gratuity calculation) completed within 1 month of intimation.
Probable Q: Within how many months must Form 18 be completed after intimation of death? Ans: 1 month.
📜 Rule 79 — Incomplete Service Records 🟠
  • If service >1 yr but <7 yrs → verify last year's service/emoluments; determine Family Pension under R.54(2)/(2A).
  • If service >7 yrs → verify last 7 years + last year's emoluments; determine per R.54(3) (enhanced rate).
  • If last 7 yrs unverifiable but last year verifiable → provisional normal-rate family pension first; verify remaining 7-yr spell within next 2 months, then re-determine enhanced rate.
  • Death gratuity: if 5-20 yrs verified → 12× emoluments; if >20 yrs but unverifiable → provisional 12× emoluments, final amount determined within 6 months of provisional authorization.
Probable Q: If the last 7 years' service cannot be immediately verified, within how long must it be verified thereafter? Ans: Within 2 months.
📜 Rules 80, 80A-80D — Forwarding, Provisional Payment & Deputation Deaths 🟡
  • Rule 80: Head of Office forwards Form 18 to Accounts Officer within 1 month of receipt of claim. Max 10% of gratuity or ₹1,000 (whichever is less) may be held over pending final dues determination.
  • Rule 80-A: Head of Office draws provisional family pension (up to max family pension) and 100% of gratuity as determined, pending final authorization.
  • Rule 80-C: Adjustment of Government dues against gratuity.
  • Rule 80-D: Payment of family pension/death gratuity when a Government servant dies while on deputation.

CCS (Commutation of Pension) Rules, 1981 🔴 VERY IMP

केंद्रीय सिविल सेवा (पेंशन विनिमय) नियम, 1981

Note: These are standalone rules (footnoted in CCS(Pension) Rules 1972 as the successor to Article 918-A, CSR); presented here based on standard exam-reference material.

📖 Definition 🔴

English: Commutation of pension means converting a portion of the monthly pension into a lump-sum, one-time payment. The pensioner surrenders that portion of monthly pension for a specified number of years, in exchange for an immediate lump sum calculated using a Commutation Table (age-based factor).

हिंदी: पेंशन का विनिमय (कम्यूटेशन) का अर्थ है मासिक पेंशन के एक भाग को एकमुश्त राशि में परिवर्तित करना। पेंशनभोगी एक निश्चित वर्षों के लिए मासिक पेंशन के उस भाग को त्याग देता है, बदले में आयु-आधारित तालिका के अनुसार गणना की गई एकमुश्त राशि प्राप्त करता है।

📜 Important Rules — Key Provisions 🔴

Probable Q: What is the maximum percentage of pension that can be commuted? Ans: 40%.
Probable Q: After how many years is the commuted portion of pension restored? Ans: 15 years.
⚠️ Important Exceptions 🟡
  • Government servants who retire under Rule 40 (compulsory retirement as penalty) or Rule 41 (compassionate allowance) — commutation still permissible, but administratively confirmed case-by-case.
  • Pension from Invalid Pension or Compensation Pension — commutation subject to medical certification of life expectancy in some cases.
  • If a pensioner dies before the commutation payment is disbursed — commutation application is treated as not having taken effect (lump sum not paid to estate).
🏛️ Important Authorities 🟠
  • Head of Office — receives and forwards commutation application.
  • Accounts Officer — authorizes the commuted value payment along with pension.
  • Competent Medical Authority — certifies fitness where medical examination is required.
⏱️ Important Time Limits 🔴
  • 1 year from retirement — window for commutation without medical examination.
  • 15 years — restoration period of commuted pension.
  • Commuted value normally becomes payable along with/soon after the first pension payment for applications made in time.

📄 One-Page Last-Day Revision Sheet

Very Imp Frequent Concept Quick

1️⃣ Preliminary

  • 🔴 In force: 1 June 1972; applies to appointments up to 31 Dec 2003
  • 🟠 8 exclusions (Railway, casual/daily, contingency-paid, CPF, AIS, foreign-local, contract, special-law)
  • 🟡 Transfer option: 6 months, final once exercised (Rule 4)

2️⃣ General Conditions

  • 🔴 No two pensions same time/service (Rule 7)
  • 🔴 Rule 8: Appointing Authority acts (min pension ₹375 floor, historic); Rule 9: President acts, UPSC mandatory
  • 🟠 15-day show-cause notice period

3️⃣ Qualifying Service

  • 🔴 Verification: after 18 years service + 5 years before retirement (Form 24)
  • 🔴 Retirement: 30 years (normal, R.48) / 20 years (voluntary, R.48-A)
  • 🟡 Dismissal/removal (R.24) & resignation (R.26) forfeit past service

4️⃣ Emoluments

  • 🔴 Emoluments (R.33) = basic pay just before retirement/death
  • 🔴 Average Emoluments (R.34) = average of last 10 months
  • 🟢 Pension based on whichever (33 or 34) is MORE beneficial

5️⃣ Classes of Pensions

  • 🔴 6 classes: Superannuation(35), Retiring(36), Absorption(37), Invalid(38), Compensation(39), Compulsory-retirement(40) + Compassionate Allowance(41)
  • 🟠 Compensation pension: 3 months' notice; Compulsory retirement pension: min 2/3rd
  • 🔴 Compassionate Allowance: max 2/3rd, for dismissed/removed in deserving cases

6️⃣ Amount of Pension

  • 🔴 <10 yrs service → Service Gratuity; ≥10 yrs → Pension = 50% of emoluments
  • 🔴 Retirement Gratuity: ¼ emoluments/6-month period, max 16.5×; Death Gratuity: 2×/6×/12×/33× slabs
  • 🔴 Max gratuity ceiling: ₹10 lakh
  • 🔴 Family Pension: 30% normal; 50% enhanced (10 yrs if died in service; 7yrs/till-67 if died post-retirement)
  • 🟢 Additional pension for 80+ years: 20%→100% by age slab

7️⃣ Determination & Authorization (Pension/Gratuity)

  • 🔴 Paperwork starts 1 year before retirement; 3-stage process
  • 🔴 Certificate to servant + Form 5 issued: 8 months before; Form 5 due back: 6 months before
  • 🔴 Form 7 Part I complete: 4 months before; PPO issued: 1 month before (superannuation) / 3 months (other retirement)

8️⃣ Family Pension/Death Gratuity (Death in Service)

  • 🔴 Form 18 completed within 1 month of death intimation
  • 🟠 Forwarded to Accounts Officer within 1 month of claim receipt
  • 🟡 Provisional family pension + 100% gratuity drawn immediately; final determination within 2-6 months if records incomplete

9️⃣ Commutation of Pension Rules 1981

  • 🔴 Max 40% of pension commutable
  • 🔴 No medical exam if applied within 1 year of retirement
  • 🔴 Restoration after 15 years
