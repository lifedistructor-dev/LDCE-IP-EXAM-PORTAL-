Appendix III — Period of Preservation of Records
परिशिष्ट 3 — अभिलेख संरक्षण अवधि

▼
Governing Rules & Destruction Procedure 🔴 VI▾

Old Record Branch entries in Form Sy-257. Weeding list prepared in Form Sy-256-A, requires Head of Office's written sanction before actual destruction. Half-yearly report on state of old records accompanies the September & March State of Work returns.

Records organised into 8 sections: I-General, II-Administration/Establishment/Records, III-GPF, IV(a)-PO Certificates, IV(b)-NSC, IV(c)-Defence Savings Certificates, V-Postal Orders/IRC, VI-SB/CTD, VII-Money Order, VIII-Govt Securities.

Likely Q: What is the preservation period for the Register of Lapsed Deposits? → Permanent.

Preservation — GPF, Certificates & Securities Sections 🟡 Concept▾

Section III (GPF): Core ledger/nomination records generally preserved Permanently or long-term given their legal/financial significance for retirees.

Section IV (PO/NSC/Defence Certificates): Issue Registers and core certificate registers → Permanent; transactional vouchers → shorter periods (3-5 years).

Section VIII (Govt Securities): Core registers → Permanent, given they evidence Government's continuing liability/asset position.

🧠 General exam pattern: Registers that establish continuing rights/liabilities (GPF ledgers, Lapsed Deposits, Securities registers, Certificate Issue Registers) = Permanent. Routine transactional/procedural records = 1-5 years.
