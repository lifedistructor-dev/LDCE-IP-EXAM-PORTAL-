🔴 e-Service Books verified thoroughly to the extent of 25% — including leave accounts & employees retiring within 5 years.

🧠 Mnemonic: "10 flows, 25 verifies" — data-flow check 10%, thorough e-Service Book verification 25%.
Probable Question: To what extent should e-Service Books be thoroughly verified during Circle Office audit? — Ans: 25%

Item 19-20 सेवा पुस्तिका जाँच

Maintained under Supplementary Rules 197 & 205 for every non-Gazetted Government servant. Checked to the extent of 25% (incl. those retiring in next 5 years).

Key Service Book verification points (Item 20)
  • First-page entries re-attested every 5 years
  • Date of Birth — no alteration without competent authority sanction; entered in words & figures
  • Thumb & finger impressions taken
  • No break in continuity of service entries
  • Annual verification-of-service certificate recorded
  • LTC encashment of Earned Leave — max. 60 days
Probable Question: How often must the first-page entries of a Service Book be re-attested? — Ans: Every 5 years

Item 27-30 यात्रा भत्ता बिल

TA Bills on Tour/Transfer checked per Supplementary Rules; if processed via PA30 (instead of ESS), amount payable must match sanction.

🔴 No TA bills processed if Sanction/Claim submitted after 60 days of journey/completion of journey.

Item 9 — SK-52 माल ढुलाई खाता

Compared with Railway Receipt/Steamer Bill of Lading; total charge initialled by Manager (Rule 387(f) read with Rule 361, Postal Manual Vol. II).

Lowest negotiated tender rates must be used for transporting goods/stationery.

Item 10-11 चालान सूचकांक व व्यय पर निगरानी

Watch over expenditure from 1st April up to inspection date, checked against annual allotment proportion (Rule 363).

Probable Question: By which date must the closing invoice number of the annual series be communicated to each office? — Ans: 31st March every year

Item 12-14 स्थानीय खरीद बही, चालान व डुप्लीकेट चाबी

Invoices — office copies filed per office in separate bundles; acknowledgements filed together; wanting acknowledgements followed up (Rule 372).

Duplicate Keys Register — safes' duplicate keys held in a locked safe under joint custody of Manager and Superintendent/Head Asst. of Circle Office (Rule 376).

General सामान्य प्रशासन

Under Rule 10 of the PO Insurance Fund Rules, administration of the Fund is vested in the Director General of Posts, who may issue subsidiary regulations not inconsistent with President's Rules.

Accounting procedure — Rules 546 to 558, Chapter XVII, Postal FHB Vol. I.

Valuation & morality investigation work centralised in the office of the Director, PLI, Kolkata (Controller of Insurance, Shimla, for triennial valuation).

Probable Question: In whom is the administration of the Post Office Insurance Fund vested? — Ans: The Director General of Posts

General प्रस्तावों की जाँच

Checked per Rule 10 of PLI Manual for Circle Offices to see: eligibility (Rules 2, 2-A, 2-B, 2-C), amount of insurance covered by Rule 3, class/condition of policy (Rule 13), medical officer's recommendation (Rule 9), sum already assured limit within Rule 3.

Register of Proposals Rejected — reasons noted vide Rule 11(b) of PLI Manual.

🔴 3% of the entries in the Register of Proposals reviewed to verify first premium payment appeared in the certified list (Form L.I. 50 / L.I. 51) or schedules.

Item 6 समनुदेशन व नामांकन

Rules in Chapter III, PLI Manual of Circle Offices; Register of Assignments (Appendix A) & Nominations (Appendix B) maintained in PLI Section.

When is an assignment legally operative?
  • ✅ Only when due notice of assignment has been delivered to the Postmaster General.
  • Priority of claims under a policy is governed by the date the notice of assignment was received (Rule 21(iv)).

All entries/changes attested by the AAO as required by Rule 31(7).

Probable Question: An assignment of a PLI policy becomes legally operative only when — Ans: Due notice has been delivered to the Postmaster General

Item 8 प्रीमियम लेजर कार्ड, व्यपगति व पुनरुद्धार

🔴 2% of Ledger Cards test-checked for: correct/timely recovery rate, annual notification of non-credits/double-credits/short-credits, lapses/forfeiture under Rules 39 & 40 of PO Insurance Fund Rules promptly notified & revival action under Rule 39 or 41.

⚠️ Important Penalty — Short Credit Interest

At the end of the contract period, entire short credits are charged with 8% interest thereon.

🔴 Notice of discontinuance of premium must be issued at least 6 months before the month up to which premium is payable, to avoid unnecessary refund.

🧠 Mnemonic: "2% cards, 8% penalty, 6-month notice" — Ledger cards 2% checked; short-credit interest 8%; discontinuance notice given 6 months ahead.
Probable Question: What interest rate is charged on short credits at the end of the contract period? — Ans: 8%
Probable Question: How many months before premium cessation must a discontinuance notice be issued? — Ans: At least 6 months

Loan Registers & Valuation ऋण रजिस्टर व मूल्यांकन

Sanctions for Loan, Loan Ledger Cards review, Register of Safe Custody, Vouchers for Loan Payments — checked with reference to Rule 42 of PO Insurance Fund Rules for exceptional circumstances.

Valuation calculation on surrender & maturity examined w.r.t. Old/New Rule 40 — automatic paid-up provisions.

Item 31 — Bonus & Claims बोनस व दावा निपटान

Bonus additions correctly accounted for; claims from outside India paid through a Post Office in India.

On death — premium recovered for the month of death (unless death on 1st of the month).

Untraced Credits & Special Register अप्रमाणित जमा

Under Rule 67(b), PLI Manual — if a claim is settled on the Drawing/Disbursing Officer's certificate but credit is not yet traced in Circle Office accounts, the untraced credit is entered in the Special Register.

Nominee payments — instructions under Rule 9(4)(h), PO Insurance Fund Rules, must be observed.

Historical note: Pakistan-paid premia up to 31.3.1948, confirmed by Director PLI Karachi, recorded per Rule 67(i).

Separate folio per work/contractor; properly indexed; closed & balanced monthly (Para 10.7.6 CPWA Code; Rule 7 Sec. II CPWD Manual).

Item 4.1-4.23 चालू/अंतिम बिल जाँच

🔴 A.E. test-checks measurements to the extent of 50%; E.E. checks the required percentage (Rules 30-32, Sec. 7 CPWD Manual; Rule 10.2.16 CPWA Code).

Extra/substituted items — need sanction of competent authority; unsanctioned items get only part-rates.

🔴 Income Tax at 2% of cost of work deducted from every RA/Final Bill (Item 4.19).

Contractor must return at least 90% of total empty plastic bags to authorised agents; shortfall recovered from final bill (Item 4.16).

Secured advance (Form 26(a)) fully adjusted from RA/Final Bill.

🧠 Mnemonic: "50 checks, 2 taxes, 90 returns" — AE checks 50% of measurements, 2% Income Tax deducted, 90% empty bags to be returned.
Probable Question: To what extent must the Assistant Engineer (AE) test-check measurements of a running bill? — Ans: 50%
Probable Question: What percentage of Income Tax is deducted from every RA/Final Bill? — Ans: 2%

Item 5-6 अग्रिम भुगतान व सुरक्षित अग्रिम

Advance payments require previous sanction of SE, Postal Civil Circle; made only in exceptional cases of real necessity; monthly statement submitted to SE (Section 33, CPWD Manual; Rules 10.2.25 & 10.2.18, CPWA Code).

Secured advances — against security of materials brought to site; must be adjusted in full from RA/Final Bill.

बयाना राशि व सुरक्षा जमा

Earnest Money obtained from each tenderer; refunded to unsuccessful tenderers. Security Deposit noted in Security Deposit Register; refunded on proper sanction after satisfactory completion of work (Section 21, CPWD Manual Vol. II).

Arbitration/Court cases — action per Section 36, CPWD Manual Vol. II; reviewed periodically by Divisional Officer; register maintained up to date.

Casual mazdoor wages per approved schedule of labour rates; unpaid items entered in Register of Unpaid Wages, Form 21A at Sub-Division.

  • Beyond 3 months → SE sanction required
  • Beyond 6 months → CE sanction required
Probable Question: Beyond how many months of employing casual labour is CE's sanction required? — Ans: 6 months (SE's sanction needed beyond 3 months)

वार्षिक अनुरक्षण अनुमान

Expenditure must be within the prescribed percentage of the capital cost of respective buildings (Para 52-64, Sec. 4 CPWD Manual Vol. II).

Work-Charged Staff provided per prescribed yardstick (Appendix 8, CPWD Manual Vol. II).

Item 18-19 वित्तीय शक्तियाँ व अनुमान जाँच

Financial powers of CE/SE/EE for expenditure/awarding works/tender acceptance/single tender negotiations — examined for correct exercise.

Administrative approval + Expenditure sanction + Technical Sanction of Competent Authority must all exist before starting any work.

Savings under one section/project cannot be diverted to another work without sanction of Competent Authority (Para 91, 'D' Code).
