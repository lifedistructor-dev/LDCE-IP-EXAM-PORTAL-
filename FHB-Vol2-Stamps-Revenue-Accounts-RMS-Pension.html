Discrepancy found on receipt of coupons?
  • ✅ Reported at once to GM(PAF) → Postmaster not liable.

Spoiled coupons sent for destruction to GM(PAF) Kolkata — value written off or replaced with fresh coupons.

Rule 30 कोषाध्यक्ष के कर्तव्य

One Postal Assistant in every HO is selected as Treasurer. Custody of cash/stamps/BPO/IPO is joint with Postmaster (each holds a safe key).

  • (i) Supplies funds to subordinate offices, postmen, village postmen.
  • (ii) Takes over account bags from Delivery PA / Branch office bags from mail.
  • (iii) Receives collections (postage, registration fee, customs duty) from postmen/PAs.
  • (c) Converts deficient-postage cash collections into postage stamps, defaces with date-stamp.
Custody responsibility — during vs after working hours?
  • During working hours: Treasurer solely responsible for safe custody of current-transaction safe.
  • Reserve safe: Jointly responsible with Postmaster.
  • After close of office: Jointly responsible with Postmaster that balances tally before locking.
Probable Question: During working hours, who is solely responsible for the safe custody of the current transactions safe? — Ans: The Treasurer

Rule 31 कोषाध्यक्ष नकद बही

Records all sums (including stamps) actually received/paid by Treasurer, entered by him personally as transactions occur. Entries serially numbered under Receipts & Payments.

Note 1: Presidency/large HOs may maintain subsidiary registers for Treasurer's assistants, with Head of Circle/Region's concurrence.

Note 3: Only actual cash transactions entered — book-transfer entries excluded.

Rule 84-86 परिचय व धारा 11 का संरक्षण

Governs payment of service pensions in India, subject to Pensions Act 1871, CSRs, or Presidential orders.

Important Section — Sec 11, Pensions Act 1871

Pension granted on political grounds, past service, infirmity, or as compassionate allowance — cannot be seized, attached, or sequestered by any Civil Court in India at a creditor's instance.

R.85: "Service pension" = pension for past employment under Indian P&T Dept (sanctioned on/after 1 April 1921); includes gratuity.

R.86: Family Pension Fund sums & Workmen's Compensation Act sums are NOT covered by this Chapter.

Probable Question: Under which Section can a pension NOT be attached by a Civil Court decree? — Ans: Section 11, Pensions Act XXIII of 1871

Rule 87-89 भुगतान स्थान व पेंशन भुगतान आदेश

Service pensions payable at any authorised Head/Sub/Branch PO in India; first payment must be at the HO/SO in whose jurisdiction the pensioner elected to receive payment.

Note 1: GDS BOs/SOs doing SB work can be authorised to pay pension only, NOT gratuity.

Note 3: Provisional Pension drawn by the Head of Office where the Govt. servant served immediately before retirement.

R.88: Payment only on Pension Payment Order (Form DG(PA)-99(j)/(k) or 99(L)/(M)) issued by Director of Accounts (Postal) to the paying HO. Accompanied by specimen signature/thumb impression + attested photograph.

R.89: Postmaster's half kept in separate files by pension class, marked prominently in red ink, in personal custody of Postmaster.

Probable Question: Can a GDS Branch Post Office doing SB work disburse a gratuity payment? — Ans: No — GDS BO/SO can pay pension only, not gratuity

Rule 99 देय तिथि

Pension fixed at monthly rates is payable monthly on or after the last working day of the month.

🔴 Exception: Pension for the month of March is paid on the first working day of April (Authority: GOI DoPPW Notification 43/4/92-P&PW(G) dated 27-11-1992).

Proviso: If pension rate varies due to disbursement of commuted value, pension for the broken part of month (at original rate) may be paid before month-end.

🧠 Mnemonic: "March waits for April" — pension of March alone is credited on 1st working day of April, all other months on the last working day.
Probable Question: On which date is pension for the month of March credited? — Ans: First working day of April

Rules 101-107 दावों का भुगतान — जीवन प्रमाण पत्र

R.101: Pensioner must ordinarily take payment in person after identification against the PPO (except provisional pension via Head of Office).

Who is exempted from personal appearance? (R.102-103)
  • Pensioner exempted by competent authority
  • Female pensioner not accustomed to appear in public (purdanashin)
  • Pensioner unable due to bodily illness/infirmity — via representative with life certificate

Rule 103 — Who can sign the Life Certificate? जीवन प्रमाण पत्र हस्ताक्षरकर्ता

  • A person exercising Magistrate powers under CrPC
  • Registrar/Sub-Registrar (Indian Registration Act)
  • Group A or B Government servant
  • Police officer not below Sub-Inspector in-charge of a station
  • Postmaster / Departmental Sub-Postmaster / Inspector of Post Offices
  • Class I Officer of RBI / SBI (or subsidiary) Grade II officer
  • A pensioned officer who, before retirement, exercised Magistrate powers
  • Justice of Peace
  • Block Development Officer, Munsif, Tehsildar, Naib Tehsildar
  • Head of Village/Gram Panchayat or Executive Committee
R.104 — NRI Pensioner

May draw pension in India through authorised agent (valid power of attorney) who must produce life certificate each time — unless an indemnity bond is executed, then once a year.

R.105-106 — Resident Pensioner via Agent

Exempted from personal appearance if agent executes bond to refund overpayments + produces life certificate at least once a year. Payment stops if certificate more than a year old.

Probable Question: How frequently must an authorised agent (with indemnity bond) produce a life certificate for an NRI pensioner? — Ans: At least once a year

Rule 107 अवयस्क / अक्षम पेंशनभोगी

Payments to guardian on production of a certificate from the Collector/Deputy Commissioner declaring him manager/guardian; same payment procedure as original pensioner.

Important Section — Sec 95(1), Indian Lunacy Act 1912

Governs payment of pension where the pensioner is certified by a Magistrate as a lunatic — sums may be paid to a manager appointed under the Act.

Rules 124-125 पेंशनभोगी की सामयिक पहचान

R.124: On first appearance on/after 1st April each year, Disbursing Officer takes thumb + all-finger impressions on the pension bill (unless specimen signature already on PPO); identified against disburser's half of PPO.

R.125: Disbursing Officer must, at least once a year, obtain proof of continued existence independent of the life certificate; require personal attendance of male pensioners not incapacitated.

🔴 Disbursing Officer is personally responsible for any wrongly-made payment; must consult Director/Dy. Director of Accounts (Postal) in doubtful cases.

Probable Question: From which date each year must a pensioner's fresh identification (thumb/finger impressions) be taken? — Ans: On first appearance on or after 1st April

Rule 126 अनुद्धृत पेंशन व बकाया

Pension undrawn for more than 1 year ceases to be payable by the Disbursing Officer.

Arrears sanction — who approves?
  • Normal resumption after >1 yr → payable by DO, but arrears need prior sanction of the pension-sanctioning authority (via Director/CCA) IF:
    • (i) it's the FIRST-time payment of arrears, OR
    • (ii) arrears exceed ₹50,000
  • Pension undrawn for 3 years → cannot be paid without authority of Director/Dy. Director of Accounts (Postal) or Circle Accountant (Telecom).
  • Delay due to error/neglect of a Govt. officer → Director/CCA may direct payment on own authority.

A Gratuity Payment Order remains in force for 1 year only; unpaid orders are void and returned to PAO/CCA.

🧠 Mnemonic: "1-50-3" → Undrawn beyond 1 year stops; arrears over ₹50,000 need sanction; undrawn beyond 3 years needs Director/CCA authority.
Probable Question: Beyond what amount of arrears does sanction of the pension-sanctioning authority become mandatory? — Ans: ₹50,000

Rule 127-128 पेंशनभोगी की मृत्यु व बकाया भुगतान

(a) Pension can be drawn for the day of death itself — the hour of death is immaterial.

(b) Arrears may be paid to heirs if they apply within 1 year of death; thereafter needs sanction of competent authority.

₹5,000 – ₹25,000Orders of Head of Department + Indemnity Bond (Form TR-14) with sureties
Above ₹25,000 / doubtful casesPayment on production of legal authority (succession certificate etc.)

Sureties: normally 2 sureties of known financial stability; if claim < ₹7,500, accepting authority may allow just 1 surety.

Rule 128 — Nomination Rules 1983 नामांकन नियम 1983

Employee submits nomination in Form A (triplicate) to Head of Office within 3 months before/after retirement.

Head of Office verifies & returns duplicate to pensioner within 30 days of receiving the nomination.

Modification of nomination submitted in Form B.

🧠 Mnemonic: "5-25 rule" → up to ₹5,000 self-sanction; ₹5,000–25,000 needs Indemnity Bond TR-14.
Probable Question: Within how many days of retirement must Form A nomination be submitted? — Ans: Within 3 months before or after the date of retirement
Probable Question: Within how many days must the Head of Office verify and return the duplicate nomination copy? — Ans: 30 days

Rule 121 — Commutation कम्यूटेशन राशि

Payment of commuted value only upon authority of GM(F)/Director of Accounts(Postal)/CCA, and only to the legally entitled person.

Rule 122-123 — Gratuities उपदान

Gratuity paid in a single sum, not in installments; only on authority from PAO/CCA communicating the sanction.

Compassionate gratuity — no separate authority needed; paid directly from PO specified in the sanction order.

⚠️ Important Penalty — Delayed Gratuity Interest
Delay PeriodInterest Rate (w.e.f. 28-7-1984)
Beyond 3 months up to 1 year7% per annum
Beyond 1 year10% per annum

Interest sanctioned by Administrative Ministry in consultation with MHA/DoPT; authorised via Presidential Sanction. Disciplinary action to be taken against the officer responsible for delay.

Where disciplinary/judicial proceedings are pending at retirement, no gratuity can be paid until conclusion of proceedings.

🧠 Mnemonic: "3 months free, then 7-then-10" → No interest for first 3 months' delay; 7% p.a. up to 1 year; 10% p.a. beyond 1 year.
Probable Question: What interest rate applies if gratuity payment is delayed beyond one year? — Ans: 10% per annum

  • Licence cancelled if bill unpaid by end of following fortnight
  • Customs Duty schedule (ACG-90) to PAO: 1st of month
  • Rule 21 (private contribution) deleted w.e.f. 01.04.2003

🔴 Ch. IV — Post Office Accounts

  • 5 Account Books: ACG.1 (HO Summary), ACG.3 (SO), ACG.3(A) (BO), ACG.45 (Unpaid Postage), ACG.4 (HO Cash Book)
  • Accounts Bundle: most schedules 1st; Unclassified Receipts/PLI 1st & 16th; Money Orders 2nd & 16th; Telephone 4th; Consolidated Treasury Receipt 15th
  • Presidency POs exception: BPO/IPO returns by 5th; MO abstracts 18th & 4th
  • Correction of errors: report by letter, never send unsolicited revised accounts

🟠 Ch. V — RMS Accounts

  • HRO personally responsible; Accountant assists (from sorters)
  • Cash Book: ACG 35 (no opening/closing balance — self-balancing)
  • Cash Abstract: ACG 33
  • Cash Account to PAO: ACG-36, closed 15th & last working day
  • Miscellaneous receipts register: ACG-40

🔴 Ch. VII — Pension Payments

  • Sec 11, Pensions Act 1871 — pension immune from Civil Court attachment
  • Due date: last working day of month; March → 1st working day of April
  • Life certificate by bonded agent: once a year
  • Undrawn >1 yr → stops; arrears >₹50,000 needs sanction; >3 yrs needs Director/CCA authority
  • Death: arrears claim within 1 yr; ₹5,000/₹25,000 slabs for sanction; Form A nomination within 3 months of retirement, verified in 30 days
  • Gratuity delay interest: 7% (3mo-1yr), 10% (>1yr)
  • Gratuity Payment Order valid: 1 year only

🧠 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics याद रखने की तरकीबें

📮 Stamps supply chain: "CSD gives, PO verifies in 3, triplicate arrives" — Circle Stamp Depot supplies → Post Office verifies within 3 days → schedule made in triplicate.
💰 5 Account Books (R.46): "Happy Sub Branch Uncles Count" → HO Summary, Sub-office Summary, Branch-office Summary, Unpaid Postage Abstract, Cash Book.
📅 Accounts Bundle dates: "1st is for most, 2nd for Money orders, 4th for phone, 15th for Treasury, 16th repeats the rest."
🚂 RMS submission: "Mid-month & Month-end" — RMS Cash Account goes to PAO twice: 15th and last working day.
🎂 Pension due date: "March waits for April" — every month's pension on last working day, except March which is paid 1st working day of April.
⏳ Undrawn pension ladder: "1 stops it, 3 needs boss" — 1 year undrawn stops payment; 3 years undrawn needs Director/CCA sanction.
💵 Death arrears slabs: "5 is free, 25 needs a bond, above is a court call" — up to ₹5,000 self-sanction; ₹5,000–25,000 Indemnity Bond TR-14; above needs legal authority.
📈 Gratuity interest: "3 months free, then 7-then-10" — no interest for 3 months; 7% p.a. till 1 year; 10% p.a. beyond.
