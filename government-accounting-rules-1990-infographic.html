Chapter 1 — Introductory

अध्याय 1 — परिचयात्मक

Rules 1 – 6
📖 Topic & Definition

EN: Framed by the President under Article 150 of the Constitution, on the advice of the C&AG, these rules prescribe the form of accounts of the Union and States. They came into force on 1st April, 1990.

HI: ये नियम संविधान के अनुच्छेद 150 के तहत राष्ट्रपति द्वारा CAG की सलाह पर बनाए गए हैं, जो संघ एवं राज्यों के लेखों का प्रारूप निर्धारित करते हैं। ये 1 अप्रैल 1990 से प्रभावी हुए।

Chapter → Sub-chapter → Micro → Nano: Introductory → Definitions / Constitutional basis / Annual Accounts / Special Depts. → Rule-wise provision → Specific term/authority.

Chapter 2 — General Outlines of the System of Accounts

अध्याय 2 — लेखा प्रणाली की सामान्य रूपरेखा

Rules 7 – 19
📖 Topic & Definition

EN: Describes how the Central and State Governments conduct banking business through the Reserve Bank of India, how transactions of Railways/Posts/Telecom/Defence are distinguished and settled, and the "feeder-network" by which initial accounts flow up to Consolidated/Annual Accounts.

HI: यह अध्याय बताता है कि केंद्र व राज्य सरकारें RBI के माध्यम से बैंकिंग व्यवसाय कैसे करती हैं, रेलवे/डाक/दूरसंचार/रक्षा के लेन-देन कैसे अलग व निपटाए जाते हैं, तथा प्रारंभिक लेखों से समेकित/वार्षिक लेखों तक की "फीडर-नेटवर्क" प्रक्रिया क्या है।

Chapter 3 — Basic Structure of the Form of Accounts

अध्याय 3 — लेखा प्रारूप की मूल संरचना

Rules 20 – 38
📖 Topic & Definition

EN: The heart of the rules — defines the Financial Year, the Cash Basis of accounting, the 3-Part structure (Consolidated Fund / Contingency Fund / Public Account), the Sector-Major-Minor-Sub-Detailed Head classification (5-tier), and the 4-digit Major Head coding scheme.

HI: इन नियमों का मूल — वित्तीय वर्ष, नकद आधार पर लेखांकन, 3-भाग संरचना (समेकित निधि/आकस्मिकता निधि/लोक लेखा), क्षेत्र-प्रमुख-लघु-उप-विस्तृत शीर्ष वर्गीकरण (5-स्तरीय), तथा 4-अंकीय प्रमुख शीर्ष कोडिंग प्रणाली को परिभाषित करता है।

🌳 Decision Tree — Capital vs Revenue Expenditure
Does it increase concrete assets of a material/permanent character?
✅ Yes → Capital expenditure classification possible
❌ No → Revenue expenditure
Is it a temporary asset or Grant-in-aid to a local body for an asset NOT belonging to Govt?
✅ Yes → NOT capital (unless specially authorised by President/CAG)
❌ No → Continue capital classification
Has classification been expressly authorised by general/special Govt orders?
✅ Yes → Classify as Capital
❌ No → Must remain Revenue

Chapter 4 — Recovery of Charges for Services Rendered/Articles Supplied

अध्याय 4 — सेवाओं/वस्तुओं के लिए प्रभार वसूली

Rules 39 – 51
📖 Topic & Definition

EN: Prescribes when a Government department may charge for services/supplies and how such charges are recorded. Departments are divided into Service Departments (core government functions) and Commercial Departments (work to a financial result, e.g., Posts, Railways).

HI: यह अध्याय बताता है कि सरकारी विभाग सेवाओं/आपूर्ति के लिए कब प्रभार ले सकता है। विभागों को सेवा विभाग (मूल सरकारी कार्य) एवं वाणिज्यिक विभाग (वित्तीय परिणाम हेतु कार्यरत, जैसे डाक, रेलवे) में बांटा गया है।

📜 Important Rules 🔴
RuleProvision (EN)प्रावधान (HI)Tier

40 Note 2Above ₹1,000 — settlement via cheque/bank draft between Governments₹1,000 से अधिक — चेक/बैंक ड्राफ्ट से निपटान🟠

EN: Exceptions where a Service Department DOES charge another: (a) Forest Dept charges for extracted products; (b) payment for convict labour (except Jail Works under PWD); (c) cost of Police Guards for Irrigation projects debited to the project; (d) Films Division charges for film production; (e) CISF charges for force provided; (f)-(g) CPWD/CWC/CEA recover establishment + tools & plant charges on percentage basis for works executed for other departments.

🌳 Decision Tree — Should a Charge Be Levied?
Is the department a Commercial Department (works to financial result)?

❌ No → Next

❌ No → Next

✅ Yes → Charge applies (Rules 47-49)
❌ No → Case-specific Govt order needed

Chapter 5 — Rules Regarding Classification of Recoveries of Expenditure

अध्याय 5 — व्यय की वसूली के वर्गीकरण संबंधी नियम

Rules 52 – 56
📖 Topic & Definition

EN: "Recovery" (Rule 52) = repayment by another department/Government/non-Govt party of a charge that was initially booked as final expenditure. This chapter decides whether such a recovery should be shown as a fresh "Receipt" or as a "Reduction of Expenditure."

HI: "वसूली" (नियम 52) = किसी अन्य विभाग/सरकार/गैर-सरकारी पक्ष द्वारा उस व्यय की प्रतिपूर्ति जो प्रारंभ में अंतिम व्यय के रूप में दर्ज किया गया था। यह अध्याय तय करता है कि ऐसी वसूली को नई "प्राप्ति" दिखाया जाए या "व्यय में कमी।"

📜 Important Rules 🔴
RuleProvision (EN)प्रावधान (HI)Tier

🌳 Decision Tree — Receipt or Reduction of Expenditure?
Recovery from a non-Government party or another Government?
✅ Yes → Classify as RECEIPT (Rule 53)
❌ No (same Govt, another dept) → Next
Is the recovering department a Commercial Department (Posts/Railways/Telecom)?
✅ Yes, for its proper function → RECEIPT
❌ No → REDUCTION OF EXPENDITURE (Rule 54)
Is it a Works-in-Progress/Stock/Suspense/Capital Account recovery?
✅ Yes → Always REDUCTION of expenditure (Rules 55-56)
❌ No → Apply general Rule 53/54

Rules 57 – 62
📖 Topic & Definition

EN: Governs how different types of Government losses — relinquished claims, embezzled cash, lost assets, counterfeit currency, irregular payments — are recorded and classified in the accounts.

🧠 One-Page Revision Sheet / एक-पृष्ठ रिवीजन शीट

📘 Ch.1 Introductory

Article 150 basis • Effective 1.4.1990 • CAA = Secretary • CGA prescribes forms on President's behalf • Railways/Posts/Telecom/Defence special provision since 1982-83

🏦 Ch.2 System of Accounts

RBI = Central/State banker • Postal/Railway/Telecom/Defence accounts distinguished separately • RBI Central Accounts Section = Nagpur • J&K, Sikkim — no RBI banking agreement • Single Entry system (mainly)

🧱 Ch.3 Basic Structure

FY: 1 Apr–31 Mar • Cash basis of accounts • 3 Parts: Consolidated Fund/Contingency Fund/Public Account • 5-tier: Sector→Major→Minor→Sub→Detailed Head • Code: 0/1 Receipt, 2/3 Rev.Exp, 4/5 Capital, 6/7 Loans • Write-off ≤₹1,000, >5 yrs

💱 Ch.4 Recovery of Charges

Inter-Govt claims ≤₹1,000 → no settlement • Service Dept ≠ charge (usually) • Commercial Dept = charges (Posts/Railways) • Key exceptions: Forest, convict labour, CISF, CPWD %

📊 Ch.5 Classification of Recoveries

Non-Govt/other Govt recovery = Receipt • Same-Govt non-commercial dept recovery = Reduction of expenditure • Commercial dept (Posts) proper-function recovery = Receipt • Works-in-progress/Stock/Capital recovery = always reduction

⚠️ Ch.6 Classification of Losses

Relinquished claim ≠ recorded as loss • Embezzled money = Receipt first, then Loss (58-2) • Counterfeit currency = cash loss • Cash losses = separate head • Inevitable losses = descriptive head

📋 Ch.7 Misc Rules

Inter-Govt charge incidence = mutual agreement, Centre's concurrence needed for changes • Treasury/Departmental account forms = President on CAG advice

📜 Ch.8 General Orders

Transit pay = borne by department "proceeding to" • TA = same head as pay (usually) • Civil Advances = 8550 • Suspense = 8658, credit follows realisation • Old Accounts Code Vol.I repealed (Rule 75)

🎯 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics / स्मृति युक्तियां
1. Three Parts — "CCP"
Consolidated Fund → Contingency Fund → Public Account. "सी-सी-पी" क्रम याद रखें।
2. Five Tiers — "SMSSD"
Sector → Major Head → Sub-Major (optional) → Minor Head → Sub-head → Detailed Head.
"क्षेत्र-प्रमुख-लघु-उप-विस्तृत" क्रम में याद रखें।
3. Major Head Code — "+2 Ladder"
0/1 (Receipt) +2 → 2/3 (Rev. Exp.) +2 → 4/5 (Capital) +2 → 6/7 (Loans). Every +2 moves one stage forward: प्राप्ति → व्यय → पूंजी → ऋण।
4. Recovery Classification — "Same Govt Reduces, Other Govt Receipts"
Same Government, non-commercial dept → Reduction of expenditure. Other Government/non-Govt party → Receipt. Commercial dept (Posts/Railways) proper function → Receipt even within same Govt.
5. Embezzled Money — "Receipt First, Loss Second"
Money that reached a Govt servant then lost = ledger shows it TWICE: once as Receipt (into Consolidated Fund), once as Loss (expenditure side). "पहले प्राप्ति, फिर हानि।"
6. Transit Pay Rule — "Proceeding, Not Leaving"
Transit pay/allowances always borne by the department the servant is going TO, not the one he's leaving. Exception: Foreign Service — foreign employer pays.
7. ₹1,000 Double Duty
₹1,000 appears twice: (a) inter-Govt claim settlement threshold (Rule 40), and (b) Suspense/Deposit write-off ceiling (Rule 38, needs >5 years pendency too).
