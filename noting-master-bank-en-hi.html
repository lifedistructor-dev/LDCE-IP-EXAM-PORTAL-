CASE 1 OF 5
Sanction of 15 Days Paid Leave on Medical Grounds for a GDS
Scenario: GDS BPM requests 15 days paid leave with a medical certificate, forwarded by SPM without clear replacement arrangements. Initiate a note as IP for the SPOs.
GDS Rules 2020 — Rule 7 Leave: Max 20 days/year
🇬🇧 English Version Department of Posts
Sub-Division: Ahmedabad North Subject: Sanction of 15 days Paid Leave on Medical Grounds – Case of Shri A.B. Patel, GDS BPM. Shri A.B. Patel, GDS BPM has submitted an application dated 01.07.2026, requesting 15 days of Paid Leave on medical grounds, supported by a valid medical certificate issued by a registered Government Medical Officer.

2. The Sub Postmaster (SPM) has forwarded the application to this office. However, the SPM has failed to record a clear recommendation regarding the availability of a suitable substitute to manage the Branch Post Office (BO) during the leave period.

3. As per Rule 7 of GDS (Conduct and Engagement) Rules, 2020, a GDS is eligible for paid leave subject to a maximum of 20 days in a calendar year, provided a combination of work adjustment or an approved substitute is available to ensure uninterrupted postal services.

4. In view of the medical emergency, it is proposed that the leave may be provisionally approved. Meanwhile, the SPM may be directed to deploy the adjacent GDS MD for a combined duty arrangement to ensure smooth functioning of the BO.

Submitted for kind approval and further orders of the Superintendent (SPOs).

Proposing Rule 14 (Major Penalty) Action for Savings Bank Fraud
Scenario: A Postal Assistant is found responsible for a savings bank fraud of ₹2 Lakhs involving silent accounts. Submit a note to initiate major penalty proceedings.
CCS (CCA) Rules 1965 — Rule 14 CCS (Conduct) 1964 — Rule 3(1)(i)(ii) Major Penalty
🇬🇧 English Version Department of Posts

A serious savings bank fraud amounting to ₹2,00,000/- has been detected at Ahmedabad HO, wherein unauthorized withdrawals were made from silent accounts.

2. The preliminary investigation report submitted by the undersigned clearly establishes the prime involvement of Shri X.Y.Z., PA. The official has violated Rule 3(1)(i) and Rule 3(1)(ii) of CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964 by failing to maintain absolute integrity and devotion to duty.

3. Since the gravity of the offense involves financial embezzlement, it is a fit case for initiating major penalty proceedings. It is proposed to issue a formal Charge Memorandum under Rule 14 of CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965.

4. Draft Charge Sheet along with Schedule I (Articles of Charge), Schedule II (Imputations), Schedule III (Documents), and Schedule IV (Witnesses) is placed below for kind approval.

Remedial Action Plan for Public Complaints on Delayed Speed Post
Scenario: Serious public complaints on CPGRAMS regarding delayed delivery of Speed Post in a specific sector due to high parcel volumes. Propose structural fixes.
CPGRAMS Portal PMA Tracking Logs Beat Bifurcation
🇬🇧 English Version Department of Posts
Sub-Division: Ahmedabad North Subject: Public complaints regarding delayed delivery of Speed Post articles in 'Area X' – regarding. Frequent public complaints have been received via CPGRAMS portal regarding delayed delivery of Speed Post articles in 'Area X' under the jurisdiction of Sub-Office (SO).

2. On checking the tracking logs and PMA (Postman Mobile App) records, it is observed that the mail delivery in this specific sector is delayed due to a sudden 40% surge in e-commerce parcel volumes and shortage of regular delivery staff.

3. At present, only one postman is managing the entire locality, which is structurally insufficient. To resolve this public grievance immediately, the following lines of action are suggested:
(a) Bifurcation of the delivery beat into two sectors.
(b) Engagement of an outsourced delivery agent/MTS for a temporary period of 30 days.

Submitted for kind approval and orders of the Superintendent (SPOs).

CASE 4 OF 5
Enhancement of Daily Cash Holding Limit for a Branch Office
Scenario: BPM requests enhancement of daily cash limit from ₹25,000 to ₹50,000 due to heavy surge in IPPB withdrawals and Sukanya deposits.
Postal Manual Vol. VI Cash Limit: ₹25,000 → ₹50,000
🇬🇧 English Version Department of Posts
Sub-Division: Ahmedabad North Subject: Enhancement of daily cash holding limit of 'BO Y' from ₹25,000 to ₹50,000. The Branch Postmaster (BPM) of 'BO Y' has submitted a representation requesting an increase in the maximum daily cash retention limit from the existing ₹25,000/- to ₹50,000/-.

2. On reviewing the transaction statistics of the said BO for the last quarter, it is observed that daily deposits under Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) and doorstep cash withdrawals through IPPB (AePS) have risen significantly.

3. Due to the lower cash limit, the BPM is forced to remittance cash to the account office almost daily, which increases transit risk and halts local doorstep banking services. The BO possesses a verified steel safe embedded in the wall for secure cash storage.

4. In terms of Postal Manual Volume VI, the proposal for enhancement of the cash limit to ₹50,000/- is justified and strongly recommended.

Submitted to the Superintendent (SPOs) for kind approval.

CASE 5 OF 5
Action Against Staff Absent Unauthorizedly Beyond 21 Days
Scenario: An MTS official is absent from the Sub-Office for over 21 days without any leave application or intimation. Initiate a disciplinary note.
CCS (Conduct) 1964 — Rule 3(1)(iii) Minor Penalty — Rule 16 Warning Memo
🇬🇧 English Version Department of Posts
Sub-Division: Ahmedabad North Subject: Unauthorized absence from duty in respect of Shri K.L. Sharma, MTS, Sub-Office (SO). A report has been received from the Sub-Postmaster (SPM) stating that Shri K.L. Sharma, MTS has been unauthorizedly absent from duty with effect from 12.06.2026 without submitting any leave application or medical intimation.

2. The SPM has sent two registered reminders to the official's residential address on 18.06.2026 and 25.06.2026 directing him to rejoin duty immediately, but no reply has been received so far.

3. Continued unauthorized absence for more than 21 days amounts to a clear violation of Rule 3(1)(iii) of CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964, which dictates that every government servant must maintain devotion to duty.

4. To curb this indiscipline, it is proposed to issue an official warning memorandum. If the official fails to report within 7 days, disciplinary proceedings under Rule 16 (Minor Penalty) may be initiated.

Submitted for further directions of the Superintendent (SPOs).
