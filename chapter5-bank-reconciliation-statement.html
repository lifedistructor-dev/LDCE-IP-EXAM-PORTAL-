5.1

Need for Reconciliation

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Bank Reconciliation Statement (BRS): a statement prepared to reconcile (tally) the bank balance as per the Cash Book with the balance as per the Bank Passbook/Statement, by identifying and listing the causes of difference.

↓ caused by two broad reasons ↓

Causes of Difference
TIMING DIFFERENCES
  • Recorded by one party, not yet by the other
ERRORS
  • By the firm in the cash book
  • By the bank in the passbook
📝 Probable Exam QuestionWhy do the balances as per cash book and passbook differ even when there is no error? Explain with the concept of timing differences.

5.1.1 Timing Differences — 7 Causes

🔴 Guaranteed source of BRS numerical items
CauseEffect on Passbook balance vs Cash Book
(a) Cheques issued but not yet presented for paymentPassbook > Cash Book (add to cash book balance)
(b) Cheques paid into bank but not yet collectedPassbook < Cash Book (deduct from cash book balance)
(c) Direct debits by bank (charges, interest on OD)Passbook < Cash Book (deduct)
(d) Amounts directly deposited by a debtor into the bankPassbook > Cash Book (add)
(e) Interest/dividends directly collected by the bankPassbook > Cash Book (add)
(f) Direct payments by bank (standing instructions — rent, insurance, bills)Passbook < Cash Book (deduct)
(g) Cheques deposited / bills discounted, dishonouredPassbook < Cash Book (deduct)

5.2

Preparation of BRS

🔴 Very Important — the actual numerical technique
Q: What kind of balance is given — Favourable (Dr. in cash book) or Overdraft (Cr. in cash book)?
Favourable / Debit balance
Deposits > withdrawals — normal cash-in-bank position
Overdraft / Credit balance
Withdrawals > deposits — bank overdraft, unfavourable

Reconciliation can start from either the Cash Book balance OR the Passbook balance — treatment of every item simply reverses when you switch the starting point.

4 Possible Situations

  • Debit (favourable) balance as per Cash Book given → find Passbook balance
  • Credit (favourable) balance as per Passbook given → find Cash Book balance
  • Credit balance as per Cash Book (overdraft) given → find Passbook balance
  • Debit balance as per Passbook (overdraft) given → find Cash Book balance

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
"Issued but not presented → ADD"
Cheques WE issued but the payee hasn't cashed yet → bank hasn't deducted it → so Passbook balance is HIGHER → ADD to cash book balance to reach passbook figure.
"Deposited but not collected → LESS"
Cheques WE deposited but bank hasn't cleared yet → bank hasn't added it → so Passbook balance is LOWER → DEDUCT from cash book balance.
"Bank knows first → LESS; Firm knows first → ADD"
If the BANK recorded something the firm doesn't know yet (charges, direct debit, dishonour) → deduct. If the FIRM recorded something the bank hasn't caught up on yet (cheque issued, deposited) → add if issued, less if deposited.
Flip the sign when you flip the start
Every "+" becomes "−" and every "−" becomes "+" if you start the BRS from the Passbook balance instead of the Cash Book balance.

  • Timing differences · Errors (firm or bank)

Add Items

Less Items

  • Cheques deposited not collected · Bank charges · Standing instruction payments · Dishonoured cheques

Balance Types

  • Debit/favourable (cash book) = Credit/favourable (passbook)
  • Credit (cash book) = Overdraft/Debit (passbook)

4 Situations

  • Fav. CB→PB, Fav. PB→CB, OD CB→PB, OD PB→CB

Golden Rule

  • Flip +/− when you flip the starting balance

Passbook

  • Deposits in Credit column, Withdrawals in Debit column (bank's view)

Last-Day Revision Notes

🔴 Read this 30 minutes before the exam
  1. 01BRS reconciles Cash Book balance with Passbook balance — it is NOT an account, just a statement.
  2. 02Two broad causes: Timing Differences and Errors (by firm or by bank).
  3. 03Cheques issued, not presented → ADD to cash book balance.
  4. 04Cheques deposited, not collected → DEDUCT from cash book balance.
  5. 05Bank charges / interest on OD (bank knows, firm doesn't) → DEDUCT.
  6. 06Interest/dividend collected by bank, direct deposits by debtor → ADD.
  7. 07Dishonoured cheque/bill (already treated as received) → DEDUCT.
  8. 08Debit balance in Cash Book = favourable; Credit balance in Cash Book = overdraft (unfavourable).
  9. 09Starting from Passbook instead of Cash Book? Reverse every +/− sign.
  10. 10Passbook: deposits shown in Credit column, withdrawals in Debit column (it's the bank's ledger of YOUR account).
