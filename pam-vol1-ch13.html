13.01 Role of Pension Section 🔴 VI▾

GM/Director/Dy.Director of Accounts (Postal) responsible for quick, correct pension finalisation for all eligible employees/dependants. 11 main functions:

Service verification (Rule 32, CCS Pension Rules 1972) Enfacement Report on applications Issue PPO/Family Pension/DCRG orders Check/audit pension & gratuity vouchers Periodical returns compilation Third-party pension processing Service charge realisation Bank disbursement processing Deputation/Foreign Service records Revision of pension cases Family pension authorisation post-death
13.02 Verification of Service (Rule 32) 🔴 VI▾

2 types of verification cases:

TypeTrigger
1. Qualifying Service VerificationAfter 18 years of service, or when 5 years remain before retirement — whichever is earlier (Rule 32, CCS Pension Rules 1972)
2. Final VerificationAt Enfacement Report stage while finalising the pension case

Register of applications maintained in Form Sy-133. Service Book checked for: completeness, non-qualifying period entries, annual verification notes, acquittance-roll verification (Rule 58(a)(iv)&(v)), military/training service counting, Form-3 pasted, CGEGIS entries.

🔴 Key timeline: Verification statements disposed within 15 working days of receipt; fortnightly outstanding-case report submitted on 1st and 16th of each month (1st also to Head of PAO via Director/Dy.Director).

Verification under Rule 32(1)&(2) is final — not re-opened except for a subsequent rule change.

13.03 Reporting on Applications for Pension 🔴 VI▾

Applications registered in Form Sy-134 (per Rules 67, 80, 82 CCS Pension Rules). PPOs must issue not later than 1 month before retirement date. Emoluments check limited to a maximum of 24 months preceding retirement (not a full Service Book audit).

12 key checks on each application (a–l): proper form; service duly verified; sub-qualifying-age service rejected; competent-authority orders for non-qualifying period recorded; correct qualifying/non-qualifying entries; correct pension class & calculation; Form-7/18 entries proper; uniform spelling across documents; photos/specimen signatures attested (Rule 55); outstanding dues intimated (Form-8); no pending/contemplated disciplinary case; terminal benefits — once authorised, not alterable except for typographical error with Govt approval.

🧠 "C-I-S-R-F" — Compensation, Invalid, Superannuation, Retiring, Family — the 5 pension classes.

13.05 Commutation of Pension 🔴 VI▾

Authority for commuted-value payment issued by Director/Dy.Director (Postal); reduced pension effective from actual payment date or 3 months from authority-issue date, whichever applies. Disbursing Officer notes date + reduced pension on both PPO halves.

Recorded in Commutation Payment Register — Form Sy-170; reconciled monthly with Detail Book. No separate check register for commuted pensions — noted directly in PPO Register & Pension Check Register (guards against overpayment).

Pensioner refused commutation on medical grounds, or applying for re-examination after declining age-addition recommendation → re-examined by Medical Board (Rules 26 & 27, CCS Commutation Rules 1981). Re-examination under Rule 26 only after ≥1 year from first exam; Rule 27 application within 1 month of receiving Part III of Form 4.

13.06 Adjustment of Pensionary Charges (Inter-Govt) 🟠 FAQ▾

Governed by Section IV, Appendix 3, Account Code Vol-I — constitutes the Article 280 (Constitution) agreement on pensionary charge distribution between Governments.

Govt granting a pensionary concession (e.g. condoning deficiency) needing another Govt's additional liability → must obtain that Govt's prior sanction
↓
If one Govt's pension rules are more generous → other Govt bears only its own (lower) proportionate share
↓
Concession granted unilaterally → granting Govt bears full increase, unless the other Govt accepts a share (then split by length of service under each)

3-year convention: Past-transaction adjustments between Departments generally limited to 3 years from date mistake is discovered (not 3 financial years back) — a convention, not a rigid rule; mutual agreement can override.

"Length of Service" = qualifying service per Rule 3(i), Section IV, Appendix 3 — unverified service cannot be ignored for apportionment.

Family Pension check register kept separate from other pension check registers. Every entry attested by AAO, countersigned by Branch Officer.

Objection Book closed ≥2 clear working days before submission due date; reviewed by Accounts Officer on the 28th of each month. Items unsettled >6 months tracked in a separate register.

Non-drawal reporting: Head Postmaster reports every 6 months (due early June/December; reminder if not received by 10th) — cases of non-drawal >1 year (all others) and >3 years (service pension, separately flagged).

13.08 Records Destruction & Bank Disbursement 🟡 Concept▾

Destruction of Pension Records: Weeded out 7 years after pensioner's/family pensioner's death, provided all arrears claims are settled & no further eligible Family Pension claimants exist.

Disbursement via Banks: Per 'Scheme for Payment of Pensions to Central Govt. Civil Pensioners by Authorized Banks.' PAO issues PPO → sent to Bank's CPPC (Centralised Pension Processing Centre). Payment adjustment via RBI-CAS Nagpur; scroll from CPPC → Account Current Section posts TE to Major Head 3201-07 → transferred to Pension Section for cent-percent audit (until further orders).

Military Personnel Payments: "IMP Payments" head reconciled with supporting schedule; consolidated debit raised against CDA(P) Allahabad via Settlement Account, routed through Kapurthala PAO.
