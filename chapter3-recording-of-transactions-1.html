3.1

Business Transactions & Source Documents

🟠 Frequently Asked
Source Document / Voucher: a document providing evidence of a transaction (cash memo, invoice, sales bill, pay-in-slip, cheque, salary slip). All recording in the books of account is done on the basis of vouchers.

Types of Accounting Vouchers

VoucherUsed For
Transaction VoucherSimple transaction — one debit, one credit
Debit VoucherCompound — one credit, multiple debits
Credit VoucherCompound — one debit, multiple credits
Journal / Complex VoucherComplex — multiple debits AND multiple credits
📝 Probable Exam QuestionDistinguish between a simple and a compound voucher, giving an example of each.
3.2

Accounting Equation

🔴 Guaranteed Numerical Question
A = L + CAlso derived as: A − L = C  |  A − C = L  ·  Also called the Balance Sheet Equation

Every transaction is analysed for its effect on Assets, Liabilities and Capital — the equation must balance after every single transaction.

Worked Mini-Example

TransactionEffect
Rohit starts business with cash ₹5,00,000Cash (Asset) ↑ ₹5,00,000 · Capital ↑ ₹5,00,000
Goods costing ₹25,000 sold for ₹35,000 to Rajani (credit)Stock ↓ ₹25,000 · Debtors ↑ ₹35,000 · Capital ↑ ₹10,000 (profit)

3.3

Using Debit and Credit

🔴 Very Important for Exam

Every account is shaped like a "T" — left side = Debit (Dr.), right side = Credit (Cr.)

Account Title
Dr. (Left)
entries increasing the item*
Cr. (Right)
entries decreasing the item*

*for Asset/Expense accounts — reversed for Liability/Capital/Revenue accounts (see below)

3.3.1 Rules of Debit and Credit — 5 Account Categories

All Accounts (5 categories)
ASSET
  • ↑ = Debit
  • ↓ = Credit
EXPENSES / LOSSES
  • ↑ = Debit
  • ↓ = Credit
LIABILITY
  • ↑ = Credit
  • ↓ = Debit
CAPITAL
  • ↑ = Credit
  • ↓ = Debit
REVENUE / GAINS
  • ↑ = Credit
  • ↓ = Debit

3.5

The Ledger

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Ledger = Principal Book of Accounts — a collection of all accounts, where the net result of transactions relating to a particular account can be ascertained on any given date.
Name of Account
Dr.
Date · Particulars · J.F. · Amount
Cr.
Date · Particulars · J.F. · Amount

Journal vs Ledger

JournalLedger
Book of first entry (original)Book of second entry
Chronological recordAnalytical record
Greater importance as legal evidenceDerived from journal
Basis of classification = TransactionBasis of classification = Account
Recording process = JournalisingRecording process = Posting

3.5.1 Classification of Ledger Accounts

Permanent AccountsTemporary Accounts
Assets, Liabilities, CapitalRevenue, Expenses
Balanced & carried forward to next periodClosed to Trading / P&L A/c at year-end
Appear in the Balance SheetAppear in the Trading/P&L Account

Rule: the account debited in the journal is debited in the ledger; the account credited is credited — with the other account's name written in the Particulars column (not the transaction description).

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
DEAD = Debit
Drawings, Expenses, Assets, Depreciation/losses — increase in these is always a Debit.
CLIP = Credit
Capital, Liabilities, Income/Revenue, Profit/Gains — increase in these is always a Credit.
"Debit what comes in, Credit what goes out"
Classic memory line for Asset accounts — helps recall the T-account left/right logic quickly.
L‑D‑P‑F‑A
Posting steps → Locate account → Date → Particulars → Folio → Amount. "Lazy Dogs Prefer Fresh Apples."
Journal = "J for Journey" (first stop)
Journal is where every transaction's journey begins (Book of Original Entry); Ledger is the destination (Principal Book) where postings collect.

Last-Day Revision Notes

🔴 Read this 30 minutes before the exam
  1. 01All recording starts from a Voucher/Source Document.
  2. 02Accounting Equation: A = L + C — must balance after every transaction.
  3. 035 account categories: Asset, Liability, Capital, Expense/Loss, Revenue/Gain.
  4. 04DEAD = Debit (Drawings, Expenses, Assets, Losses); CLIP = Credit (Capital, Liabilities, Income, Profit).
  5. 05Journal = book of original/first entry, chronological. Ledger = principal/second book, analytical.
  6. 06Journalising = recording in Journal; Posting = transferring to Ledger.
  7. 07Simple entry = 2 accounts; Compound entry = more than 2 accounts.
  8. 08Ledger Folio (L.F.) column is filled only at the time of posting, not while journalising.
  9. 09Permanent accounts (A, L, C) → Balance Sheet; Temporary accounts (Revenue, Expense) → P&L A/c, closed every year.
  10. 10Posting rule: write the other account's name in Particulars — never the transaction narration.
