★ One-Page Revision Sheet

Core Definition

  • Accounting = identifying, measuring, recording, classifying, summarising, interpreting & communicating financial information.
  • Called the "language of business."

Accounting Cycle (6 steps)

  • Journalising → Posting & Balancing → Trial Balance → Trading A/c → P&L A/c → Balance Sheet

3 Bases of Accounting

  • Cash basis — recognise on receipt/payment
  • Accrual basis — recognise on earning/incurring (mandatory for companies u/s 128(1))
  • Hybrid — mix of both

5 Branches of Accounting

  • Financial · Cost · Management · Social Responsibility · Human Resources

Users of Information

  • Internal: Owners, Management, Employees
  • External: Creditors, Investors, Customers, Tax authorities, Government, Researchers, Public

Role of Accountant (6 hats)

  • Record keeper · Info provider · Asset protector · Financial advisor · Tax manager · Public relation officer

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Sections / Rules

  • Companies Act 2013, Sec 128(1): All companies must maintain books of accounts on accrual basis.
  • Double-entry bookkeeping system — foundational rule of modern accounting.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Hybrid basis is an exception-blend: revenues/assets on cash basis, expenses/liabilities on accrual basis.
  • Cash basis ignores credit transactions entirely until cash moves — an exception to the accrual norm.

📅 Important Dates

  • 23 centuries ago: Kautilya's Arthashastra — earliest Indian accounting reference.
  • 12th century: earliest records of debit & credit.
  • 1494: Luca Pacioli (Italy) develops double-entry bookkeeping.
  • 18th–19th century: Industrial Revolution → joint stock companies → modern financial accounting.
  • 20th century: Management Accounting emerges.
  • 21st century: Social Responsibility Accounting emerges.

🏛️ Important Authorities

  • AICPA (American Institute of Certified Public Accountants) — classic definition of accounting.
  • AAA (American Accounting Association) — process-based definition.
  • Government & tax authorities — use accounting data for taxation, subsidy, policy.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

No statutory time-limit specified in this unit — accounting period is generally the financial year (concept covered in Unit 2).

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision in this conceptual unit — accounting supports computation & settlement of tax, and legal compliance (PF, ESI, TDS), where penalties fall under respective Acts.

📝 Probable Question: Explain any three branches of accounting. (6 marks)
Cash Basis
  • Income received
  • Expense paid
  • Ignores credit txns till cash moves
Accrual Basis
  • Income earned (received or not)
  • Expense incurred (paid or not)
  • Mandatory for companies — Sec 128(1)
Hybrid Basis
  • Revenue/assets → cash basis
  • Expenses/liabilities → accrual basis
  • Best-of-both combination
Decision Tree: Which basis applies?
Is the entity a company under Companies Act 2013? → Yes → Accrual basis compulsory (Sec 128(1))
Not a company, wants simplicity, tracks only cash moves? → Cash basis
Wants a practical blend? → Hybrid/Mixed basis
🟡 Conceptual Understanding "Accrual" and "mercantile" are the same thing — the syllabus uses both names interchangeably.
