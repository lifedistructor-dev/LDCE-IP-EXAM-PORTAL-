★ One-Page Revision Sheet

CAS Meaning

  • Maintaining accounts using computers — hardware + software process transactions into reports

6 Components of CAS

  • Hardware · Software · People · Procedure · Data · Connectivity

3 Types of Accounting Software

  • Readymade (Tally, Busy, Marg) — small biz
  • Customised — medium/large biz
  • Tailor-made — large biz, highest cost

3 Codification Methods

  • Sequential codes (CL001, CL002...)
  • Block codes (100-199, 200-299...)
  • Mnemonic codes (SJ = Sales Journal)

Key Fact

  • Fundamentals of accounting (debit/credit rules) DON'T change — only the recording method changes

Audit Trail

  • Ability to trace figures from source document to final accounts, in CAS

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Rules

  • Accounting equation used for grouping accounts: Assets = Liabilities + Capital + (Revenues − Expenses).
  • CAS auto-generates: day books/journals, ledger, trial balance, trading A/c, P&L A/c, balance sheet.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • CAS cannot detect unanticipated errors — unlike a human accountant who might sense something is "off," a computer only follows its programming.
  • Despite computerisation, the fundamentals of accounting stay the same — debit/credit golden rules are unchanged.

📅 Important Dates

No historical dates in this unit — content is technology/software-oriented rather than legal/chronological.

🏛️ Important Authorities

No external regulatory authority cited — this unit covers technical/software concepts rather than statutory ones.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

No statutory time-limit — but a key CAS advantage is that reports are generated "within seconds" once data entry is complete.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — CAS limitations (security breaches, system failure) are operational risks, not statutory penalties.

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Computerised Accounting System processes transactions via hardware and software to produce accounting reports.
  • The fundamental principles of debit and credit remain unchanged in computerised accounting.
  • Accounting software is of three types: readymade, customised, and tailor-made.
  • Grouping of accounts follows the accounting equation; codification uses sequential, block, or mnemonic methods.
  • Audit trail allows tracing figures from source document to final accounts.
