1 Topic & Definition 🔴 VIP

Topic: When share capital is not enough to meet long-term needs, companies borrow long-term funds by issuing Debentures — through private placement or public offer.

Definition — Debenture: From Latin 'debere' (to borrow). A written instrument under the company's common seal acknowledging a debt, containing a contract for repayment of principal and payment of interest at a fixed rate.

Sec. 2(30), Companies Act 2013: 'Debenture' includes Debenture Inventory, Bonds, and any other securities of a company, whether constituting a charge on assets or not.

Bond: Also an instrument acknowledging debt; traditionally issued by Government, now the terms 'debenture' and 'bond' are used interchangeably.

Likely Q: "Define debenture. What does Section 2(30) of the Companies Act, 2013 include in this term?"

2 Mind Map — Types of Debentures 🔴 VIP

Types of Debentures (5 classification angles)
  • By Security: Secured (fixed/floating charge) vs Unsecured/Naked
  • By Tenure: Redeemable vs Irredeemable/Perpetual
  • By Convertibility: Convertible (Fully/Partly) vs Non-Convertible
  • By Coupon Rate: Specific Coupon Rate vs Zero Coupon Rate (Deep Discount)
  • By Registration: Registered vs Bearer (transferable by delivery, uses coupons)
Mnemonic — "STCCR": Security, Tenure, Convertibility, Coupon rate, Registration — the 5 angles to classify debentures.
Likely Q: "Explain the various types of debentures a company can issue." (classic theory question, 4–6 marks)

3 Comparison — Shares vs Debentures 🟠 Frequently Asked

Shares

  • Represents ownership (owned capital)
  • Return = Dividend (variable, appropriation of profit)
  • Not normally repaid during company's life
  • Voting rights to holders
  • Not secured by any charge
  • Cannot be converted into debentures

Debentures

  • Acknowledgement of debt (borrowed capital)
  • Return = Interest (fixed, charge against profit — payable even if no profit)
  • Repayable on expiry of specified period
  • No voting rights normally
  • Usually secured — fixed or floating charge
  • Can be converted into shares if terms provide (Convertible Debentures)
Likely Q: "Distinguish between shares and debentures." (Very common — appears almost every year)

6 Debentures as Collateral Security 🟠 Frequently Asked

When a company takes a loan/overdraft, the lender may ask for debentures as additional/secondary security besides the primary security (asset pledged). If the company defaults, the lender first recovers from the primary security; if it falls short, the lender can invoke the collateral debentures.

Method 1 — No Entry

  • No journal entry passed (no liability created)
  • Just a note below the loan in Notes to Accounts: "Secured by issue of ___ debentures as collateral security"

Method 2 — Entry Passed

  • Debenture Suspense A/c Dr. → To Debentures A/c
  • Debenture Suspense shown as deduction from Debentures in Notes to Accounts
  • Reversed when loan is repaid
Likely Q: "What is meant by issue of debentures as collateral security? Explain the two methods of recording it in the books."

8 Important Rules — Debenture Redemption Reserve (DRR) 🔴 VIP — Most Tested Rule

Category of CompanyDRR Requirement
All India Financial Institutions (AIFIs) & Banking CompaniesNo DRR required (public or private issue)
NBFCs & Housing Finance Companies (public issue)25% of outstanding debentures (public issue); Nil for privately placed
Other companies (manufacturing/infra) — public issue25% of value of outstanding debentures
Listed companies — private placement25% DRR required
Unlisted companies — private placement25% of value of outstanding debentures

Investment condition: On/before 30th April each year, invest/deposit ≥ 15% of debentures maturing during that financial year (in bank deposits, Govt securities, or notified bonds).

Mnemonic — "25 by Public, 15 by April": DRR = 25% of debentures; invest 15% of maturing debentures by 30 April.
Likely Q: "What percentage of Debenture Redemption Reserve is required as per Rule 18(7) of Companies (Share Capital and Debentures) Rules, 2014? Which companies are exempt?"

9 Important Exceptions 🟡 Conceptual

  • The Companies Act, 2013 places no restriction on issuing debentures at a discount (unlike shares, which cannot ordinarily be issued at a discount).
  • No DRR is required for debentures issued by AIFIs and Banking Companies — a blanket exemption regardless of public/private issue.
  • Companies exempted from creating DRR may redeem debentures wholly out of capital; others must transfer 100% of face value to DRR if redeeming purely out of profits.
  • DRR is required only in respect of the non-convertible portion of partly convertible debentures — the convertible portion is exempt.
  • Debentures issued as Collateral Security create no liability by themselves — hence Method 1 requires no journal entry at all.
Likely Q: "Is a company required to create DRR for fully convertible debentures? Justify."

12 Important Penalties / Charges 🔴 VIP

  • Interest on debentures is a charge against profit — must be paid even if the company earns no profit (unlike dividend, which is only an appropriation).
  • Failure to deduct/deposit TDS on debenture interest attracts Income Tax Act penal provisions (disallowance/interest under IT law).
  • Amount credited to DRR cannot be utilised for any purpose other than debenture redemption — misuse is a compliance violation.
Likely Q: "Why is interest on debentures called a 'charge against profits' and not an 'appropriation of profits'?"

13 Methods of Redemption of Debentures 🔴 VIP

1. Payment in Lump Sum Whole amount paid at maturity, at par or premium
▼
2. Payment in Instalments Total liability divided by years; specific debentures drawn by lot
▼
3. Purchase in Open Market Company buys back its own debentures for cancellation
▼
4. Conversion into Shares/New Debentures Only for Convertible Debentures; based on actual proceeds originally realised

Sinking Fund Method — 11-Step Logic 🟡 Conceptual

  • Year 1: Set aside fixed profit → Debenture Redemption Fund (DRF) A/c → invest in DRF Investment (DRFI)
  • Yr 2 onward: Receive interest on DRFI → transfer to DRF → invest interest + fresh appropriation
  • Last year: Receive interest, set aside final profit, encash all DRFI
  • Transfer profit/loss on encashment to DRF A/c → Pay debentureholders → Transfer DRF balance to General Reserve
Likely Q: "Explain the Sinking Fund method of redemption of debentures with the necessary journal entries." (High-value numerical, 8 marks)

14 Journal Entries — Ledger Cheat-Sheet 🔴 VIP

1. Issue at Par (2 instalments) ▶
Bank A/c Dr. To Debenture Application A/c (Application money received) Debenture Application A/c Dr. To Debentures A/c (Transferred on allotment) Debenture Allotment A/c Dr. To Debentures A/c (Amount due on allotment) Bank A/c Dr. To Debenture Allotment A/c (Allotment money received)
2. Issue at Discount, Redeemable at Par ▶
Debenture Allotment A/c Dr. Discount on Issue of Debentures A/c Dr. To Debentures A/c (face value)
3. Issue at Premium, Redeemable at Par ▶
Debenture Allotment A/c Dr. To Debentures A/c (face value) To Securities Premium Reserve A/c (premium)
4. Issue at Par, Redeemable at Premium ▶
Debenture Allotment A/c Dr. Loss on Issue of Debentures A/c Dr. (premium on redemption) To Debentures A/c (face value) To Premium on Redemption of Debentures A/c
5. Collateral Security (Method 2) ▶
Debenture Suspense A/c Dr. To Debentures A/c (Issued as collateral security for bank loan) -- reversed on loan repayment -- Debentures A/c Dr. To Debenture Suspense A/c
6. Interest on Debentures (with TDS) ▶
Debenture Interest A/c Dr. To Income Tax Payable A/c (TDS) To Debentureholders A/c Debentureholders A/c Dr. To Bank A/c (Interest paid) Income Tax Payable A/c Dr. To Bank A/c (TDS deposited with govt.) Statement of P&L Dr. To Debenture Interest A/c (Transferred at year-end)
7. Redemption at Par (Lump Sum) ▶
Debentures A/c Dr. To Debentureholders A/c Debentureholders A/c Dr. To Bank A/c
8. Redemption at Premium ▶
Debentures A/c Dr. Premium on Redemption of Debentures A/c Dr. To Debentureholders A/c Debentureholders A/c Dr. To Bank A/c
9. Sinking Fund — Key Entries ▶
Statement of P&L Dr. To Debenture Redemption Fund (DRF) A/c (Fixed profit set aside) DRF Investment A/c Dr. To Bank A/c (Amount invested) Bank A/c Dr. To Interest on DRFI A/c (Interest received, reinvested each year except last) DRF A/c Dr. To General Reserve A/c (Final transfer after redemption)
Likely Q: Board papers commonly ask for the FULL chain: "Issue at discount, redeemable at premium, with DRR transfer and final redemption" — practice it as one continuous problem.

15 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics 🟢 Quick Revision

Debenture vs Share return: "Share gets a Slice (dividend, variable); Debenture gets a Deal (interest, fixed)."
DRR %: "25 for the Reserve, 15 by April" — 25% DRR of outstanding debentures; invest 15% of maturing amount by 30 April.
Redemption methods: "Little Investors Prefer Conversion" → Lump sum, Instalments, open market Purchase, Conversion.
No DRR needed: "Banks & AIFIs Don't Reserve" — Banking Cos. and All India Financial Institutions are exempt from DRR.
