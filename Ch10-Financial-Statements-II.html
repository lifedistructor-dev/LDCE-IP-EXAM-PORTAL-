Core Idea: Adjustments are items NOT in the Trial Balance (given as "Additional Information") that must be recorded twice — once in the Trading & P&L A/c, once in the Balance Sheet — because every adjustment entry has two effects.

10.2Closing Stock

Very Important
Closing Stock A/c  Dr.
  To Trading A/c
Cost of unsold goods at year-end. Next year it becomes opening stock. If adjusted through Purchases A/c ("adjusted purchases"), it is NOT separately shown on credit side of Trading A/c.
🎯 Trap: When closing stock appears INSIDE the trial balance (not as additional info), show it ONLY in the Balance Sheet — never again in Trading A/c.

10.8Bad Debts (incl. Further Bad Debts)

Very Important
Amount not realisable from debtors — a loss. If given as ADDITIONAL information (not in trial balance), it is called Further Bad Debts.
Bad Debts A/c  Dr.
  To Debtors A/c
🎯 Trap: Further bad debts REDUCE debtors on the assets side AND increase the bad debts expense on the debit side — a double effect students often miss.

10.10Provision for Discount on Debtors

Frequently Asked
Estimated discount to encourage prompt payment — calculated ONLY on good debtors (Debtors − Further Bad Debts − Provision for Doubtful Debts).
Profit and Loss A/c  Dr.
  To Provision for Discount on Debtors A/c
🎯 Formula: Provision for Discount = (Debtors − Further Bad Debts − Provision for Doubtful Debts) × Rate%

10.11Manager's Commission

Very ImportantFrequently Asked
Commission on net profit — default assumption: before charging commission, unless stated otherwise.

Before Charging Commission

Commission = Net Profit (before commission) × Rate/100

After Charging Commission

Commission = Net Profit (before commission) × Rate/(100+Rate)

Profit and Loss A/c  Dr.
  To Manager's Commission A/c
🎯 Probable Question: MCQ/numerical testing BOTH formulas in the same question — the "after charging" formula is the most commonly confused point in this chapter.
Trick: "Before" → straight %. "After" → divide by (100 + rate), not 100.

10.12Interest on Capital

ConceptualQuick Revision
Calculated on opening capital; if additional capital is introduced during the year, interest on that portion runs only from the date it was brought in.
Interest on Capital A/c  Dr.
  To Capital A/c
🎯 Trap: Interest on capital REDUCES net profit (debit side of P&L) but is simultaneously ADDED BACK to capital in the Balance Sheet — net effect on total Balance Sheet is nil, only the profit figure moves.

🚨 Last-Day Revision Notes

  • Every adjustment touches BOTH statements — Trading/P&L AND Balance Sheet. Never stop after just one.
  • Provision for Discount on Debtors comes AFTER Provision for Doubtful Debts is deducted — order matters.
  • Manager's Commission default = before charging commission, unless the question explicitly says "after".
  • Depreciation reduces the asset value directly — it is NOT shown as a liability.
  • Old Provision for Doubtful Debts (from trial balance) is always netted off against the current year's requirement.
  • Interest on Capital and Interest on Drawings move in opposite directions on the Capital account — don't mix signs.
Quick Trick — "OPAI": Outstanding & Accrued = Add · Prepaid & Income-in-advance = Subtract.
