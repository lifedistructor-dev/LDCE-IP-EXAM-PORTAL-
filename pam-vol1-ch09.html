9.01 Introduction — Voted vs Charged Expenditure 🔴 VI▾

Budget = statement of estimated annual receipts & expenditure. Expenditure estimates split into Charged and Voted. In DoP, Charged = sums required to satisfy a court-of-law decree; met from Consolidated Fund of India, presented to Lok Sabha as Detailed Demand for Grants (DDG) except for the charged portion.

Budget preparation responsibility: Secretary, DoP (= Chief Accounting Authority). Detailed procedure → Postal Manual Vol-II. Budget Division (PA Wing, Directorate) formulates & submits to MoF; scheme-expenditure estimates come from Corporate Planning Division.

🧠 "Charged = Court order; Voted = everything else, needs Lok Sabha vote."

9.02 Detailed Demand for Grants (DDG) 🔴 VI▾

DDG = form of expenditure estimates from Consolidated Fund, included in Annual Financial Statement, voted in Lok Sabha per Article 113 of the Constitution.

Prepared by Head of Circles in consultation with Circle Internal Financial Advisor, based on Budget Circular from MoF (Dept. of Economic Affairs), routed via Budget Division PA Wing.

Article 112: Annual Financial Statement must distinguish Revenue-account expenditure from other expenditure. GFR Rule 50(2) reinforces this; GFR Rule 84 defines Capital vs Revenue Expenditure.

4 estimates prepared: (a) Preliminary schedule of demands (b) Budget Estimate & Revised Estimate (c) Three-monthly estimate of important alterations (d) Six-monthly estimate.

2 expenditure parts: Standing Charges (permanent establishment, TA, contingent — varies yearly) vs New Expenditure (schemes + any abnormal increase) — prepared separately, new items need prior competent-authority approval before joining standing budget.

9.03 Budget Cell Structure at Circle Level 🟠 FAQ▾

Each Circle's Budget Cell headed by Chief Postmaster General (CPMG), with 2 units:

Budget Control Unit (BCU)
Headed by Head of Postal Accounts Office. Prepares/compiles Non-Scheme DFG for the circle; coordinates with Budget Division, Directorate.
Budget Operating Unit (BOU)
Headed by Director Postal Services (HQ). Both units under overall supervision of Head of Circle (HOC).

Budget Division (Directorate) verifies Circle DFGs, compiles data, prepares final DDG for scrutiny by AS&FA and approval by Secretary, DoP.

9.04 Scheme Expenditure — 4 Major Schemes 🟠 FAQ▾

Plan-scheme demand prepared by Corporate Division (Directorate), incorporated into DDG by Budget Division. NE-region revenue expenditure demanded separately under Major Head 2552.

1. Postal Operations (Rural Access, Network Integration, IT Systems, Business Promotion, Philately, Quality of Service) 2. Human Resource Management (Work Studies, PPS, Training Upgrade) 3. Estate Management 4. Setting up of India Post Payments Bank

🧠 "P-H-E-I" = Postal Ops, HR Mgmt, Estate Mgmt, IPPB — the 4 major schemes.

9.05 Revenue & Capital Expenditure — Object Heads 🔴 VI▾

Statement shown object-head-wise, separately for Revenue (Major Head 3201/2552) and Capital (Major Head 5201/5465) sections, alongside BE/RE of previous year & actuals of the year before that.

Major Head 3201/2552 — Revenue Expdr. Object Codes (sample)
01 Salaries · 02 Wages · 03 OT Allowance · 04 Pensionary Charges · 06 Medical · 11 Domestic Travel · 13 Office Expenses · 14 Rent/Rates/Taxes · 21 Supplies & Materials · 26 Advertisement · 27 Minor Works & Maintenance · 50 Other Charges · 64 Write-off/Losses
Major Head 5201/5465 — Capital Expdr. Object Codes (sample)
101.01 PO Land · 101.02 PO/RMS Building · 101.03 Mail Motor Services · 104 Mechanisation & Modernisation · 202.02 Admin Office Building · 203.02 Staff Quarters · 5465 IPPB Investment

DFG shows Voted and Charged requirements separately — care taken that no Voted-type expenditure is mixed into the Charged item.

9.06 Revenue Receipts — Major Head 1201 🟡 Concept▾

Estimated after detailed projection of revenue from all products/services DoP offers. Broad categories:

Postal Receipts (Speed Post, Business Post, e-Post, Philately, EMS, Registered/Parcel post etc.) Commission on Money Orders & Postal Orders (MO, IPO, IMO, EMO) Sale of Central Recruitment Fee Stamps Other Services & Fees (SB/Certificates management, RTI fees, Aadhaar/UIDAI charges, MGNREGA wages) Net receipt from other Postal Administrations IPPB Commission Other Receipts (Media Post, Logistics Post, Mutual Fund commission, examination fees)
9.07 Control Over Expenditure & Revenue Targets 🔴 VI▾

Secretary (Posts) is responsible for controlling DoP's whole expenditure against grants; HOCs must provide inputs & watch expenditure monthly (Budget vs Expenses, Budget vs Revenue Receipts per minor head). Goal: detect probable excesses/savings — no expenditure should occur under a head with no allotment.

Revenue targets & recoveries (approved by MoF) monitored by HOCs; Director of Postal Accounts furnishes actual revenue/recovery figures by end of each month. Joint responsibility of HOC + PAO to reconcile booked vs subsidiary figures.

Budget Division submits Monthly Statement to Secretary (Posts) — scheme & non-scheme progress vs allotment
↓
Financial Review of Revenue/Expenditure prepared quarterly (Apr-Jun, Jul-Sep, Oct-Dec, Jan-Mar) → submitted to MoC & MoF, in "Cash Management System" format (Month / Revenue / Cumulative / Capital / Cumulative / Total Expdr. / Total Cumulative)

Likely Q: Who is responsible for controlling DoP's overall expenditure against grants? → Secretary (Posts), assisted by HOCs' monthly monitoring.
