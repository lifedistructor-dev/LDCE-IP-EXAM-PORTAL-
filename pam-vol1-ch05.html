5.01 Introduction — What is a Remittance 🔴 VI▾

Remittance = transfer of cash/cheque between jurisdiction of one Head Post Office and another (Rule 152, P&T FHB Vol-I). Also covers liabilities/emergent advances paid by one HPO on another's behalf, charged as remittance.

RSAO
Remittance within Same Accounts Office — i.e. intra-circle remittance (same Circle PAO jurisdiction)
POR
Post Office Remittance — i.e. inter-circle remittance (2+ different Circles; per Rule 785, Postal Manual Vol-II)

Under CBS, SB deposits/withdrawals at a unit other than the account-holding unit are also settled via Remittance Heads (reconciled by PAO). CBS cheques are realized via Central Grid Circle, accounted at originating office through Remittance heads.

5.02 Key Definitions 🔴 VI▾
Remittance: Transfer of funds/equivalent between two disbursing officers of same/other Department.
Inter-sol Remittance GL: CBS remittances between two Service Outlets.
Grid Clearance Remittance GL: Transactions via instruments realized at Central Grid Circle across different Circles.
Intra-circle Remittance (RSAO): Within same circle accounts jurisdiction.
Inter-circle Remittance (POR): Between two different circle accounts jurisdictions.
Presentation Session (CTS): Morning session — other-bank cheques received by POs & POSB cheques received by banks are presented for clearing.
Return Session (CTS): Afternoon session — "Realised"/"Returned" remarks marked by banks & POs.
Outward/Inward Presentation: Bank cheques PO presents to banks (Outward) / PO cheques banks present to PO (Inward).
Outward/Inward Return: Rejected PO cheques returned to banks (Outward) / rejected bank cheques returned to PO (Inward).

🧠 "RSAO stays home, POR travels" — RSAO = same circle, POR = across circles.

5.03 POR Head Rationalisation & Apportionment 🟠 FAQ▾

All POR Heads (circle-wise) rationalised to just 2 heads: POR Inward & POR Outward (per Appendix V charter of accounts). ERP records both "from circle" & "to circle" for each transaction → generates e-schedules for reconciliation.

Apportionment of Revenue/Expenditure across circles done periodically by the Nodal Office in consultation with Circle PAO — original credit/debit sum retained at originating circle; transfer only after competent authority approval.

5.04 Cash-in-Transit & Cheque-in-Transit Monitoring 🔴 VI▾
Cash transferred between units of same DDO → checked for delay in receiving-office acknowledgement; system-paired
↓
Until acknowledged, amount = "Cash in Transit" in remitting office's books
↓
Cash between different DDOs → watched meticulously; unacknowledged delays → objection statement
↓
Cheque transfers HO→sub-units → accounted as "Cheque in Transit", GL mapped & system-paired

No manual/direct posting permitted — all remittances must be system paired; un-reconciled list sent to Head of Office & Regional Heads for corrective action.

CBS Inter-sol: For a day, the Remittance Head should net to zero residuary balance DOP-wide. A Nodal PAO monitors CBS Inter-sol auto-pairing/balancing; variations referred to CBS tech team at CEPT (Centre of Excellence – Postal Technology).

5.05 Role of HPO / Postmaster — RA Numbering 🔴 VI▾

Remittances brought to schedule (Outward & Inward separately) with a Remittance Advice (RA) Number — running serial starting from 1 on 1st April each financial year.

AnnexureCovers
Annexure-ISchedule of Remittances within circle — RSAO Intra-circle
Annexure-IISchedule of Remittances between circles — POR Inter-circle
Annexure-IIIMonthly Statement of Remittances between Postal Circles

Both Annexure I & II schedules contain: Date, RA No., Receiving Office, Description, Debit/Credit Amount, Remarks.

5.06 Role of Postal Accounts Office — Reconciliation 🔴 VI▾

PAO maintains Register of Remittances per circle; confirms e-schedule abstract matches CGA report during T+3 upload.

Register/e-register scrutinised & reviewed by AAO + Branch Officer fortnightly. Abnormal variation/non-adjustment beyond 3 months → reported to Head of Office/PAO.

Annexure-III (Monthly Statement): Sent by 6th of the 2nd month following month of account; receiving office returns it verified by last week of the 2nd month. Electronic (PDF/Excel) format preferred over manual.

Unlinked Items: Separate registers for Debit & Credit unlinked items; broadsheet register per circle, closed monthly, submitted to DAP. Delay beyond 2 months → reported to Divisional Heads.

Likely Q: By what date is the Monthly Statement of Remittances sent to other Circles, and by when must it be returned? → Sent by 6th of 2nd following month; returned by last week of that 2nd month.

5.07 CTS Grid Clearance – Chennai, Mumbai, Delhi 🟠 FAQ▾

Dedicated GL for Grid clearance: RSAO – Grid Clearance (GL 4878200013 / 87200013), mapped to CGA Major Head 8782.

Postmaster prepares separate Inward/Outward e-schedules per circle with RA numbers (FY-wise from 1st April); PAO verifies RSAO grid-clearance GL against schedule figure; discrepancies taken up with Postmaster; manual schedules discouraged.

🧠 "8782 = the Grid gateway" — CTS Grid Clearance maps to Major Head 8782.
