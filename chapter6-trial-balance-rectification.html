6.1

Meaning of Trial Balance

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Trial Balance: a statement showing the balances (or totals) of debits and credits of all ledger accounts, prepared to verify the arithmetical accuracy of posting.

Normally prepared at the end of an accounting period — monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annually.

Golden Rule

All Assets, Expenses & Receivables → Debit column
All Liabilities, Revenues & Payables → Credit column

  • Shows both totals AND balances
6.4

Significance of Agreement of Trial Balance

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Q: Does the error affect equal debits = equal credits?
YES → Trial Balance will NOT tally → error is easy to detect
NO → Trial Balance WILL still tally → error hides silently (e.g. wrong account, Errors of Principle, Compensating Errors)
6.4.1

4 Types of Errors

🔴 The single most-tested classification in this chapter
TypeMeaningAffects Trial Balance?
Errors of CommissionWrong posting, wrong totalling/balancing, wrong casting of subsidiary books, wrong amount recordedUsually YES
Errors of OmissionComplete omission (transaction never recorded) or Partial omission (recorded but not posted)Complete = NO; Partial = usually YES
Errors of PrincipleViolates accounting principles — e.g. capital expenditure treated as revenue expenditureNO
Compensating ErrorsTwo or more errors cancel each other's net effectNO

6.5

Searching for Errors

🟠 Frequently Asked
  • Recast the totals of the debit and credit columns
  • Compare trial balance heads/amounts with the ledger
  • Compare with previous year's trial balance for unexplained differences
  • Re-check balances of individual ledger accounts
  • Re-verify postings from books of original entry

Two Powerful Number Tricks

If the difference is...Suspect...
Divisible by 2An amount = half the difference may be posted on the WRONG SIDE of another account
Divisible by 9A transposition error (e.g. 459 written as 954) or misplaced decimal

Suspense Account

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Suspense Account: a temporary account opened for the amount of difference in the trial balance, so that the trial balance can be tallied provisionally while the search for errors continues. It is closed once all errors are located and rectified.

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
C‑O‑P‑C
4 error types → Commission, Omission, Principle, Compensating. "Careless Officers Prefer Chaos."
"Principle & Compensating never disturb the peace"
Errors of Principle and Compensating Errors NEVER affect the trial balance's agreement — the trickiest exam trap.
÷9 = Transposition, ÷2 = Wrong Side
Difference divisible by 9 → suspect digit transposition (459↔954). Difference divisible by 2 → suspect an item posted to the wrong side of another account.
Suspense = "Temporary Parking Lot"
Suspense Account temporarily "parks" the unexplained trial balance difference until every error is found — then it empties out to zero.
