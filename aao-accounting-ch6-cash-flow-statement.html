1 Topic & Definition 🔴 VIP

Topic: Cash Flow Statement is the third key financial statement — showing inflows and outflows of cash and cash equivalents classified into Operating, Investing, and Financing activities.

Governing Standard: AS-3 (Revised), issued by ICAI — mandatory for all listed companies to prepare and present alongside other financial statements annually. It replaced the earlier Fund Flow Statement, which is no longer relevant.

Likely Q: "Which Accounting Standard governs the preparation of Cash Flow Statement? Is it mandatory for all companies?"

2 Objectives & Benefits 🟠 Frequently Asked

Primary objective: Provide useful information about cash inflows/outflows during a period, classified as operating, investing and financing — helping users assess the enterprise's ability to generate cash and its need to utilise it.

  • Evaluates changes in net assets, financial structure (liquidity/solvency)
  • Helps develop models to assess/compare present value of future cash flows
  • Enhances comparability by eliminating effects of differing accounting treatments
  • Helps balance cash inflow/outflow and check accuracy of past cash flow forecasts
Likely Q: "State any three benefits of preparing a Cash Flow Statement."

3 Cash & Cash Equivalents (as per AS-3) 🔴 VIP

Cash

  • Cash in hand
  • Demand deposits with banks

Cash Equivalents

  • Short-term, highly liquid investments
  • Readily convertible to known cash amounts
  • Insignificant risk of value change
  • Maturity ≤ 3 months from date of acquisition

Note: Investment in shares is generally EXCLUDED from cash equivalents — except e.g. preference shares acquired shortly before their specified redemption date with insignificant repayment risk.

Mnemonic: "3 months or less, or it's not cash-less" — the 3-month maturity test is the key exam fact.
Likely Q: "What is the maximum maturity period for an investment to qualify as a cash equivalent?" → 3 months

5 Cash Inflow/Outflow Map 🟠 Frequently Asked

OPERATING + Sale of goods/services, royalties, fees, commission
− Payments to suppliers, employees, taxes
▼
INVESTING + Sale of PPE, long-term investments; interest/dividend received
− Purchase of PPE, non-current investments
▼
FINANCING + Issue of shares/debentures, loans raised
− Redemption of shares/debentures, loan repayment, dividend & interest paid
Special Note: Same activity can classify differently by business type — e.g. purchase/sale of shares is an OPERATING activity for a share brokerage firm, but an INVESTING activity for any other enterprise.
Likely Q: "Why is purchase of shares an operating activity for a share broker but an investing activity for a manufacturing company?"

6 Treatment of Peculiar/Special Items 🔴 VIP — Most Confusing Part

1. Extraordinary Items ▶
Non-recurring items (e.g. loss by theft/earthquake/flood) — classified & disclosed SEPARATELY under whichever activity (operating/investing/financing) they relate to.
2. Interest & Dividend — Non-Financial Enterprise ▶
ItemClassification
Interest paidFinancing Activity
Dividend paidFinancing Activity
Interest receivedInvesting Activity
Dividend receivedInvesting Activity
3. Interest & Dividend — Financial Enterprise ▶
Interest paid, interest received, AND dividend received → all OPERATING activities (its main business is lending/borrowing). Only dividend PAID remains a Financing activity.
4. Taxes on Income & Gains ▶
  • Tax on operating profit → Operating activity
  • Dividend tax → Financing activity (along with dividend paid)
  • Capital gains tax on sale of fixed assets → Investing activity
5. Non-Cash Transactions ▶
Transactions not involving cash/cash equivalents are EXCLUDED entirely from the Cash Flow Statement — e.g. acquisition of machinery by issue of shares, or redemption of debentures by issue of shares. Disclosed elsewhere in the financial statements instead.
Mnemonic — Non-financial firm: "Pay = Finance, Receive = Invest" for both interest and dividend.
Likely Q: "How is interest paid and dividend received treated in the cash flow statement of a non-financial company?" (Extremely common trap question)

7 Indirect Method — Operating Activities 🔴 VIP — Guaranteed Numerical

Starts from Net Profit before Tax and Extraordinary Items (NOT the final Statement of P&L figure) and adjusts for non-cash & non-operating items, then working capital changes.

Net Profit before Tax & Extraordinary Items + Non-cash items (Depreciation, Goodwill w/off, Provisions) + Non-operating expenses (Finance costs, Loss on sale of assets) − Non-operating incomes (Dividend received, Profit on sale of assets) = Operating Profit before Working Capital changes + Increase in Current Liabilities + Decrease in Current Assets − Increase in Current Assets − Decrease in Current Liabilities = Cash Generated from Operations − Income Tax Paid ± Effect of Extraordinary Items = NET CASH FROM OPERATING ACTIVITIES
Mnemonic — Working Capital adjustments: "CA up = cash down; CL up = cash up" — Increase in Current Assets is deducted; Increase in Current Liabilities is added (and vice versa).
Likely Q: "Calculate Net Cash from Operating Activities using the Indirect Method from the given Balance Sheets and additional information." (Guaranteed 8-mark numerical, appears every single year)

8 Ascertaining Cash from Investing & Financing 🟠 Frequently Asked

Prepare a ledger account (e.g. Machinery A/c, Long-term Loan A/c) using opening/closing balances plus additional info (sale/purchase, depreciation) — the balancing figure gives the purchase/proceeds figure needed for the Cash Flow Statement.

1. Open the relevant asset/liability account e.g. Machinery A/c, Investment A/c, Long-term Loan A/c
▼
2. Post opening balance, additions (via P&L for profit on sale), disposals
▼
3. Closing balance = given figure; balancing figure = cash paid/received
Likely Q: "From the following details of Machinery Account, calculate cash flow from investing activities." (Classic ledger-based numerical)

9 Important Exceptions 🟡 Conceptual

  • Purchase/sale of dealing or trading securities → classified as Operating activity (they are inventory for resale), NOT investing.
  • Cash advances/loans made by a financial enterprise → Operating activity (its main business); by any other enterprise → Investing activity.
  • A single transaction may split across categories — e.g. instalment on a deferred-payment asset purchase: interest portion → Financing; principal/loan portion → Investing.
  • Non-cash investing/financing transactions are excluded entirely from the Cash Flow Statement (disclosed elsewhere only).
Likely Q: "Why are cash flows from dealing/trading securities classified as operating activities and not investing activities?"

10 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics 🟢 Quick Revision

3 Activities: "OIF" — Operating, Investing, Financing (in the order they appear in the statement).
Cash equivalent test: "3 months or less, or it's not cash-less."
Non-financial firm — interest/dividend: "Pay = Finance, Receive = Invest."
Financial firm — interest/dividend: "Interest (paid+received) & Dividend received = Operating; only Dividend Paid = Financing."
Working capital in indirect method: "CA up = cash down; CL up = cash up."
Indirect method starting point: Always "Net Profit BEFORE Tax and Extraordinary Items" — never the final P&L figure.

11 One-Page Revision Sheet 🟢 Quick Revision

Governing Standard

  • AS-3 (Revised), ICAI; mandatory for listed companies; replaced Fund Flow Statement

Cash Equivalents Test

  • Short-term, highly liquid, insignificant risk, maturity ≤ 3 months

3 Activities

  • Operating (main business) → Investing (long-term assets/investments) → Financing (owners' capital + borrowings)

Non-Financial Firm

  • Interest/Dividend PAID → Financing; Interest/Dividend RECEIVED → Investing

Financial Firm

  • Interest paid/received + Dividend received → Operating; Dividend paid → Financing

Tax Treatment

  • Income tax on operating profit → Operating; Dividend tax → Financing; Capital gains tax → Investing

Indirect Method Skeleton

  • Net Profit before Tax & Extraordinary Items → +non-cash/non-operating exp − non-operating income → ± working capital changes → −Tax paid → Net Cash from Operations

Non-Cash Transactions

  • Fully excluded from Cash Flow Statement (e.g. asset bought via share issue)
