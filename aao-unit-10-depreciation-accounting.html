★ One-Page Revision Sheet

Definition (R.N. Carter)

  • "Gradual and permanent decrease in the value of an asset from any cause"

SLM Formula

  • Depreciation p.a. = (Cost − Scrap Value) ÷ Useful Life

WDV Formula

  • Rate = [1 − ⁿ√(Scrap Value ÷ Cost)] × 100
  • Applied on WRITTEN DOWN value each year, not original cost

5 Key Characteristics

  • Allocation of cost, not valuation
  • Book value ↓, not market value
  • Gradual & continuous
  • Only for tangible depreciable assets

2 Recording Methods

  • Charge to Asset A/c (asset shown at WDV)
  • Provision for Depreciation A/c (asset shown at original cost)

Related Terms

  • Depletion = natural resources (mines, oil, timber)
  • Amortization = intangible assets (goodwill, patents)

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Sections / Rules

  • Companies Act 2013, Sec 123(1): every company must provide depreciation before declaring dividend.
  • Sec 123, Part C: prescribes useful life — general plant & machinery = 15 years; general furniture & fittings = 10 years.
  • AS 10 (Indian Accounting Standard): depreciation = systematic allocation of depreciable amount over useful life.
  • Income Tax Act: depreciation is deductible while computing business income tax.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Depreciation is NOT charged on intangible or wasting assets — those use amortization/depletion instead.
  • WDV method: asset value never reaches zero (unlike SLM which can fully write off to zero/scrap value).

📅 Important Dates

No historical date — but useful-life prescriptions under Companies Act 2013 Sec 123 Part C set the calculation baseline (e.g., 15 years for plant & machinery).

🏛️ Important Authorities

  • Spicer & Pegler and R.N. Carter — standard definitions quoted in the syllabus.
  • Indian Accounting Standards (AS 10) — governs depreciable amount definition.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Partial-year use → depreciation charged proportionately (e.g., 3/12 months) for the period actually used.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision in this unit — non-provision of depreciation before dividend declaration is a Companies Act compliance issue, not detailed here as a penalty.

Depreciable assets = fixed assets used for more than one accounting period, not meant for resale (buildings, machinery, vehicles, furniture, computers).

📝 Probable Question: Distinguish between straight line method and written down value method of depreciation (any five points). (8 marks — most repeated question in this unit)
Charging to Asset A/c
  • Depreciation credited directly to Asset A/c
  • Asset shown at WRITTEN DOWN VALUE in balance sheet
Provision for Depreciation A/c
  • Depreciation credited to a separate Provision A/c
  • Asset shown at ORIGINAL COST; provision shown as a liability
Asset A/c method:
(a) Asset A/c Dr / To Bank A/c — purchase
(b) Depreciation A/c Dr / To Asset A/c — yearly charge
(c) P&L A/c Dr / To Depreciation A/c — closing
(d) Bank A/c Dr, P&L A/c Dr (if loss) / To Asset A/c — sale
Provision method — only the middle entry changes:
(b) Depreciation A/c Dr / To Provision for Depreciation A/c
📝 Probable Question: Explain the two methods of recording depreciation with journal entries. (6 marks)
Decision Tree: Profit or Loss?
Sale price > Written Down Value at date of sale → PROFIT — credit Profit & Loss A/c
Sale price < Written Down Value at date of sale → LOSS — debit Profit & Loss A/c
Sale price = Written Down Value → No profit, no loss
🟡 Conceptual Understanding Profit/Loss on sale = Sale Proceeds − Written Down Value on the date of sale (NOT original cost). Always bring the asset's book value up to date with depreciation for the part of the year used, before comparing to sale price.

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Depreciation is the allocation of the cost of a fixed asset over its useful life.
  • It is a charge against profit, debited to Profit & Loss Account.
  • SLM charges a fixed amount every year; WDV charges a fixed rate on the reducing balance.
  • Depreciation can be recorded by charging to the asset account or via a provision for depreciation account.
  • Companies Act 2013, Sec 123(1) mandates depreciation before dividend declaration.
