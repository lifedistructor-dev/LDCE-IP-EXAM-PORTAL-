Why Special Purpose Books (Subsidiary Books)?

🟡 Conceptual Understanding

As a business grows, journalising every transaction becomes cumbersome. Repetitive transactions are grouped into special journals (day books) — economical and enables division of labour.

4.1

Cash Book

🔴 Very Important — practical numerical every year
Cash Book: records all cash receipts & payments. Serves as both a Journal (original entry) AND a Ledger (cash account) — so no separate Cash A/c is opened in the ledger when a cash book is kept.

4.1.2 Double Column (Cash + Bank) Cash Book

Contra Entry — when cash is deposited into bank or withdrawn from bank, BOTH aspects appear in the cash book itself (cash column + bank column). Marked 'C' in the L.F. column and not posted to the ledger.
SituationTreatment
Cash deposited into bankBank column (Dr./receipt side) + Cash column (Cr./payment side) — Contra 'C'
Cash withdrawn from bank (self)Cash column (Dr.) + Bank column (Cr.) — Contra 'C'
Cheque received & deposited same dayBank column, receipt side
Cheque received, deposited laterCash column on receipt; contra entry on the day of deposit
Cheque dishonouredCredit side, Bank column (reverses the earlier receipt)
Bank overdraftBank column shows a credit balance (withdrawals > deposits)

4.1.3

Petty Cash Book & the Imprest System

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Imprest System: a fixed sum (e.g. ₹2,000) is advanced to the petty cashier at the start of a period. As it's spent on small repetitive expenses (postage, conveyance, stationery, etc.), the cashier is reimbursed exactly the amount spent — restoring the full imprest amount for the next period.

Advantages of Petty Cash Book

  • Saves chief cashier's time — he focuses only on large transactions
  • Effective control — division of work reduces fraud risk
  • Convenient recording — keeps the main cash book from becoming bulky (Materiality principle)

Q: Who prepares the note — buyer (us) or seller?
Debit Note — WE prepare, sent to our supplier when WE return goods (purchases return)
Credit Note — WE prepare, sent to our customer when THEY return goods (sales return)

4.6

Journal Proper (Journal Residual)

🔴 Very Important — "which entries go where" MCQs
Records everything that does NOT fit any special journal.
Journal Proper Records
Opening Entries
  • New year's opening balances
Adjustment Entries
  • Outstanding rent, prepaid insurance, depreciation, advance income
Rectification Entries
  • Correcting recording/posting errors
Transfer / Closing Entries
  • Drawings → Capital
  • Trading & P&L transfers
Other Entries
  • Goods withdrawn for personal use
  • Goods given as free samples
  • Loss by fire/theft
  • Credit purchase/sale of assets (not goods)
  • Bills of exchange, consignment

4.7

Balancing the Accounts

🟠 Frequently Asked
Balancing: totalling both sides of an account and inserting the difference on the shorter side (as "Balance c/d") to make both sides equal — then bringing it down as "Balance b/d" for the next period.

🔴 Quick-fire revision

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
"C for Contra, C for Cancelled (from ledger)"
A contra entry is marked 'C' in the L.F. column and is NEVER posted to the ledger — both effects already sit inside the cash book itself.
Debit Note = "Debit note leaves with us"
WE issue a Debit Note when returning goods to our supplier (Purchases Return); a Credit Note is issued BY US when our customer returns goods to us (Sales Return).
Imprest = "I'm-prest with a Fixed Amount"
Petty cashier always starts each period with the SAME fixed imprest sum — reimbursement exactly replaces what was spent.
"OART C" for Journal Proper
Opening, Adjustment, Rectification, Transfer/closing, Compound/other entries — the five buckets of Journal Proper.
Purchases/Sales Book = "Goods on Credit only"
Cash purchases/sales NEVER go in these books — only in the Cash Book. Purchases/Sales books record only credit transactions of goods (not fixed assets).

Last-Day Revision Notes

🔴 Read this 30 minutes before the exam
  1. 01Cash Book = Journal + Ledger in one — no separate Cash/Bank A/c opened in ledger.
  2. 02Contra entry = both aspects inside cash book, marked 'C', never posted to ledger.
  3. 03Petty Cash uses the Imprest System — reimbursement = exact amount spent.
  4. 04Purchases/Sales Books record ONLY credit transactions of GOODS (not assets, not cash).
  5. 05Debit Note → issued by buyer for purchases return; Credit Note → issued by seller for sales return.
  6. 06Journal Proper = catch-all: opening, adjustment, rectification, transfer/closing, and "other" entries.
  7. 07Balancing = insert difference on shorter side as "Balance c/d"; bring down as "Balance b/d".
  8. 08Bank overdraft = credit balance in the bank column (withdrawals exceed deposits).
  9. 09Cheque received but not yet deposited → recorded in Cash column first, contra entry on deposit day.
  10. 10Dishonoured cheque → credit side, Bank column, customer's name in particulars.
