Sec 2(a)

DIRECTOR GENERAL (DG)

केंद्र सरकार द्वारा नियुक्त डाक सेवाओं के महानिदेशक; महानिदेशक के कर्तव्य निभाने हेतु प्राधिकृत कोई भी अधिकारी भी शामिल
DG of Postal Services appointed by Central Govt; includes any officer authorised to perform DG's duties

Rule🟠 Frequently Asked
Sec 3

SERVICES TO BE PROVIDED

કેન્દ્ર સરકાર નિર્ધારિત કરે તેવી સેવાઓ ટપાલ ઓફિસ આપશે; નિર્દેશક જનરલ પ્રવૃત્તિઓ અને શુલ્ક માટે વિનિયમો બનાવી શકે; સેવા અન્ય કાયદાને આધીન
केंद्र सरकार द्वारा निर्धारित सेवाएं डाकघर प्रदान करेगा; महानिदेशक गतिविधियों व शुल्क हेतु विनियम बना सकते हैं; सेवा अन्य कानून के अधीन
Post Office provides services as Central Govt prescribes; DG makes regulations for activities & charges; service subject to other laws
Rule🔴 Very Important
Sec 4

EXCLUSIVE PRIVILEGE — POSTAGE STAMPS

ટપાલ ઓફિસને ટપાલ સ્ટેમ્પ જારી કરવાનો વિશિષ્ટ અધિકાર છે (ભૌતિક અથવા ડિજિટલ); નિર્દેશક જનરલ સ્ટેમ્પ/ટપાલ સ્ટેશનરીના સપ્લાય-વેચાણ માટે વિનિયમો બનાવે
डाकघर को डाक टिकट जारी करने का विशिष्ट अधिकार है (भौतिक या डिजिटल); महानिदेशक टिकट/डाक स्टेशनरी की आपूर्ति-बिक्री हेतु विनियम बनाते हैं
Post Office has EXCLUSIVE privilege of issuing postage stamps (physical OR digital); DG regulates supply/sale of stamps & postal stationery
🎯 Q: Is the exclusive privilege under the 2023 Act as wide as under the 1898 Act? (Ans: No — narrowed to ONLY postage stamps; see Comparison section)
Definition🟡 Conceptual
Sec 4(3)

"POSTAGE STAMP" & "POSTAL STATIONERY"

ટપાલ સ્ટેમ્પ = ચુકવણી દર્શાવતી કોઈપણ સ્ટેમ્પ (ભૌતિક/ડિજિટલ), વસ્તુ પર લગાવેલ/છાપેલ/એમ્બોસ કરેલ; ટપાલ સ્ટેશનરી = પ્રીપેડ સ્ટેમ્પવાળા પરબિડીયા, પોસ્ટકાર્ડ વગેરે
डाक टिकट = भुगतान दर्शाने वाला कोई भी टिकट (भौतिक/डिजिटल), वस्तु पर लगा/मुद्रित/उभरा हुआ; डाक स्टेशनरी = प्रीपेड टिकट वाले लिफाफे, पोस्टकार्ड आदि
Postage stamp = any stamp (physical/digital) denoting payment, affixed/printed/embossed on an item; Postal stationery = pre-stamped envelopes, postcards, letter cards
Rule🟢 Quick Revision
Sec 5

ADDRESSES & POSTCODES

કેન્દ્ર સરકાર સરનામા, સરનામા ઓળખકર્તા અને પોસ્ટકોડના ઉપયોગ માટે ધારાધોરણો નિર્ધારિત કરી શકે
केंद्र सरकार पता, पता पहचानकर्ता एवं पोस्टकोड के उपयोग हेतु मानक निर्धारित कर सकती है
Central Govt may prescribe standards for addressing, address identifiers and use of postcodes

છેલ્લા દિવસની નોંધ
अंतिम दिन के नोट्स
Last-Day Revision Notes

📌 If you read nothing else, read this:

  • It's Act No. 43 of 2023, assented 24 Dec 2023, but came into force only on 18 June 2024 — examiners love this gap.
  • It repeals the Indian Post Office Act, 1898 — and has only 16 sections (vs the long old Act).
  • Exclusive privilege = postage stamps ONLY (physical or digital) — NOT a broad mail monopoly.
  • No penalty/offence chapter exists in this Act — don't get tricked into inventing a fine amount.
  • Unpaid charges are recovered as arrear of land revenue (Sec 7) — civil remedy, not criminal.
  • Central Government = Rules (Sec 12); Director General = Regulations (Sec 13, needs CG's prior approval).
  • DG can receive delegated powers from CG — except rule-making (Sec 11).
  • Interception (Sec 9) needs ONE of 6 grounds: Security / Foreign relations / Public order / Emergency / Public safety / Contravention of law.
  • Officer liable only if fraudulent or wilful (Sec 10(2)); Post Office liable only "as prescribed" (Sec 10(1)).
  • Difficulty-removal orders (Sec 15) cannot be issued after 2 years from commencement.
  • Rules/Regulations must be laid before Parliament for 30 days (Sec 14).

સામાન્ય પ્રશ્નો
सामान्य प्रश्न
Frequently Asked Questions
Q. What replaced the Indian Post Office Act, 1898?
The Post Office Act, 2023 (Act No. 43 of 2023), which repeals the 1898 Act under Section 16.
Q. When did the new Act actually start operating?
18 June 2024 — NOT the assent date (24 Dec 2023). Commencement was fixed by notification S.O. 2352(E) dated 17 June 2024.
Q. Does the Post Office still have a broad monopoly over carrying letters?
No — under Sec 4 of the 2023 Act, the exclusive privilege is limited to issuing postage stamps, narrower than the wide monopoly historically associated with the 1898 regime.
Q. Can postage stamps be digital?
Yes — Sec 4(3)(a) explicitly allows postage stamps "in any form, physical or digital."
Q. Who decides what services the Post Office offers?
The Central Government, by rules (Sec 3(1)). The DG only handles operational regulations and charges (Sec 3(2)).
Q. Is there a penalty section in this Act?
No dedicated offences/penalty chapter. The only financial remedy mentioned is civil recovery of unpaid charges as an arrear of land revenue (Sec 7).
Q. Can the Director General make Rules?
No — the DG can only make Regulations (Sec 13), and only with the Central Government's prior approval. Sec 11 expressly excludes rule-making from powers delegable to the DG.
Q. What happen to old Rules made under the 1898 Act?
They continue in force (Sec 16(2) savings clause) as long as they're consistent with the 2023 Act, until superseded by new rules/notifications/orders made under it.
