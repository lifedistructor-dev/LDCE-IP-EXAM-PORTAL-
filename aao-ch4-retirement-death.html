🧾 4.1 Amount Due to Retiring/Deceased Partner 🔴

Definition: The sum finally payable to the outgoing partner (retiring) or his legal representatives/executors (deceased), after all adjustments.

➕ Additions (Credits)

  • Credit balance of Capital A/c (+ Current A/c)
  • Share of Goodwill
  • Share of Accumulated Profits (reserves)
  • Share of gain on Revaluation
  • Share of profit up to date of retirement/death
  • Interest on capital (if applicable, up to that date)
  • Salary/commission due up to that date

➖ Deductions

  • Debit balance of Current A/c
  • Share of goodwill written off (if any)
  • Share of accumulated losses
  • Share of loss on revaluation
  • Share of loss up to that date; drawings; interest on drawings

8 Accounting Aspects Involved 🔴 EXAM FAVOURITE LIST

  1. New Profit Sharing Ratio & Gaining Ratio
  2. Treatment of Goodwill
  3. Revaluation of Assets & Liabilities
  4. Unrecorded Assets/Liabilities adjustment
  5. Distribution of Accumulated Profits/Losses
  6. Share of Profit/Loss up to date of retirement/death
  7. Adjustment of Capital (if required)
  8. Settlement of Amount Due
🧠 Mnemonic — "RGRUASC-S": Ratio-Gaining, Goodwill, Revaluation, Unrecorded items, Accumulated P&L, Share of profit, Capital adj, Settlement
📝 Probable Q: "What items constitute the amount due to a retiring partner?" / "List the accounting aspects involved in retirement of a partner." — Guaranteed short-answer.

🧮 4.2 New Profit Sharing Ratio 🔴

Ratio in which remaining partners share future profits after retirement/death.
New Share = Old Share + Share Acquired from Outgoing Partner

3 Situations

  1. No info given → remaining partners acquire in their OLD ratio among themselves (default assumption)
  2. Acquisition ratio specified (other than old ratio) → calculate new share using the formula above
  3. New ratio directly specified → just use it as given
📝 Probable Q: "A, B, C share profits 5:3:2. B retires; A and C acquire his share 2:1. Find new profit sharing ratio." — Standard opening calc in nearly every numerical.

📈 4.3 Gaining Ratio 🔴

Definition: The ratio in which continuing partners acquire the share of the retiring/deceased partner.
Gaining Ratio = New Share − Old Share (for each continuing partner)
  • Needed for calculation ONLY when the new PSR of continuing partners is directly specified
  • If they acquire in old ratio → Gaining Ratio = Old Ratio (no calculation needed)
  • Used to distribute the goodwill compensation burden among gaining partners

⚖️ Gaining Ratio vs Sacrificing Ratio

BasisGaining RatioSacrificing Ratio
MeaningRatio of gain in future profit shareRatio of loss/sacrifice in profit share
FormulaNew Share − Old ShareOld Share − New Share
When usedRetirement / DeathAdmission
Who benefitsContinuing (remaining) partnersIncoming new partner
🧠 Mnemonic — "Gain=New−Old (Retirement); Sacrifice=Old−New (Admission)"
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed): "Distinguish between Gaining Ratio and Sacrificing Ratio." — Direct repeat of the "Do it Yourself" exercise; asked almost every year.

💎 4.4 Treatment of Goodwill 🔴 MOST TESTED TOPIC

Retiring/deceased partner is compensated for his share of goodwill by the gaining partners, in their gaining ratio — because gaining partners benefit from the increased future profit share.

4.4.1 When Goodwill Does NOT Appear in Books — 4 Methods 🔴

Goodwill A/c Dr. (full value) To All Partners' Capital A/cs (incl. retiring/deceased) — OLD ratio

Goodwill stays in the Balance Sheet of reconstituted firm.

Step 1: Goodwill A/c Dr. (full value) → To All Partners' Capital A/cs — OLD ratio Step 2: Remaining Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. → To Goodwill A/c — NEW ratio
Step 1: Goodwill A/c Dr. (retiring partner's share only) → To Retiring Partner's Capital A/c Step 2: Remaining Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. → To Goodwill A/c — GAINING ratio
Continuing Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. (in GAINING ratio) To Retiring/Deceased Partner's Capital A/c

Simplest & most frequently used method in exam numericals.

4.4.2 When Goodwill Already Appears in Books 🟠

Written off by debiting all partners' capital A/cs (including retiring/deceased) in their old ratio, then re-raised/adjusted per the required method above.

🧠 Mnemonic — "Raise = Old ratio; Write-off = New/Gaining ratio": Whenever goodwill is CREDITED to partners (raised), it's in OLD ratio (everyone contributed to it). Whenever it's DEBITED (written off/adjusted), it's in NEW or GAINING ratio (only those who benefit going forward pay).
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed): "Pass journal entries for treatment of goodwill on retirement" under all 4 alternatives — very frequently tested, sometimes as MCQ too.

🔍 4.5 Revaluation of Assets & Liabilities 🔴

Same process as in admission — Revaluation Account prepared for gain/loss on asset/liability revaluation and unrecorded items. KEY DIFFERENCE from Admission: net gain/loss is shared among ALL partners INCLUDING the retiring/deceased partner, in the OLD profit sharing ratio (since it relates to the period before reconstitution, and all partners were part of the firm then).
EventEntry
Increase in AssetAsset Dr. → To Revaluation (Gain)
Decrease in AssetRevaluation Dr. → To Asset (Loss)
Increase in LiabilityRevaluation Dr. → To Liability (Loss)
Decrease in LiabilityLiability Dr. → To Revaluation (Gain)
Unrecorded AssetAsset Dr. → To Revaluation (Gain)
Unrecorded LiabilityRevaluation Dr. → To Liability (Loss)
Net Gain/Loss on Revaluation → transferred to ALL Partners' Capital A/cs (old ratio) — including outgoing partner

📦 4.6 Accumulated Profits & Losses 🟠

Reserves/P&L balance belong to ALL partners (old ratio) — including outgoing partner.
Reserves A/c Dr. → To All Partners' Capital A/cs (old ratio) [if reserve/profit] All Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. (old ratio) → To P&L A/c [if accumulated loss]
📝 Probable Q: Journal entry for transfer of General Reserve on retirement — standard 2-mark sub-part.

💵 4.7 Disposal of Amount Due to Retiring Partner 🔴

Section 37, Indian Partnership Act 1932: If no agreement on settlement mode, the outgoing partner may choose EITHER (a) interest @ 6% p.a. till payment, OR (b) a share of profits earned using his money (based on capital ratio).
How is retiring partner settled?
Full cash immediately:
Retiring Partner's Capital A/c Dr. → To Cash/Bank A/c
Treated as Loan:
Retiring Partner's Capital A/c Dr. → To Retiring Partner's Loan A/c

Partly cash + partly loan:

Retiring Partner's Capital A/c Dr. (Total Amount Due) To Cash/Bank A/c (Amount Paid) To Retiring Partner's Loan A/c (Balance)

Instalment payment (principal + interest):

(a) Interest A/c Dr. → To Retiring Partner's Loan A/c (b) Retiring Partner's Loan A/c Dr. → To Cash/Bank A/c

*Note: Loan balance shown on Liabilities side of Balance Sheet until fully paid.

📝 Probable Q: Numerical — "Prepare Retiring Partner's Loan Account showing instalment payments with interest." — Common 4-6 mark question.

💰 4.8 Adjustment of Partners' Capitals 🟡

Remaining partners may adjust capitals to be proportionate to the new PSR. Sum of continuing partners' capitals = total capital of new firm (unless specified). Then divide as per new PSR to find each partner's required capital.

Excess Capital → Partners' Capital A/c Dr. → To Cash/Bank A/c (withdrawn) Capital Shortfall → Cash/Bank A/c Dr. → To Partners' Capital A/c (brought in)

⚰️ 4.9 Death of a Partner 🔴 UNIQUE TO THIS SECTION

Accounting treatment is similar to retirement — claim goes to the deceased partner's Executors, settled the same way. Key difference: Death can occur any time during the year (not just at year-end) — so the deceased partner's claim must include profit/loss, interest on capital/drawings for the intervening period (from last Balance Sheet date to date of death).

Calculating Deceased Partner's Share of Profit (Intervening Period) 🔴 UNIQUE NUMERICAL

Since closing books mid-year is impractical, profit for the intervening period is estimated by 3 methods:

Deceased's Share = Last Year's Profit × (Period/12) × His Share
Average Profit = Total Profit of past years / No. of years Deceased's Share = Average Profit × (Period/12) × His Share
Profit Rate = Last Year's Profit / Last Year's Sales Estimated Profit (intervening period) = Profit Rate × Sales in Intervening Period Deceased's Share = Estimated Profit × His Share

Journal Entry

Profit & Loss Suspense A/c Dr. To Deceased Partner's Capital A/c (Share of profit for intervening period)
🧠 Mnemonic — "Death = Retirement + Time-Slice Profit": Same steps as retirement, PLUS calculate intervening-period profit using Last Year / Average / Sales-basis method, credited via P&L Suspense A/c.
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed): "X died on [mid-year date]. Calculate his share of profit for the period... and pass the journal entry." Also: "Prepare deceased partner's Executor's Account." — Very high frequency, unique to this topic.

⏱️ Last-Minute Memory Tricks

📌 "Gain = New − Old; Sacrifice = Old − New" — never confuse retirement (gaining) with admission (sacrificing).
📌 "Credit Goodwill = Old ratio; Debit Goodwill = New/Gaining ratio"
📌 "Death = Retirement + Time Slice" — remember P&L Suspense A/c for the extra step unique to death cases.
