BO Inspection દરમિયાન Cash Shortage મળતાં કાર્યવાહી અંગે Note તૈયાર કરો.
▼
Subject: Cash shortage detected during inspection of Branch Office – action to be taken During the periodical inspection of the Branch Office conducted on —, a shortage of Rs. — was noticed in the cash balance maintained by the Branch Postmaster (BPM), as verified against the cash book and physical cash count. The shortage was pointed out to the BPM on the spot and his explanation was obtained in writing. As per Rule 33 of the General Financial Rules (GFR), 2017, any loss or shortage of public money, irrespective of the cause, must be immediately reported by the subordinate authority to the next higher authority and the Statutory Audit Officer, even if the amount is subsequently made good. As per Financial Handbook Volume I provisions on cash accountal, the shortage must be recovered from the official concerned immediately, and where recovery is not possible on the spot, the amount may be ordered for recovery from salary in suitable instalments with the approval of the competent authority. Simultaneously, a preliminary enquiry report is required to be submitted to the Divisional Office within three days, along with the BPM's explanation. It is proposed that: (i) recovery proceedings be initiated against the BPM under the extant financial rules, (ii) the matter be reported to the Superintendent of Post Offices for further disciplinary action if warranted, and (iii) surprise cash verification of the BO be intensified for the next two quarters. Submitted for approval of the Sub Divisional Inspector.

Subject: Persistent delay in delivery of mail by Postman – corrective action Repeated complaints have been received from the public regarding delay in delivery of ordinary and registered articles in the beat assigned to Postman Shri —. On scrutiny of the delivery slips and beat register, it is observed that articles are being delivered two to three days beyond the prescribed norm, contrary to the Postal Manual Volume VI provisions on delivery norms. The Postman was called for explanation, and it is stated that the delay was caused due to an increase in the number of delivery points without a corresponding review of the beat. However, similar delays have occurred in the past despite prior warning, indicating a pattern of negligence. It is proposed that: (i) a formal warning be issued to the Postman under the CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965, with a copy placed in his service record, (ii) the beat be re-surveyed and re-distributed if genuinely overloaded, and (iii) daily delivery performance be monitored by the Sub Postmaster for the next one month. Continued default may attract disciplinary proceedings. Submitted for orders of the Assistant Superintendent of Post Offices.

As per the Post Office Savings Bank Manual and POSB (CBS) procedural guidelines, all such discrepancies must be reconciled through the CBS back-office within seven working days of receipt of complaint, with the transaction log cross-checked against the physical voucher. It is proposed that the reconciliation report be obtained and the complaint closed accordingly, with a copy endorsed to the Divisional Office. Submitted for approval.

As a result, the daily bag closing time is being missed, leading to onward transmission delays. As per extant instructions, mail processing work is time-bound and any delay directly affects the delivery performance and Citizens' Charter compliance of the Division. The matter was discussed with the available staff, and it is noted that the existing strength is insufficient to handle the current volume within the prescribed hours even with maximum effort. It is proposed that: (i) the vacant posts be filled on priority through transfer/promotion or engagement of substitute staff, (ii) until regular filling, staff may be temporarily diverted from counter duties during non-peak hours to assist in sorting, and (iii) overtime allowance be sanctioned for the interim period to clear the backlog. The matter is submitted for the kind consideration and orders of the Divisional Superintendent, with a request for early action to avoid further disruption of service.

Subject: Suspected fraudulent transaction in Savings Bank account – investigation It has come to notice that a withdrawal of Rs. — was made from SB Account No. — using a withdrawal form bearing a signature that does not match the specimen signature on record. The depositor has denied having made or authorized the withdrawal, raising a strong suspicion of fraud. As per Rule 33 of the General Financial Rules (GFR), 2017, any loss or shortage of public money must be immediately reported to the next higher authority, irrespective of the cause or manner of detection. Further, as per Rule 34 of GFR 2017, losses exceeding Rs. 50,000 arising from suspected fraud must invariably be reported to the Police for investigation. The withdrawal form, specimen signature card, and CCTV footage, if available, are to be preserved as evidence. It is proposed that: (i) the SB account be frozen with immediate effect and the depositor informed, (ii) the matter be reported to the Superintendent of Post Offices and the Vigilance Wing for detailed investigation, (iii) a First Information Report be lodged with the local police under Rule 34 of GFR 2017 as fraud is prima facie established, and (iv) the staff who processed the transaction be examined for possible complicity or negligence. Submitted for urgent orders of the competent authority.

Leave Regularization માટે મળેલી અરજી અંગે Note તૈયાર કરો.
▼
Subject: Application for regularization of leave Shri/Smt. — remained absent from duty from — to — without prior sanction of leave due to sudden illness, and has now submitted an application along with a medical certificate requesting that the period be regularized as Earned Leave/Medical Leave, as admissible under the CCS (Leave) Rules, 1972. On verification, it is seen that the medical certificate is issued by an authorized medical practitioner and the leave account of the official shows sufficient balance to cover the period of absence. As per Rule 25 of the CCS (Leave) Rules, 1972, a Government servant who remains absent after the expiry of leave is treated as willfully absent and is entitled to no leave salary for that period unless the leave is extended or the absence is regularized by the competent authority. As per Rule 32(2)(a) of the same Rules, the disciplinary authority is required to proceed against such absence without waiting for it to prolong. Since the absence is satisfactorily explained and supported by valid medical evidence, it may be regularized as leave of the kind due and admissible, at the discretion of the competent authority. It is proposed that: (i) the period of absence from — to — be regularized as Medical Leave/Earned Leave as applicable, debiting the leave account accordingly, and (ii) the official be advised to obtain prior sanction for leave in future save in genuine emergencies. The case is submitted for approval of the competent authority, and the leave account may be updated accordingly upon sanction.
