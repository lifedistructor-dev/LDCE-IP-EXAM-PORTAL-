1 Topic & Definition 🔴 VIP

Topic: A company raises long-term capital mainly through Shares (ownership capital) and Debentures (debt capital). This chapter covers accounting for Share Capital — issue, calls, forfeiture and reissue of shares.

Definition — Company: Per Chief Justice Marshall — a company is "a person, artificial, invisible, intangible and existing only in the eyes of law." It is registered under the Companies Act, 2013.

Definition — Share: A share is a fractional unit into which the total share capital of a company is divided; it is the basis of ownership interest.

Likely Q: "Define a company. State its main features." / "What is a share? Distinguish it from a debenture."

2 Mind Map — Share Capital Categories 🟡 Conceptual

AUTHORISED CAPITAL Max capital as per Memorandum of Association (also: Nominal/Registered Capital)
▼ (part offered to public)
ISSUED CAPITAL Part of authorised capital actually offered for subscription
▼ (part actually taken up)
SUBSCRIBED CAPITAL Part of issued capital actually subscribed by public
▼ (part demanded so far)
CALLED-UP CAPITAL Part of face value the company has actually called/demanded
▼ (minus Calls-in-Arrears)
PAID-UP CAPITAL Called-up Capital − Calls-in-Arrears = amount actually received

Uncalled Capital = Subscribed − Called-up. Reserve Capital = portion of uncalled capital kept back, callable only on winding-up.

3.1 Decision Tree — Over-Subscription Handling 🟠 Frequently Asked

Applications received > Shares offered → Over-Subscription. What do directors do?
Option 1: Full RejectionAccept some applications in full, reject others fully; refund rejected money in full.
Option 2: Pro-Rata AllotmentAllot proportionately to all; excess application money adjusted towards allotment due.
Option 3: CombinationPartly reject + partly pro-rata — the most common practice.

Under-Subscription: Applications < shares offered. Allotment possible only if Minimum Subscription (90%) is met.

Likely Q: "What alternatives are available to directors in case of over-subscription of shares? Explain with journal entries."

5 Important Rules (Table F / SEBI) 🔴 VIP

RuleLimit
Minimum Application Money≥ 5% of face value of share
Minimum Subscription≥ 90% of the issued amount (SEBI DIP Guidelines 2000)
Gap between two callsAt least 1 month (unless Articles say otherwise)
Amount per callNot more than 25% of face value of share
Notice period for a callMinimum 14 days' notice to shareholders
Calls must be madeOn a uniform basis on all shares of same class
Mnemonic — "5-25-1-14": 5% min. application → 25% max. per call → 1 month gap between calls → 14 days' notice.

6 Important Exceptions 🟡 Conceptual

  • A company cannot ordinarily issue shares at a discount — EXCEPT: (a) reissue of forfeited shares, and (b) issue of sweat equity shares.
  • Discount on reissue of forfeited shares cannot exceed the amount originally forfeited (received) on those shares.
  • Unlimited companies are legally permitted under the Companies Act but not found in practice in India.
  • Where Articles are silent on interest for calls-in-arrears/advance, Table F rates automatically apply — otherwise no interest need be charged at all if nothing is specified.
  • Securities Premium is NOT debited at forfeiture if premium was already fully received — only debited if premium is still outstanding.
Likely Q: "Under what circumstances can a company issue shares at a discount?"

10 Comparison Charts 🟠 Frequently Asked

Equity Shares vs Preference Shares

Equity Shares

  • No fixed/preferential dividend
  • Paid after preference shareholders
  • Voting rights (generally)
  • No preferential capital repayment

Preference Shares

  • Fixed/preferential dividend rate
  • Paid before equity shareholders
  • Preferential repayment on winding up
  • Can be cumulative/non-cumulative, participating/non-participating, redeemable/irredeemable

Calls-in-Arrears vs Calls-in-Advance

Calls-in-Arrears

  • Shareholder has NOT paid a due call
  • It's an asset-side deduction (debit balance)
  • Company MAY charge interest (max 10% p.a.)
  • Can lead to forfeiture

Calls-in-Advance

  • Shareholder has PAID before it was called
  • It's a liability (current liability in Balance Sheet)
  • Company MAY pay interest (max 12% p.a.)
  • Not part of paid-up capital
Likely Q: "Distinguish between Equity Shares and Preference Shares." / "Differentiate Calls-in-Arrears from Calls-in-Advance."

11 Journal Entries — Ledger Cheat-Sheet 🔴 VIP

1. On Application ▶
Bank A/c Dr. To Share Application A/c (Application money received on ___ shares @ Rs.___)
2. On Allotment (5 entries) ▶
Share Application A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c (Transfer of application money to capital) Share Application A/c Dr. To Bank A/c (Refund on rejected applications) Share Allotment A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c (Amount due on allotment) Share Application A/c Dr. To Share Allotment A/c (Excess application money adjusted to allotment) Bank A/c Dr. To Share Allotment A/c (Allotment money received)
3. On Calls ▶
Share Call A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c (Call money due on ___ shares @ Rs.___) Bank A/c Dr. To Share Call A/c (Call money received)
4. Calls-in-Arrears / Calls-in-Advance ▶
Bank A/c Dr. Calls-in-Arrears A/c Dr. To Share Call A/c (Call received except on ___ shares) Bank A/c Dr. To Calls-in-Advance A/c (Amount received before call was due) Calls-in-Advance A/c Dr. To Particular Call A/c (Advance adjusted against call due)
5. Issue at Premium ▶
Bank A/c / Share Allotment A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c (face value) To Securities Premium Reserve A/c (premium)
6. Forfeiture of Shares (issued at par) ▶
Share Capital A/c (called-up amt) Dr. To Share Forfeiture A/c (paid-up amt) To Share Allotment A/c (unpaid) To Share Calls A/c (unpaid, individually)
7. Forfeiture (premium not received) ▶
Share Capital A/c Dr. Securities Premium Reserve A/c Dr. To Share Forfeiture A/c To Share Allotment/Calls A/c
8. Reissue of Forfeited Shares ▶
Bank A/c Dr. Share Forfeiture A/c (discount) Dr. To Share Capital A/c Share Forfeiture A/c (profit bal.) Dr. To Capital Reserve A/c (Profit on reissue transferred)
Likely Q: Every board exam/AAO paper has at least one full numerical on "Journalise the transactions" covering application → allotment → calls → forfeiture → reissue. Practice the full chain, not entries in isolation.

12 Forfeiture & Reissue — Worked Logic 🟠 Frequently Asked

Shareholder defaults on call money
▼
Directors forfeit shares (per Table F/Articles) Share Capital debited (called-up amt); unpaid calls credited back; balance → Share Forfeiture A/c
▼
Reissue decided — at par / premium / discount Discount allowed ≤ amount already forfeited on those shares
▼
Balance left in Share Forfeiture A/c (for reissued shares only) → transferred to Capital Reserve A/c

Key Rule: Only the PROPORTIONATE profit on shares actually reissued goes to Capital Reserve — not the whole Share Forfeiture balance, if only part of the forfeited shares were reissued.

Likely Q: Numerical: "200 shares forfeited, 150 reissued at discount — pass entries and find amount transferred to Capital Reserve." (classic proportion-based question)

13 Memory Tricks & Mnemonics 🟢 Quick Revision

Share Capital flow: "Aunty Is Singing Carols Peacefully" → Authorised → Issued → Subscribed → Called-up → Paid-up.
Share issue stages: "Apple Ate Cake" → Application → Allotment → Calls.
Interest rates: "10 before, 12 after" — Arrears (before company gets money) = 10%; Advance (after, company owes) = 12%.
Rules number set: 5-25-1-14 (application%-call%-month gap-notice days).
Discount exception: "Re-issue & Sweat" — only Reissue of forfeited shares and Sweat equity shares can be issued at discount.

14 One-Page Revision Sheet 🟢 Quick Revision

Capital Flow

  • Authorised → Issued → Subscribed → Called-up → Paid-up

Key %'s

  • Min. Application: 5% | Max Call: 25% | Min. Subscription: 90%

Key Days

  • Min. subscription: 120 days | Refund if not met: 130 days | Grace before interest: 8 days

Interest Rates (Table F max)

  • Calls-in-Arrears: 10% | Calls-in-Advance: 12% | Refund delay [Sec 73(2)]: 15%

Shares at Discount — only for

  • Reissue of forfeited shares | Sweat equity shares

Forfeiture Formula

  • Share Capital (called-up) Dr. → Forfeiture A/c (paid-up) + Allotment/Calls (unpaid)

Reissue Profit

  • Balance in Forfeiture A/c (for reissued shares only) → Capital Reserve

15 Last-Day Revision Notes 🟢 Quick Revision

✓ Companies Act, 2013 — Sec 43 defines equity & preference shares.
✓ OPC: only Indian resident citizen; paid-up ≤ ₹50L; turnover ≤ ₹2Cr (3-yr avg).
✓ Minimum subscription = 90% of issue; must come within 120 days; refund within 130 days.
✓ Application money ≥ 5% of face value.
✓ Call ≤ 25% of face value; 1 month gap; 14 days' notice.
✓ Calls-in-Arrears interest max 10%; Calls-in-Advance interest max 12%.
✓ Shares at discount allowed ONLY on reissue of forfeited shares & sweat equity.
✓ Forfeiture: Share Capital Dr. (called-up) → credit unpaid Allotment/Calls + Forfeiture A/c (paid-up).
✓ On reissue: discount debited to Forfeiture A/c ≤ amount originally forfeited.
✓ Profit on reissue (proportionate only) → transferred to Capital Reserve.
✓ Securities Premium NOT touched at forfeiture if premium already received in full.
✓ Delay in refund beyond 8 days → 15% p.a. interest [Sec 73(2)].
