Grades of Government Servants 🟠 FREQUENT

सरकारी कर्मचारियों की श्रेणियाँ — Pay Level निर्धारण का आधार

Chapter → Sub-Chapter → Micro:
TA Rules → Grades → Pay-Level Determination → Transit/Attendant Rules

📖 Definition / परिभाषा 🔴
English

A Government servant's entitlement for Travelling Allowance purposes is based on the Pay Level of the post currently held — irrespective of whether it is held substantively, temporarily, in an officiating capacity, or on ad hoc basis. Daily-rated staff, piece-workers, casual labourers, and unskilled staff brought onto monthly rates are treated at par with regular staff.

हिंदी

यात्रा भत्ते के प्रयोजन हेतु किसी सरकारी कर्मचारी की पात्रता उस पद के पे लेवल पर आधारित होती है जिसे वह वर्तमान में धारण कर रहा है — चाहे वह स्थायी, अस्थायी, कार्यवाहक या तदर्थ आधार पर हो। दैनिक वेतनभोगी, टुकड़ा-श्रमिक, आकस्मिक श्रमिक और मासिक दर पर लाए गए अकुशल कर्मचारी नियमित कर्मचारियों के समान माने जाते हैं।

Probable Q: What allowance is admissible to an attendant accompanying a disabled Government servant on tour, and what is NOT admissible?
Ans: Mileage Allowance admissible; Daily Allowance NOT admissible.
⚠️ Important Exceptions 🟡
  • Daily-rated/piece-worker/casual/unskilled staff brought to monthly rates → treated as regular staff for grade purposes (exception to the general assumption that only regular staff counts).
  • In-transit rule is an exception to "current post" rule — here the LOWER of two grades applies, not the current one.
🧠 Mnemonic: "Grade follows the Post, Transit follows the Lower, Attendant gets only Mileage" — three quick rules to remember for Grades chapter.

Different Kinds of Travelling Allowance 🔴 VERY IMP

यात्रा भत्ते के प्रकार — SR Rule 21

SR Rule-21: 5 Kinds of Travelling Allowance
↓
1. Permanent TA
2. Conveyance/Horse Allowance
3. Mileage Allowance
4. Daily Allowance
5. Actual Cost of Travelling
📖 Definition 🔴

English: SR Rule-21 lists five kinds of Travelling Allowance that may be drawn by Government servants under different circumstances: Permanent TA, Conveyance/Horse Allowance, Mileage Allowance, Daily Allowance, and Actual Cost of Travelling.

हिंदी: SR नियम-21 सरकारी कर्मचारियों को विभिन्न परिस्थितियों में मिलने वाले पाँच प्रकार के यात्रा भत्ते सूचीबद्ध करता है: स्थायी TA, वाहन/घोड़ा भत्ता, मार्गीय भत्ता, दैनिक भत्ता और यात्रा की वास्तविक लागत।

Probable Q: List the five kinds of Travelling Allowance as per SR Rule-21.
📜 1. Permanent Travelling Allowance 🟠
  • Fixed monthly allowance for Govt. servants whose duties necessitate extensive travel.
  • Covers cost of all journeys within official responsibilities; paid year-round regardless of actual travel.
  • For journeys outside official sphere → normal TA + DA claimable for entire trip (incl. portion inside sphere).
  • 🔴 Not applicable during leave, temporary transfer, or joining time.
  • 🔴 Cannot be claimed concurrently with any other TA type unless specifically authorized. (SR 22, 23 & 24)
Probable Q: During which periods is Permanent Travelling Allowance NOT admissible? Ans: Leave, temporary transfer, joining time.
📜 2. Conveyance or Horse Allowance 🟠
  • For Govt. servants requiring extensive travel at/near headquarters, not qualifying for Daily Allowance.
  • Available year-round; NOT forfeited during absence from HQ (unlike some other TA types).
  • Unlike Permanent TA — can be claimed alongside other TA types.
  • If authorized specifically for motorcycle/motor car maintenance → no mileage allowance for journeys on that vehicle (up to approved limit).
🧠 Mnemonic: "PC-MDA" → Permanent, Conveyance, Mileage, Daily, Actual cost — the 5 kinds of TA under SR-21.

Mileage Allowance & Duty Point 🔴 VERY IMP

मार्गीय भत्ता एवं ड्यूटी प्वाइंट — SR 29 to SR 32, SR 71

📖 Definition — Mileage Allowance 🔴

English: Mileage Allowance (SR 29) is a form of Travelling Allowance calculated based on the distance travelled, intended to cover the cost of a specific journey.

हिंदी: मार्गीय भत्ता (SR 29) यात्रा भत्ते का वह रूप है जो तय की गई दूरी के आधार पर गणना की जाती है, जो किसी विशिष्ट यात्रा की लागत को कवर करने हेतु है।

📜 Principles of Calculation (SR 30-31) 🟠
  • Route Calculation: Based on shortest practicable route; if multiple equally-short routes, choose cheapest. If alternative rail routes don't significantly differ in time/cost, actual route may be used even if slightly longer.
  • Cheaper Routes: If servant travels a cheaper (not shortest) route, mileage = actual route used, ensuring no profit to recipient. (SR 30)
  • Competent Authority Discretion: May allow mileage based on route actually used, with documented reasons. Delegated to all Heads of Departments. (SR 31)
  • Road Mileage for Rail-Connected Journeys: Competent Authority decides full road mileage vs rail-limited amount; may consider public interest (time savings/inspections). (SR 31)
  • Non-Admissibility of Longer Routes: Not permissible if shortest route couldn't be used due to unavailability of reserved accommodation in entitled class. (SR 31)
  • Disruptions in Train Service: Floods/strikes → stations deemed temporarily disconnected from rail → full road mileage claimable without restriction. (SR 31)
Probable Q: If train service is disrupted due to floods/strikes, what mileage can be claimed? Ans: Full road mileage without restriction (stations deemed disconnected from rail).
📜 Duty Point Rules (SR 32) 🔴
  • At Headquarters: the location/office where the Govt. servant performs duties.
  • At Outstation: the location/office visited for work; if multiple points exist, decided by mode of arrival:
  • Arrived by rail/steamer/air → duty point = location farthest from the railway station/harbour/jetty/airport.
  • Arrived by road → duty point = location farthest from where the journey began.
Probable Q: How is the duty point determined at an outstation when a Government servant arrives by air?
Ans: The location farthest from the airport.
📜 Temporary Duty at Headquarters (SR 71) 🟡

When temporary duty is assigned at a location within HQ different from the normal place of work, and the servant need not attend their own office that day → duty point = location where duty is actually performed that day.

Journey Undertaken
↓
Shortest Route Available? → Mileage on shortest route
Cheaper Longer Route Used? → Mileage on actual (no profit)
↓

🧠 Mnemonic: "Farthest from the Gate" — for duty point, always the point farthest from the entry (station/airport/road start).

📜 Sportsmen & Reimbursement Guidelines 🟡
  • Managers/Coaches/Sportspersons in national/international events within India → First Class by train (SR 34), regardless of pay level.

🔑 Key Exam Notes on this Example
  • 🔴 Married daughter not dependent → excluded from "family" definition → her TA is not admissible.
  • 🟠 Son who travelled 6 months later is NOT regarded as "accompanying" the officer — unless competent authority sanctions relaxation of the time-limit.
  • 🟡 Chauffeur allowed only one II Class fare (SR 116(a)(I)(iv)).
  • 🔴 Maid/domestic servant NOT eligible for TA — not included in definition of "family".
  • 🟢 For personal effects: always take the lower of actual expenditure vs. maximum admissible (weight-based cap).
Probable Q: Why is TA not admissible for the married daughter in a transfer TA claim? Ans: She is not dependent on the officer, hence not included in the definition of "family".
Time Limit Rule
Family members must travel within a reasonable period to be regarded as "accompanying" the transferred officer — else competent authority sanction needed for relaxation.
Weight Cap Rule
Personal effects transport reimbursement = lower of (actual freight paid) or (freight for maximum admissible weight by goods train).
🧠 Mnemonic: "D-M-C" → Dependent family only, Married daughter excluded (unless dependent), Chauffeur gets only II Class — remember these 3 traps in every TA numerical.

📄 One-Page Last-Day Revision Sheet

Very Imp Frequent Concept Quick

1️⃣ Grades

  • 🔴 Grade = Pay Level of current post (any tenure type)
  • 🟠 In transit → lower of two grades applies
  • 🟡 Attendant of disabled officer → Mileage only, no DA

2️⃣ Kinds of TA (SR-21)

  • 🔴 5 kinds: Permanent, Conveyance/Horse, Mileage, Daily, Actual Cost
  • 🟠 Permanent TA NOT admissible during leave/temp transfer/joining time

3️⃣ Conveyance Allowance Conditions

  • 🔴 Min 200 km/month; not for foot/bicycle
  • 🟠 Min pay ₹2,800 (except CGHS doctors exempt)
  • 🟢 Non-admissible if vehicle unused/faulty >15 days or tour ≥15 days

4️⃣ Mileage & Duty Point

  • 🔴 Shortest/cheapest route principle; no profit motive
  • 🟠 Rail disruption (flood/strike) → full road mileage, no cap
  • 🟡 Duty point = farthest point from station/airport/road-start

5️⃣ Travel Entitlements

  • 🔴 Domestic: L14+ =Business; L12-13=Eco/AC1; L6-11=Eco/AC2; L≤5=1st/AC3
  • 🔴 International: L17+ =1st; L14-16=Business; L≤13=Economy
  • 🟠 Sportspersons in national events → First Class by train regardless of pay level

6️⃣ Transfer TA Numerical — Traps

  • 🔴 Non-dependent married daughter — no TA
  • 🔴 Domestic servant/maid — no TA (not "family")
  • 🟠 Late-traveling family member (e.g. after 6 months) — not "accompanying" unless relaxation sanctioned
  • 🟢 Personal effects — reimbursed at lower of actual vs. max admissible weight-cap
