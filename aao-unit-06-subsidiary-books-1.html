§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Sections / Rules

  • Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 — legal definition of Bill of Exchange.
  • Purchases posting = 2-step (personal a/c daily + purchases a/c monthly total).

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Asset purchases (even on credit) are excluded from the purchases book.
  • Trade discount is excluded from delivery charges/cartage when calculated.

📅 Important Dates / Terms

  • 1881 — Negotiable Instruments Act.
  • Due date = bill date + term + 3 days grace.

🏛️ Important Authorities

Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 is the governing statute cited for bills of exchange; no regulatory body named otherwise.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Bill terms commonly: 30/60/90/120 days or 1–6 months + 3 days grace.

🚫 Important Penalties

Dishonour of a bill = non-payment when presented — a business consequence, not a statutory penalty in this unit.

Posting from Purchases Book (2 steps)
Step 1 (daily): Credit side of each supplier's personal A/c — "By Purchases A/c"
Step 2 (monthly total): Debit side of Purchases A/c — "To Sundry Creditors A/c"
🟡 Conceptual Understanding Trade discount is deducted in the invoice itself and never journalised — only the NET figure enters the books. Delivery charges/cartage are ADDED after trade discount, not part of it.

Step 1 (daily): Debit side of each debtor's personal A/c
Step 2 (monthly total): Credit side of Sales A/c — "By Sundry Debtors A/c"
📝 Probable Question: Distinguish purchases book and sales book. (3 marks)
Also called Returns Inward Book — records goods returned BY customers (no immediate cash paid).
Credit Note = source document for returns inward — seller informs buyer that buyer's account is credited for the value returned.
🟢 Quick Revision Debit note ↔ Purchases Returns (outward) | Credit note ↔ Sales Returns (inward). Pair them by first letter: D-Debit-Purchases-Out, C-Credit-Sales-In.
📝 Probable Question: What is a credit note? How does it differ from a debit note? (3 marks)
Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881: "An instrument in writing containing an unconditional order, signed by the maker, directing a certain person to pay a certain sum of money only to, or to the order of, a certain person or to the bearer of the instrument."
Feature
Written document · Unconditional order · Certain sum of money · Signed by drawer · Bears stamp · Accepted by acceptor · Paid to drawer/endorsee

Due Date Calculation Examples
1 March + 2 months + 3 days grace → 4 May
12 July + 1 month + 3 days grace = 15 Aug, but public holiday → due 14 Aug (preceding day)
1 Oct + 30 days + 3 days grace → 3 Nov
🟡 Conceptual Understanding If due date falls on an ordinary/declared holiday → payment due the PRECEDING day. If it falls on an emergency/unforeseen holiday → payment due the SUCCEEDING day. This distinction is a classic exam trap.

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Subsidiary books are sub-divisions of the journal for transactions of similar nature.
  • Purchases/Sales books record ONLY credit transactions of goods, not assets or cash deals.
  • Trade discount is never recorded — only the net amount is entered.
  • Debit note ↔ Purchases Returns (outward); Credit note ↔ Sales Returns (inward).
  • A bill of exchange has 3 parties — drawer, drawee, payee — and 3 days of grace before its due date.
  • Journal proper records residual transactions not covered by the other 7 books.
