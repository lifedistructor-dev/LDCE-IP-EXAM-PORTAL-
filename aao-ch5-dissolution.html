🔀 5.1 Dissolution of Partnership 🔴

Definition: Changes the existing relationship between partners, but the firm may continue its business as before.

7 Ways Partnership Dissolves

  1. Change in existing Profit Sharing Ratio among partners
  2. Admission of a new partner
  3. Retirement of a partner
  4. Death of a partner
  5. Insolvency of a partner
  6. Completion of the venture (if firm formed for a specific purpose)
  7. Expiry of the partnership period (if formed for a fixed term)
🧠 Mnemonic — "CARDIC-E": Change in PSR, Admission, Retirement, Death, Insolvency, Completion, Expiry

⚖️ 5.2 Dissolution of a Firm 🔴

Section 39, Indian Partnership Act 1932: Dissolution of partnership between ALL partners of a firm = Dissolution of the Firm. Brings the firm's existence to an end — no business transacted afterward except winding-up activities.

⚠️ Dissolution of Firm ALWAYS involves dissolution of partnership, but NOT vice versa.

5 Modes of Dissolution of Firm 🔴 EXAM FAVOURITE LIST

With consent of all partners, or per a contract between them.

  • All partners (or all but one) become insolvent
  • Business becomes illegal
  • Unlawful event occurs (e.g., partner becomes alien enemy due to war)
  • Expiry of fixed term
  • Completion of the venture
  • Death of a partner
  • Adjudication of a partner as insolvent

In "partnership at will" — any partner gives written notice of intent to dissolve.

At the suit of a partner, court may order dissolution when:

  • A partner becomes insane
  • A partner becomes permanently incapable of performing duties
  • A partner is guilty of misconduct affecting the business
  • A partner persistently breaches the partnership agreement
  • A partner transfers his whole interest to a third party
  • Business can only be carried on at a loss
  • Court finds it "just and equitable" on any ground
🧠 Mnemonic — "ACCND": Agreement, Compulsory, Contingencies, Notice, Dissolution-by-Court
📝 Probable Q: "Explain the various modes/grounds of dissolution of a partnership firm." — Very common 4-6 mark descriptive question.

📊 Distinction: Dissolution of Partnership vs Dissolution of Firm 🔴 GUARANTEED QUESTION

BasisDissolution of PartnershipDissolution of Firm

Assets/LiabilitiesRevalued; new Balance Sheet drawnAssets sold, liabilities paid off

🧠 Mnemonic — "BACEC": Business, Assets, Court, Economic relationship, Closure of books — the 5 comparison points to always cover.
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed, nearly every year): "Distinguish between Dissolution of Partnership and Dissolution of Firm." — Write as a table for full marks.

💰 5.3 Settlement of Accounts 🔴 SECTION 48 & 49

(a) Treatment of Losses — Section 48 🟠

1st: Out of Profits
↓
2nd: Out of Capital of Partners
↓
3rd (last resort): Partners individually, in PSR

(b) Application of Assets — Order of Payment 🔴 MUST MEMORISE ORDER

1. Pay outside Debts of the firm (creditors, loans, O.D., bills payable)
↓
2. Pay Partners' Loans/Advances (proportionately, NOT capital)
↓
3. Pay Partners' Capital balances
↓
4. Residue (if any) → distributed in Profit Sharing Ratio

*Secured loans have precedence over unsecured loans.

Private Debts vs Firm's Debts — Section 49

RuleOrder of Application
Firm's propertyFirst → Firm's debts; Surplus → divided among partners for their private debts
Partner's private propertyFirst → his private debts; Surplus → firm's debts (if firm's liabilities exceed its assets)

*Private property EXCLUDES personal property of a partner's wife/children.

Garner vs. Murray Rule: If a partner is insolvent and cannot bring in his capital deficiency, the loss is a capital loss for the firm, borne by the solvent partners in the ratio of their capitals as on the date of dissolution (not profit sharing ratio). *Detailed accounting treatment for insolvency not covered at this level.
🧠 Mnemonic — "DCLR": Debts (outside) → Capital-type loans of partners → Loans... wait, order is: Debts, then Partners' Loans, then Capital, then Residue = "D-L-C-R"
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed): "State the order of settlement of accounts on dissolution of a firm as per Section 48." — Must reproduce exact order.

📑 5.4 Accounting Treatment — Realisation Account 🔴 CORE NUMERICAL TOOL

Definition: Prepared to ascertain the net profit or loss on realisation of assets and payment of liabilities. ALL assets (except cash/bank & fictitious assets) and ALL external liabilities are transferred to this account. Balance = profit/loss on realisation → transferred to Partners' Capital A/cs in PSR.

Format (Fig 5.1)

Dr. (Assets transferred + Payments)Cr. (Liabilities transferred + Receipts)
Land, Building, Plant, Furniture, Debtors, Bills Receivable (book values)Sundry Creditors, Bills Payable, Bank O.D., O/s Expenses, Provision for Doubtful Debts
Cash/Bank (payment of liabilities)Cash/Bank (sale of assets)
Cash/Bank (unrecorded liabilities paid)Partner's Capital A/c (asset taken over by partner)
Partner's Capital A/c (liability assumed by partner)Loss transferred to Partners' Capital A/cs (PSR)
Profit transferred to Partners' Capital A/cs (PSR)
🧠 Mnemonic — "Realisation ≠ Revaluation": Realisation A/c = used ONLY at dissolution (firm closes); Revaluation A/c = used at admission/retirement/death (firm continues). This is a very common confusion — a frequent exam question!

✍️ 5.4.1 Journal Entries on Dissolution 🔴 MOST TESTED — MEMORISE ALL

Realisation A/c Dr. → To Assets A/c (individually, at book value)

Debtors transferred at GROSS value; Provision for Doubtful Debts goes to credit side (with liabilities).

Liabilities A/c (individually) Dr. → To Realisation A/c
Bank A/c Dr. → To Realisation A/c
Partner's Capital A/c Dr. → To Realisation A/c
Realisation A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c
Realisation A/c Dr. → To Partner's Capital A/c

Part settlement (asset value < amount due) — pay cash for balance:

Realisation A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c (balance cash paid)

If asset value > amount due — creditor pays cash difference to firm:

Bank A/c Dr. → To Realisation A/c

(a) Paid by firm: Realisation A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c

(b) Paid by a partner on firm's behalf: Realisation A/c Dr. → To Partner's Capital A/c

(c) Partner undertakes dissolution work for agreed remuneration:

  • If firm pays expenses: Partner's Capital A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c
  • If partner personally pays: No entry required
  • For agreed remuneration: Realisation A/c Dr. → To Partner's Capital A/c
Bank A/c Dr. → To Realisation A/c
Realisation A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c

Profit: Realisation A/c Dr. → To Partners' Capital A/cs (PSR)

Loss: Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. (PSR) → To Realisation A/c

Reserve Fund/General Reserve A/c Dr. → To Partners' Capital A/cs (PSR)
Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. (PSR) → To Fictitious Asset A/c
Partner's Loan A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c

*NOT via Realisation A/c — paid directly, and AFTER outside creditors but BEFORE capital.

Debit balance (partner owes firm): Bank A/c Dr. → To Partner's Capital A/c

Credit balance (firm owes partner): Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. → To Bank A/c

*Final check: total payable to partners MUST equal available Bank/Cash balance — all accounts close to zero.

🧠 Mnemonic — "TALS-PLR-URRPF": Transfer Assets, transfer Liabilities, Sale of assets, asset taken over, Payment of liabilities, Liability taken over, Realisation expenses — then Unrecorded items, Reserve transfer, Profit/loss, Fictitious assets, Loan, final Settlement.
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed, biggest question in chapter): "Prepare Realisation Account, Partners' Capital Accounts and Bank Account on dissolution of the firm." — 8-10 marks, appears almost every exam without fail.

⏱️ Last-Minute Memory Tricks

📌 "D-L-C-R": Debts (outside) → Loans (partners') → Capital (partners') → Residue (PSR) — the sacred order of asset application on dissolution.
📌 "Realisation = Firm dies; Revaluation = Firm lives" — quick way to never mix up the two accounts.
📌 "CARDIC-E" for the 7 causes of partnership dissolution; "ACCND" for the 5 modes of firm dissolution.

Solving Sequence for Dissolution Numericals

Step 1
Transfer all assets (excl. cash/bank/fictitious) & external liabilities to Realisation A/c
Step 2
Record sale of assets, payment of liabilities, realisation expenses
Step 3
Record assets/liabilities taken over by partners
Step 4
Transfer unrecorded assets/liabilities
Step 5
Balance Realisation A/c → Profit/Loss → Partners' Capital A/cs (PSR)
Step 6
Transfer Reserves/Fictitious Assets to Capital A/cs
Step 7
Pay off Partners' Loan A/c directly via Bank
Step 8
Settle final Capital A/c balances — Bank/Cash reduces to zero
