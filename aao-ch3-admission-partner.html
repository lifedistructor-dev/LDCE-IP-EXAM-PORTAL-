🔄 3.1 Modes of Reconstitution 🟡

Definition: Any change in the existing agreement among partners = Reconstitution of the firm. Old agreement ends; new one begins. Firm continues.
4 Modes of Reconstitution
Admission of Partner
Change in PSR (existing partners)
Retirement of Partner
Death of Partner

*This chapter covers Admission + Change in PSR. Retirement/Death → Chapter 4.

➕ 3.2 Admission of a New Partner 🔴

  • Per Partnership Act 1932: admission needs unanimous consent of all existing partners (unless deed says otherwise)
  • New partner gets 2 rights: (1) share of assets, (2) share of profits
  • Brings agreed capital + often premium/goodwill to compensate old partners for loss of super-profit share

6 Matters Requiring Adjustment on Admission 🔴 EXAM FAVOURITE LIST

  1. New Profit Sharing Ratio
  2. Sacrificing Ratio
  3. Valuation & Adjustment of Goodwill
  4. Revaluation of Assets & Reassessment of Liabilities
  5. Distribution of Accumulated Profits (Reserves)
  6. Adjustment of Partners' Capitals
🧠 Mnemonic — "NSGRAC": New ratio, Sacrificing ratio, Goodwill, Revaluation, Accumulated profits, Capital adjustment
📝 Probable Q: "State the matters that require adjustment at the time of admission of a new partner." — Nearly guaranteed short-answer question.

🧮 3.3 New Profit Sharing Ratio 🔴

New partner acquires his share from old partners — old partners' ratio changes accordingly. If not specified how the new partner acquires his share, it's assumed he gets it from old partners in their old profit sharing ratio.

New Partner's Share = as agreed Remaining Share (for old partners) = 1 − New Partner's Share Old Partner's New Share = Remaining Share × his Old Ratio proportion
📝 Probable Q: Numerical — "A and B share profits 3:2. They admit C for 1/5 share. Calculate new profit sharing ratio." (Very frequent, 1-2 marks standalone or part of larger sum)

🎁 3.4 Sacrificing Ratio 🔴

Definition: The ratio in which old partners agree to sacrifice their share of profit in favour of the incoming partner.
Sacrificing Ratio = Old Share − New Share (for each old partner)
  • Used to distribute the goodwill/premium brought by new partner among old partners
  • Default (if unspecified): sacrificing ratio = old profit sharing ratio
  • If new ratio is given directly (not the acquisition ratio) → sacrificing ratio MUST be calculated as Old Share − New Share
🧠 Mnemonic — "Sacrifice = Old minus New": Whoever's share drops, sacrificed; whoever's share rises... that's the new partner (or a gaining partner in PSR change).
📝 Probable Q (very high frequency): "Calculate the sacrificing ratio of old partners." — Standard opening step in almost every admission numerical.

💎 3.5 Goodwill — Meaning & Factors 🔴 HIGH WEIGHTAGE

Definition: Goodwill = monetary value of a firm's good name, reputation & business connections built over time — an intangible asset. It is the "present value of a firm's anticipated excess (super) earnings." Goodwill exists ONLY when the firm earns super profits (above normal). Firms earning only normal profit or losses have NO goodwill.

5 Factors Affecting Goodwill 🟠

  • Nature of business — stable demand/high value products = higher goodwill
  • Location — central, high customer traffic = higher goodwill
  • Efficiency of management — better productivity & cost control
  • Market situation — monopoly/limited competition = higher goodwill
  • Special advantages — licences, patents, trademarks, favourable long-term contracts
🧠 Mnemonic — "NLEMS": Nature, Location, Efficiency, Market, Special advantages

6 Occasions Needing Goodwill Valuation 🟡

  1. Change in PSR among existing partners
  2. Admission of new partner
  3. Retirement of a partner
  4. Death of a partner
  5. Dissolution (sale of business as going concern)
  6. Amalgamation of firms

🧾 3.5.4 Methods of Valuation of Goodwill 🔴 NUMERICAL CORE

Goodwill = Average Profits × No. of Years' Purchase

Based on the idea that a new business earns nothing in its first few years — so the buyer pays goodwill equal to expected profits for those years.

Normal Profit = Capital Employed × Normal Rate of Return / 100 Super Profit = Average Profit − Normal Profit Goodwill = Super Profit × No. of Years' Purchase

4 Steps: (1) Calculate average profit → (2) Calculate normal profit → (3) Super profit = Average − Normal → (4) Goodwill = Super profit × years purchase.

(a) Capitalisation of Average Profits:

Capitalised Value = Average Profits × 100 / Normal Rate of Return Capital Employed = Total Assets (excl. goodwill) − Outside Liabilities Goodwill = Capitalised Value − Capital Employed

(b) Capitalisation of Super Profits:

Goodwill = Super Profit × 100 / Normal Rate of Return
🧠 Mnemonic — "A-S-C": Average profits method (simplest), Super profits method (excess only), Capitalisation method (back-calculate from normal rate)
📝 Probable Q: Numerical on ANY of the 3 methods — extremely common, 4-6 marks. Practice all three with capital employed & normal rate given.

⚖️ 3.5.5 Treatment of Goodwill on Admission 🔴 MOST TESTED TOPIC

Does new partner bring goodwill in cash?
YES → Premium Method
NO → Revaluation Method

(a) Premium Method 🟠

New partner pays goodwill in cash → shared by old partners in sacrificing ratio.

Cash A/c Dr. To Premium/Goodwill A/c Premium/Goodwill A/c Dr. To Old Partners' Capital A/cs (in sacrificing ratio)

If old partners withdraw the premium:
Old Partners' Capital A/c Dr. → To Cash A/c

(b) Revaluation Method (Hidden/Implicit Goodwill) 🟠

New partner brings NO cash for goodwill → Goodwill A/c raised in books, credited to old partners in OLD ratio (not sacrificing ratio, since goodwill wasn't there before).

Goodwill A/c Dr. (full value) To Old Partners' Capital A/cs (in old ratio)

(c) Hidden Goodwill 🟡

When goodwill value isn't directly given — inferred by comparing implied total capital (based on new partner's capital & share) with actual total capital of all partners. The difference = hidden goodwill, shared by old partners in old ratio.

Implied Total Capital = New Partner's Capital × (1 / his share) Hidden Goodwill = Implied Total Capital − Actual Combined Capital
🧠 Mnemonic — "Premium=Sacrificing ratio; Revaluation/Hidden=Old ratio": Cash goodwill → distributed by SACRIFICE; raised-in-books goodwill → distributed by OLD ratio.
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed): "Pass journal entries for treatment of goodwill" under each of the 3 scenarios — appears virtually every exam.

📦 3.6 Accumulated Profits & Losses 🟠

General Reserve, Reserve Fund, or P&L (Cr) balance existing before admission → belongs to OLD partners only → distributed to their capital A/cs in old ratio. Similarly, accumulated LOSSES (P&L Dr balance) are also transferred to old partners in old ratio.
General Reserve A/c Dr. To Old Partners' Capital A/cs (old ratio) [if reserve] Old Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. (old ratio) To P&L A/c (Dr balance) [if accumulated loss]
📝 Probable Q: "Pass journal entries for adjustment of General Reserve and accumulated losses at the time of admission." — Common 2-3 mark sub-part.

🔍 3.7 Revaluation of Assets & Reassessment of Liabilities 🔴

At admission, assets/liabilities are revalued to reflect current values. Gain/loss goes through the Revaluation Account; net result shared by OLD partners in old ratio (new partner NOT entitled — these gains/losses relate to pre-admission period).
EventRevaluation A/c Entry
Increase in AssetAsset Dr. → To Revaluation (Gain)
Decrease in AssetRevaluation Dr. → To Asset (Loss)
Increase in LiabilityRevaluation Dr. → To Liability (Loss)
Decrease in LiabilityLiability Dr. → To Revaluation (Gain)
Unrecorded Asset foundCash/Asset Dr. → To Revaluation (Gain)
Unrecorded Liability foundRevaluation Dr. → To Cash/Liability (Loss)
Revaluation A/c shows CREDIT balance → Net Gain → Cr. to Old Partners (old ratio)
↕
Revaluation A/c shows DEBIT balance → Net Loss → Dr. to Old Partners (old ratio)
🧠 Mnemonic — "Asset Up=Gain, Liability Up=Loss": increase in asset value and decrease in liability = gain (credit revaluation); decrease in asset value and increase in liability = loss (debit revaluation).
📝 Probable Q (guaranteed): Numerical — "Prepare Revaluation Account, Partners' Capital Accounts and Balance Sheet after admission." — The BIGGEST question type in this chapter (8-10 marks).

💰 3.8 Adjustment of Capitals 🟡

Sometimes partners agree capitals should be made proportionate to the new profit sharing ratio. New partner's capital is often used as the base to calculate what old partners' capitals should be.

1. New partner's capital ÷ his share = Total Capital of new firm
↓
2. Total Capital × each old partner's new share = his required capital
↓
3. Compare with adjusted old capital (after goodwill/revaluation/reserves)
↓
4. Shortfall → partner brings in cash; Surplus → partner withdraws cash

🔁 3.9 Change in PSR Among Existing Partners 🟡

Partners may change their PSR without any admission/retirement. Some partners gain, others sacrifice — essentially one partner "buying" profit share from another.

  • Goodwill adjustment: raise goodwill at full value in old ratio, then write off in new ratio — OR directly debit gaining partners, credit sacrificing partners
  • Same adjustments apply as in admission: revaluation of assets/liabilities, accumulated profits/losses, capital adjustment (if specified)
Gaining Ratio = New Share − Old Share (for gaining partners) Sacrificing Ratio = Old Share − New Share (for sacrificing partners)
