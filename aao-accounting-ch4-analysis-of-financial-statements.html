1 Topic & Definition 🔴 VIP

Topic: Financial statements alone are just summarised reports — to be useful for decisions, they must be critically analysed and interpreted. This chapter covers the meaning and tools of Financial Statement Analysis.

Definition: The process of critical evaluation of financial information in the financial statements to understand and make decisions regarding the operations of the firm.

Analysis vs Interpretation: Analysis = methodical simplification/classification of data. Interpretation = explaining the meaning and significance of that data. They are complementary — analysis without interpretation is useless.

Likely Q: "Distinguish between Analysis and Interpretation of financial statements."

3 Objectives of Financial Analysis 🟡 Conceptual

  • Assess current profitability & operational efficiency
  • Ascertain relative importance of different components of financial position
  • Identify reasons for change in profitability/financial position
  • Judge ability to repay debt — both short-term & long-term liquidity

5 Comparative Statements — Method & Formula 🔴 VIP — Guaranteed Numerical

Also called Horizontal Analysis. Shows figures for two (or more) periods side by side with absolute and % change — same accounting principles must be used, else deviations are footnoted.

% Increase/Decrease = (Absolute Change ÷ First Year Figure) × 100

6 Common Size Statements — Method & Formula 🔴 VIP — Guaranteed Numerical

Also called Vertical Analysis / Component Percentage Statement. Each item is expressed as a % of a common base within the same period — enables inter-firm comparison of companies with different sizes.

Common Size % = (Individual Item ÷ Base Item) × 100

7 Comparison — Horizontal vs Vertical Analysis 🟠 Frequently Asked

Comparative Statement (Horizontal)

  • Compares figures ACROSS two or more periods
  • Base = same item's own prior-year figure
  • Shows absolute + % change over time
  • Good for time-series / intra-firm trend

Common Size Statement (Vertical)

  • Compares items WITHIN the same period
  • Base = common total (Sales or Total Assets)
  • Shows proportion/structure at a point in time
  • Good for inter-firm comparison (different sizes)
Likely Q: "Distinguish between Horizontal Analysis and Vertical Analysis." (Very frequently asked, 3–4 marks)

8 Limitations of Financial Analysis 🔴 VIP

  • Does not consider price level changes (inflation)
  • Misleading without knowledge of changes in accounting procedure
  • Just a study of the reports of the company — nothing beyond what's disclosed
  • Only monetary information considered; non-monetary aspects ignored
  • Based on accounting concepts — does not reflect current position (inherits limitations of financial statements themselves)
Mnemonic — "PAMS-C": Price level ignored, Accounting procedure changes misleading, Mere study of reports, Monetary only, Statements' own limitation → Current position not reflected.
