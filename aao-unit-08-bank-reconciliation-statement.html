What is BRS

  • A statement reconciling bank column of cash book with the bank statement (pass book), explaining every difference

Mirror-Image Rule

  • Cash book Dr = Bank statement Cr (favourable balance)
  • Cash book Cr = Bank statement Dr (overdraft)

2 Root Causes of Difference

  • Timing differences (8 causes)
  • Errors in recording (cash book OR bank)

2 Presentation Methods

  • Balance method (starts from one balance, adjusts to reach the other)
  • Plus & Minus method

Golden Add/Less Rule (from cash book balance)

  • Add: cheques issued not presented + bank-only credits
  • Less: cheques deposited not credited + bank-only debits

Overdraft Fact

  • Cash book can NEVER show unfavourable (overdraft) cash balance, but its BANK column can

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Rules

  • Deposits are Debited in cash book but Credited in bank statement — and vice versa for withdrawals (mirror-image relationship).
  • Bank overdraft = borrowing facility available only on a Current Account.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Cash book's CASH column can never be unfavourable, but its BANK column CAN show a credit (overdraft) balance — the one true exception to "cash can't go negative."

📅 Important Dates

No fixed statutory date — BRS may be prepared daily, weekly, or monthly depending on transaction volume; must be updated at financial year-end for accurate balance sheet reporting.

🏛️ Important Authorities

Internal reconciliation procedure between business (cash book) and banker (pass book/e-statement) — no external regulatory authority named.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Cash book need not be adjusted for month-to-month reconciliation, but MUST be updated at year-end for the correct balance sheet figure.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — BRS instead serves to discourage misuse of funds by discouraging fraud, per its stated objectives.

🟡 Conceptual Understanding The reversal happens because the cash book is written from the BUSINESS's point of view (bank owes them = asset = debit), while the bank statement is written from the BANK's point of view (bank owes the customer = liability = credit). Only Current Accounts get overdraft facility.

Two root causes: (i) Timing differences — same item recorded at different times · (ii) Errors in recording — by business or by bank.

Memory Pattern: Which side is bigger?
Business hasn't recorded a BANK-SIDE GAIN yet (dividends, direct deposit, bills collected) → Bank statement balance is HIGHER
Business hasn't recorded a BANK-SIDE LOSS yet (charges, dishonour, direct payment by bank) → Cash book balance is HIGHER
Cheque issued, not yet presented → Bank statement HIGHER (cash book already reduced, bank hasn't)
Cheque deposited, not yet credited → Cash book HIGHER (cash book already increased, bank hasn't)
🟢 Quick Revision Errors can occur in EITHER record — the cash book (business-side) or the bank statement (banker-side) — both must be checked when the difference isn't explained by timing alone.

🟢 Quick Revision Adjusting the cash book itself is NOT compulsory for interim reconciliation, but IS compulsory at financial year-end so the balance sheet shows the true bank balance.
📝 Probable Question: Prepare a bank reconciliation statement from cash book balance, given a list of adjustment items. (8 marks — most repeated numerical question)

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Bank statement is a copy of the customer's account maintained by the bank.
  • Debit balance in cash book = favourable; Credit balance in cash book = overdraft (and the reverse for the bank statement).
  • Differences arise from timing differences and/or recording errors.
  • BRS can be prepared using the Balance method or the Plus & Minus method.
  • Cash book must be updated at year-end to reflect the correct bank balance in the balance sheet.
