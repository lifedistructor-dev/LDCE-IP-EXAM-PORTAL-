3 Types of Expenditure

  • Capital — benefit > 1 year, shown in Balance Sheet (asset side)
  • Revenue — benefit within 1 year, in Trading/P&L A/c
  • Deferred Revenue — revenue in nature, benefit over several years, written off gradually

4 Considerations to Classify

  • Nature of business
  • Effect on revenue-generating capacity
  • Purpose of expenditure
  • Materiality of amount

2 Types of Receipts

  • Capital receipt — non-recurring, substantial, Balance Sheet (liabilities side)
  • Revenue receipt — recurring, smaller, distributable as profit

Golden Test

  • Goods for the firm's OWN TRADE = revenue expenditure
  • Same item bought as an ASSET (not for resale) = capital expenditure

Deferred Revenue Treatment

  • Amount written off this year → debit side of P&L A/c
  • Unwritten-off balance → asset side of Balance Sheet

Materiality Exception

  • Immaterial amount → treated as revenue even if benefit extends beyond one year (e.g. cost of a waste basket)

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Rules

  • Classification is based on the matching principle — expenses matched with the revenue of the period they generate.
  • Nature of business decides whether an item is "goods" (revenue) or "asset" (capital).

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Materiality exception: immaterial-value items are expensed as revenue even if their benefit technically extends beyond one accounting period.
  • Same item, different treatment: furniture is "goods" (revenue) for a furniture dealer but an "asset" (capital) for any other business — nature of business overrides the item itself.

📅 Important Dates

No historical dates in this unit — classification is a conceptual/recurring accounting-period matter, not date-specific.

🏛️ Important Authorities

No external regulatory body cited — classification follows accounting concepts (matching, periodicity) rather than a named statute in this unit.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

  • Deferred revenue expenditure benefit "usually accrues for a period of two or more years" — written off gradually over that span.

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — misclassification affects the accuracy of profit/loss and balance sheet, not a statutory penalty, in this unit's scope.

📝 Probable Question: State the considerations in determining capital and revenue expenditure. (5 marks — very frequently asked)
Expenditure whose benefit extends beyond one accounting period — acquisition of fixed assets or anything adding to the revenue-earning capacity of the business. Non-recurring.
Feature
Benefit > 1 accounting period · Includes fixed asset acquisition + all expenses till asset is ready for use · Contributes to revenue-earning capacity · Non-recurring · Shown on Assets side of Balance Sheet
Examples: Land & building acquisition · Office equipment/computer/AC · Plant & machinery including installation & trial run charges.
📝 Probable Question: State the features of capital expenditure with examples. (4 marks)
Expenditure for day-to-day running or maintaining the earning capacity of the business. Recurring in nature, benefit expires within the same accounting period.
Feature
Recurring · Maintains earning capacity · Benefit expires same period · Shown on Debit side of Trading/P&L A/c
Examples: Purchase of goods for resale · Administrative/selling/distribution expenses · Manufacturing expenses.
📝 Probable Question: Distinguish capital expenditure from revenue expenditure. (5 marks — high repeat rate)
A REVENUE expenditure in nature, but its benefit is derived over a subsequent period or periods (usually 2+ years) — so it's temporarily deferred and written off gradually rather than charged fully in the year incurred.
Feature
Revenue in nature, multi-year benefit · Not fully written off in year of expenditure · Balance after writing off shown on Assets side of Balance Sheet
Examples: Heavy advertising expenditure · Major repairs to plant and machinery.

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Capital expenditure benefits more than one accounting period; revenue expenditure benefits only the current period.
  • Deferred revenue expenditure is revenue in nature but written off over several years.
  • Capital receipts are non-recurring and shown on the liabilities side of the balance sheet.
  • Revenue receipts are recurring and can be distributed as profit.
