Mr. Gopal's Garment Business

A story-based case study to master Capital, Assets, Purchases, Debtor, Creditor, Expenses & Drawings — built for India Post AAO Exam revision.

Exam Answers — Tap to Expand
💰
(i) Capital High Priority
Amount business started with
▼
Capital is the owner's own money invested to start the business.
Initial Investment = Rs 8,00,000
🪑
(ii) Fixed Assets High Priority
Long-term use items, not for resale
▼
Fixed Assets are items bought for continued use in the business, not for selling.
Furniture (50,000) + Computer (50,000) = Rs 1,00,000
👗
(iii) Purchases High Priority
Goods bought for resale
▼
Purchases = goods bought specifically to sell again (stock-in-trade).
Garments (4,00,000) + Men's Garments (2,00,000) = Rs 6,00,000
🧾
(iv) Creditor Medium
Party the business owes money to
▼
A Creditor is a supplier from whom goods were bought on credit — business must pay him.
Mr. Satish (Creditor) = Rs 2,00,000
🙋
(v) Debtor Medium
Party who owes money to business
▼
A Debtor is a customer who bought goods on credit — he must pay the business.
Mr. Rajesh (Debtor) = Rs 1,50,000
📉
(vi) Expenses Good to Know
Cost of running the business
▼
Expenses are routine costs paid to run daily business operations (rent, salary, etc.).
Total Expenses = Rs 15,000
🏠
(vii) Drawings Good to Know
Owner's personal withdrawal
▼
Drawings = cash or goods taken by the owner from business for personal/domestic use.
Withdrawn by Mr. Gopal = Rs 20,000
