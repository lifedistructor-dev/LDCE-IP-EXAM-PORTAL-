6.01 Role of Account Current Section 🔴 VI▾

Responsible for inter-Government & inter-Departmental adjustments and accounting of cash transactions at Bank branches. Places Postmasters in account with only one branch and/or accredited PSU banks under RBI jurisdiction.

Non-Banking treasury cash transactions → incorporated lump-sum under head "Adjusting Account with P&T"; settlement between Centre/State/Postal balances routed via Central Accounts Section (CAS), RBI Nagpur.

For non-cash inter-departmental transactions (incl. Railways/Defence), Director/Dy.Director (Postal) sends advice 3 times a month: 10th, 20th, end of month (25th April latest for March) to CAS Nagpur.

Proforma Adjustments (no monetary settlement via RBI) permitted by CGA when: (i) normal RBI settlement missed the 25th April deadline, and (ii) aggregate ≥ ₹1 lakh gross credit/debit.

6.02 Accounts Jurisdiction & Put-Through Statement 🟠 FAQ▾

23 Circle PAOs each hold defined accounts jurisdiction (same list as Chapter II). Charges from Stationery/Printing Offices at Kolkata & Delhi routed via Dy. Chief Pay & Accounts Officers to relevant PAO; sealing wax charges shown separately.

Put Through Statement: CAS RBI Nagpur sends monthly statement of total Receipts/Payments per bank branch to Postal Accounts Office in the first week of the succeeding month. PAO scrutinises against Bank's Date-wise Monthly Statement; discrepancies reported via Discrepancy Statement (Annexure-B).

Where Treasury acts as bank agent: Treasury Officer sends duplicate payment/receipt schedule on 11th and 1st of following month, with paid cheques/chalans; copy also to Civil AG.

6.03 Bank Scrolls & Treasury Schedules – Receipt, Checks, Pairing 🔴 VI▾

Treasury schedules due bi-monthly: 11th and 1st of following month — indexed in Skeleton Register Form DG(PA)-522.

Daily Scroll checks: tally with challans/vouchers/cheques; verify progressive totals; cheques above ₹10 lakh need 2 authorised signatories; verify cheque series matches PAO issuance; e-scrolls tallied with hard copy; no cross-department cheque clearance errors.

Pairing: e-schedules (from F&A) paired against e-scrolls (from banks); items unpaired go to Register of Unlinked Items — Form DG(PA)-506/523. Pairing process (paras 6.13–6.17) must complete by 15th of the following month.

Circle Broadsheet (Form DG(PA)-508 for Bank / DG(PA)-525 for Treasury): differences not settled within 1 month → notified to Branch Officer; beyond 2 months → to Head of Office. Broadsheet + Unlinked Items Register submitted to Branch Officer by the last day of the 2nd month following month of account.

Likely Q: By which date must the pairing of bank scrolls with postal schedules be completed? → 15th of the following month.

6.04 RBI Balance Reconciliation (Head 8675) 🔴 VI▾

PAO verifies its Postal Account balance (per RBI books) agrees with departmental figures under "8675 – Deposits with Reserve Bank." Differences reconciled next month; compliance intimated to Postal Wing (Directorate) by 10th of the 2nd following month.

Register submitted to Branch Officer on the last day of the month. Directorate reports the reconciled position (Postal vs RBI figures) to CGA by first week of the 2nd following month.

4 kinds of Inter-Govt/Inter-Dept transactions: (1) Postal txns in State Section of Civil AG accounts (2) Postal txns in Central Section of Civil AG accounts (3) Postal txns in Railway/Defence Accounts (4) Postal-origin txns adjustable against State/Central/Railway/Defence balances.

6.05 Advice of Clearance & Outward Settlement Account 🟠 FAQ▾

Advice of Clearance: Net amounts advised to CAS RBI for adjustment; Register of Advices maintained (Form DG(PA)-526), submitted to Branch Officer by end of the 2nd following month.

Register of Compilation of Schedules (Form DG(PA)-528) → transcribed to Register of Outward Advices (Form DG(PA)-527), separate folio per Accounts Officer, Sections A (Central) & B (State).

Outward Settlement Account (Form A.O. 11-A): Submitted to Branch Officer by 15th of the 2nd following month. Items outstanding >3 months → flagged to Head of Office quarterly; none should exceed 6 months. Quarterly report (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec cycle) due by end of the 2nd month after quarter-end.

Monthly Statement from CAS RBI: Sent to PAO by the 25th of each month (10th May for March adjustments).

6.06 Inward Settlement Account & Objection Categories 🟠 FAQ▾

Settlement Accounts generally received by the 1st week of the 2nd following month; schedules/vouchers arrive earlier as advices are issued.

4 Objection categories (Form A.O. 23, communicated via Form A.O. 22): (i) Debits/Credits without supporting documents (ii) Amounts not adjustable in DoP books (iii) Debits/Credits without sufficient details (iv) Other irregularities (total errors, illegible figures).

Sy-slips must return to Account Current Section within 7 days. Objection Book for a month submitted to Branch Officer by the 25th of the following month. Items unresolved → adjusted to "Unclassified Suspense" under head 8661; register closed by 20th, sent to Account Current by 25th.

Where classification is doubtful, executive given 1 month to object/concur before automatic adjustment under final head.

6.07 Inward/Outward Adjustment Entries 🟡 Concept▾
Inward: Credit/Debit Revenue-Service Head ↔ Dr/Cr "Central A/c Office-RB Suspense" → on Clearance Memo → Deduct RB Suspense, Dr/Cr "8675-Deposits with RBI"
↕
Outward: Advice issued to CAS RBI based on booked figures → on Clearance Memo → Deduct "A/c with States/Railways/Defence" ↔ per contra "8675-Deposits with RBI"

Pay & Accounts Offices (P&AO): Settled via Mail Transfer (favour of P&AO) or RBI cheque (favour of DoP); Bank Drafts used only where no RBI office exists locally.

6.08 Railways/Defence/MEA/Foreign Govt/UK Transactions 🔴 VI▾
CounterpartySettlement Route
Railways / DefenceAdvice via CAS RBI at 10th/20th/month-end → head "Adjusting A/c with Railway/Defence" (Section M-Remittances) → cleared per contra to 8675
Foreign GovernmentsSettled directly by PAO with the Foreign Government concerned
United Kingdom (Inward)Chief Accounting Officer (High Commission London) → Addl. AG Maharashtra (Nagpur) converts £→₹ at avg. exchange rate → RBI adjusts vs PAO Nagpur balance

Important UK Inward London A/c items: Cost of passages, passage rebate in England, GPF subscription recoveries, MO settlement payments to Commonwealth Relations Office, PO Certificate discharge value at India Office, High Commissioner's advances.

PAO Nagpur adjusts items pertaining to itself directly; other Circles' items routed via Postal Remittances head.

Annexure-II: Opening a Drawing & Deposit Account (DDA) 🟠 FAQ▾

Authority: PA Wing letter dt. 25.04.2008. DDA opened in name of Director of Accounts (Postal) in RBI-accredited banks (SBI + 2 nationalised banks per state).

Annual cash turnover limit: ₹40 lakh per annum
Annexure A & B (quadruplicate) + bank concurrence letter → forwarded to PAO, countersigned by Divisional & Regional Head
Documents countersigned by DA(Postal) → sent to bank's Corporate Office for authorisation
PAO issues authority letter → account opened in name of Head Postmaster, operated by Sub Postmaster
2 separate accounts — Drawing & Deposit — used strictly for their purpose
Cheque books only via PAO indent; no current account permitted under any circumstance
CSI/SAP reconciliation via T-codes FF67 and FEBAN; cleared items shown in green — all must turn green by end of following month

🧠 "₹40 lakh, 2 accounts, 0 current accounts" — the 3 numbers to remember for DDA rules.
