4 Types of Errors

  • Omission (complete/partial)
  • Commission
  • Principle
  • Compensating

The Golden Filter

  • One-sided error → trial balance WON'T tally
  • Two-sided error → trial balance WILL tally (hides the error)

NOT Disclosed by Trial Balance

  • Complete omission · Error of principle · Compensating errors · Wrong entry in subsidiary book (recorded correctly on both sides)

4 Stages of Rectification

  • 1. Before trial balance
  • 2. While preparing trial balance
  • 3. After trial balance, before final accounts
  • 4. After final accounts

Suspense Account

  • Temporary account holding the trial balance difference until all errors are found & corrected

Transposition Clue

  • If TB difference divisible by 9 → suspect transposition of figures (e.g. 127 written as 172)

§ Exam Vault — Sections, Dates, Authorities

📜 Important Rules

  • One-sided errors before trial balance → rectified directly (no journal entry, just correct the account).
  • One-sided errors after trial balance → rectified via Suspense A/c.

⚖️ Important Exceptions

  • Error of complete omission affects TWO+ accounts yet is NOT disclosed by trial balance — the classic exception exam trap (most errors affecting 2 accounts ARE disclosed, but complete omission is the exception because both sides are equally missing).

📅 Important Dates

No historical dates — but rectification is tied to accounting-cycle stages (journalising → posting → balancing → trial balance → final accounts).

🏛️ Important Authorities

Internal accounting-control procedure — no external regulatory authority cited in this unit.

⏱️ Important Time Limits

No statutory time-limit — but rectification is expected before final accounts wherever possible, since errors after final accounts require Profit & Loss Adjustment A/c treatment (advanced stage).

🚫 Important Penalties

No penalty provision — errors are corrective, not punitive, in this unit's scope.

Decision Tree: Will the trial balance catch it?
Does the error make Debit ≠ Credit (one-sided effect)? → YES → Disclosed, TB won't tally
Does the error keep Debit = Credit (both sides equally wrong/missing)? → NO → Not disclosed, TB tallies

🟢 Quick Revision "Halve the difference" trick: if a ledger balance was posted to the WRONG SIDE of the trial balance (instead of omitted), the resulting TB difference is exactly DOUBLE that balance — so halving it reveals the culprit figure.
📝 Probable Question: Explain the steps to locate errors after the trial balance is prepared. (6 marks)
When trial balance doesn't tally, the difference is placed to Suspense A/c — debit side (if credit total is higher) or credit side (if debit total is higher) — a temporary holding account.
🟡 Conceptual Understanding Suspense A/c is progressively cleared as each one-sided error is found and rectified. Once ALL errors are located and corrected, the Suspense A/c balance becomes NIL and the account disappears from the books.

Decision Tree: How to rectify?
One-sided error found BEFORE trial balance → Correct the account directly — no journal entry needed
One-sided error found AFTER trial balance is prepared → Rectify via Suspense A/c
Two-sided error (any stage) → Pass a proper rectifying journal entry between the two accounts affected
🟢 Quick Revision The core rule: rectify a one-sided error by directly correcting the account BEFORE the TB is drawn, but route it through Suspense A/c AFTER the TB is drawn — because by then, the account may already be closed/summarised.
📝 Probable Question: Explain the different stages of rectification of errors. (6 marks)
In computerised systems, arithmetic/posting errors (casting, carry-forward, ledger transfer) are largely eliminated since ledger, trial balance and final accounts are auto-generated from journal entries. Errors that remain are typically data-entry errors (wrong account/wrong amount chosen by the user) — i.e., errors of principle and commission can still occur, but mechanical posting errors mostly cannot.
📝 Probable Question: Which types of errors are eliminated in computerised accounting, and which can still occur? (3 marks)

✓ Points to Remember (from textbook)

  • Errors can occur at journalising, posting, balancing, or trial balance preparation stages.
  • Errors are classified as: omission, commission, principle, and compensating.
  • One-sided errors are disclosed by trial balance; two-sided/balanced errors are not.
  • Suspense account temporarily holds the trial balance difference until all errors are rectified.
  • Rectification method depends on the stage at which the error is discovered.
