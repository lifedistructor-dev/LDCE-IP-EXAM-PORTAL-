Chapter XVIII — Internal Audit
अध्याय 18 — आंतरिक लेखा परीक्षा

▲
18.01 Purpose & Scope 🔴 VI▾

Departmentalization of accounts requires an efficient Internal Audit organisation, set up at Postal HQ, New Delhi-1. Governed by the Internal Audit Manual (model guidance, supplemented by instructions from time to time — not exhaustive).

IA checks initial accounts of DDOs/NCDDOs for rule/procedure compliance across all Heads of Account. Performed "on-the-spot," coordinated via Internal Audit sections of Directorate + Circle PAOs, who also process Draft Audit Paras, CAG Paras, PAC Paras — IA acts on behalf of the Chief Accounting Authority.

Scope of each IA visit: General review of all records since last inspection (or since formation, for new units) + detailed check of at least 1 month, selected by Sr.DDG (HQ-level IA) or Head of PAO (Circle-level IA).

18.02 Inspection Staff & Programme 🟠 FAQ▾

Heads of PAOs deploy staff on a roster basis so all eligible staff get IA exposure. IA programme cross-checked against P&T statutory audit schedule to avoid overlap on same dates.

Annual IA programme prepared by IA section with Head-of-Circle approval, copy to Directorate; arrears avoided except in extraordinary circumstances (reported to Directorate + Head of Circle).

🔴 Key number: Minimum 12 surprise visits per year by Head of PAO/nominated officer (not below Sr. Accounts Officer rank), report to Directorate.

Staff up to Junior Accountant level rotate through IA duty. Illustrative pending-issue checklist for branch officers: 8553 Recoverable Advances, 8661 Credit/Debit Suspense, objections >6 months, unlinked drawing/remittance items, NPS/SDBS issues, Loans & Advances, customer discrepancies, missing Service Books for Rule 32 verification, IPPB/POSB/PLI-RPLI reconciliation.

18.03 Procedure of Audit 🔴 VI▾
Work allotted per IA Manual, acknowledged by team members Daily progress report by each member Working papers maintained, submitted to Team Head Computerised IA where systems permit Auditee communication via "Rough Memo" (signed by Team Head) Subordinate offices selected to avoid repeat coverage Objections must cite Rules/orders — never generic Routine errors rectified on the spot

IA team needs skills in: Business Rules of DoP products, statutory rules, application software (MS Office), data analysis, accrual accounting, tax management, fund management, report writing, handling Draft Audit Paras.

18.04 Inspection Report Structure 🔴 VI▾

Reports written in polite, dispassionate language — no sarcasm, accusatory tone, or judgemental fixing of responsibility (that's for administrative authorities). Objections must be Rule-supported.

3-Part Report Structure
  • Part I: (a) Introductory (b) Outstanding objections, brief (c) List of persistent irregularities
  • Part II: Section A = Major irregularities (recoveries/principle/finance-loss); Section B = Notable but non-major irregularities
  • Part III: Test Check Notes — minor irregularities, procedural assurances

Paras numbered serially from 1 across both Part I and II. Suspected fraud/misappropriation → included in Part I. Money-value objections settled only after complete recovery or write-off.

Part III issued on the spot (signed by supervising officer/AAO-in-charge) — does not need detailed reply, only acknowledgement + corrective action, verified at next inspection.

18.05 Issue & Disposal Timelines 🔴 VI▾

Report scrutinised by IA Section; Parts I & II finally passed by GM/Director/Dy.Director (PAF); sent in duplicate to Head of Office inspected.

Inspection Report must be issued within 1 month of inspection completion — tracked via Register Form DG(PA)-97, reviewed by GM/Director on 10th of every month
↓
Departmental Officer allowed ~1 month to dispose the report
↓
On receipt of reply, disposal targeted within a fortnight
↓
Reports outstanding >6 months tracked in Register Form DG(PA)-97-A, entered at start of 7th month, reviewed by Head of Office in the last week of each month

🔴 Key dates: Report issued within 1 month → disposal ~1 month → reply-processing within a fortnight → 6-month-old items tracked separately.

18.06 Internal Audit within the PAO Itself 🟠 FAQ▾

IA Section also checks other PAO sections' own compliance with procedural rules, register maintenance, and due-date returns — functioning like a Director of Inspection, and liaising with the Directorate's Internal Check Organization.

IA Section should be small, expert staff, directly under Head of Office / Director / Dy.Director — not burdened with original (routine) work. Common defects circularised periodically via office orders.

14 illustrative items entrusted to IA Section (partial): dedicated IA Team formation; test-check of accounting work; monitoring implementation of office orders; investigating arrears; liaison with HQ Inspection Party; PF Suspense review; Exchange/Settlement Account defect review; quarterly cash-book review; Service Book test-check; independent budget-proposal rechecks; Debt/Deposit write-off examination.

18.07 GDS TRCA Audit Point 🟡 Concept▾

Field-unit IA by Circle PAOs must invariably check GDS TRCA cases — revision, regulation, ex-gratia calculation, Severance Allowance — correlated against actual DDO drawal, for the month marked by Head of PAO. All TRCA/establishment revisions since the last inspection are checked and irregularities recorded.
