8.1

Meaning of Bill of Exchange

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Bill of Exchange (Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881): an instrument in writing containing an unconditional order, signed by the maker, directing a certain person to pay a certain sum of money only to, or to the order of, a certain person or to the bearer.

Essential Features

  • Must be in writing, signed by the maker
  • Contains an unconditional ORDER to pay
  • Sum and date of payment must be certain
  • Payable to a certain person; properly stamped
  • Before acceptance it's just a "draft"; becomes a bill once the drawee writes "accepted" and signs
📝 Probable Exam QuestionDefine a bill of exchange and state its essential features.

🔴 Very Important — 1-mark question every year

DRAWER

DRAWEE
  • Person on whom drawn
  • The buyer / debtor
  • Becomes "Acceptor" once he signs
PAYEE
  • Receives payment
  • Usually = Drawer
  • Can change: bank (if discounted) or a creditor (if endorsed)

8.2

Promissory Note

🔴 Very Important — Comparison guaranteed in exam
Promissory Note: an instrument in writing containing an unconditional undertaking (promise), signed by the maker, to pay a certain sum only to, or to the order of, a certain person. Cannot be made payable to bearer (RBI Act).

Bill of Exchange vs Promissory Note

ContainsAn ORDER to payA PROMISE to pay
Parties3 — Drawer, Drawee, Payee2 — Drawer (Maker) & Payee

8.3

Advantages of a Bill of Exchange

🟢 Quick Revision
  • Framework for relationship — structures the credit transaction
  • Certainty of terms — amount, date, interest all fixed in writing
  • Convenient credit — seller can still get immediate cash via discounting/endorsing
  • Conclusive legal proof of the credit transaction
  • Easy transferability — settle debts via endorsement and delivery
8.4

Maturity of a Bill

🔴 Very Important — date calculation numericals
Maturity = the due date of the bill. Add 3 days of grace to the nominal due date.
Q: Does the calculated maturity date fall on a public holiday?
Ordinary holiday → maturity = the preceding business day
Emergency holiday (govt-declared) → maturity = the next working day

Example: Bill dated March 05, payable 30 days after date → due 33 days later = April 07 (30 + 3 grace days).

📝 Probable Exam QuestionCalculate the date of maturity for a bill dated [X], payable [N] months/days after date.

8.7

Accounting Treatment — 4 Fates of a Bill

🔴 The core numerical of this chapter
For the drawer → it's a Bills Receivable (asset). For the acceptor → it's a Bills Payable (liability).
A Bill Receivable can be...
1. Retained
  • Held till maturity, collected directly or via bank
2. Discounted
  • Encashed early with the bank (minus discount)
3. Endorsed
  • Transferred to a creditor to settle a debt
4. Sent for Collection
  • Given to bank just before maturity to collect on drawer's behalf

Journal Entries — In the Drawer's Books

EventEntry
Receiving the billBills Receivable A/c Dr. → To Debtor's A/c
Retained, collected on maturityCash/Bank A/c Dr. → To Bills Receivable A/c
Discounted with bankBank A/c Dr., Discount A/c Dr. → To Bills Receivable A/c
Endorsed to a creditorCreditor's A/c Dr. → To Bills Receivable A/c
Sent for collectionBills Sent for Collection A/c Dr. → To Bills Receivable A/c

In the Acceptor's Books (same regardless of drawer's choice)

EventEntry
Accepting the billCreditor's A/c Dr. → To Bills Payable A/c
Maturity — bill honouredBills Payable A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c

8.8

Dishonour of a Bill

🔴 Very Important for Exam
Dishonour: the drawee fails to pay on the maturity date. The acceptor's liability is restored — so the receiver reverses the earlier entry, regardless of what they did with the bill.
Bill was...Rectifying entry on dishonour
Retained till maturityDrawee's A/c Dr. → To Bills Receivable A/c
Discounted with bankDrawee's A/c Dr. → To Bank A/c
Endorsed to a creditorDrawee's A/c Dr. → To Endorsee's A/c
Sent for collectionDrawee's A/c Dr. → To Bills Sent for Collection A/c
📝 Probable Exam QuestionWhat is meant by dishonour of a bill? Record the entries in the drawer's books if the bill had been discounted before dishonour.
8.9

Renewal of a Bill

🟠 Frequently Asked
Renewal: the acceptor requests more time before maturity; the old bill is cancelled and a fresh bill (often plus interest for the extension) is drawn and accepted.

8.10

Retiring of a Bill

🟠 Frequently Asked
Retiring: the drawee pays the bill BEFORE the due date (by mutual agreement). The holder allows a discount called Rebate on Bills for the early-payment period.
RenewalRetiring
Payment DELAYED beyond original maturityPayment made EARLY, before maturity
Drawee pays extra — interestDrawee is rewarded — gets a Rebate (discount)

Key Terms Glossary — Chapter 8

🔴 Quick-fire revision

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
D‑D‑P = Draws, is Drawn upon, gets Paid
Drawer draws the bill, Drawee is drawn upon (becomes Acceptor once signed), Payee gets paid.
Bill = Order (3 parties); Note = Promise (2 parties)
A Bill of Exchange is an ORDER from creditor to debtor (3 parties). A Promissory Note is a PROMISE from debtor to creditor (2 parties, no acceptance needed).
"3 days of Grace, like 3 cheers"
Always add 3 days of grace to the nominal due date to find the maturity date.
Renewal = Late + Interest; Retiring = Early + Rebate
Renewal pushes payment LATER (drawee pays interest). Retiring brings payment EARLIER (drawee earns a rebate/discount).
Dishonour = "Undo Button"
Whatever the drawer did with the bill (retained/discounted/endorsed/sent for collection), dishonour simply reverses that specific entry, debiting the drawee again.

Last-Day Revision Notes

🔴 Read this 30 minutes before the exam
  1. 01Bill of Exchange = order to pay, drawn by creditor, needs acceptance. Promissory Note = promise to pay, drawn by debtor, no acceptance needed.
  2. 023 parties in a bill: Drawer, Drawee/Acceptor, Payee. Only 2 in a note: Maker, Payee.
  3. 03Add 3 days of grace to find the maturity date; adjust for holidays.
  4. 04Drawer's asset = Bills Receivable; Acceptor's liability = Bills Payable.
  5. 05Discounting = cash from bank early, minus interest. Endorsing = transfer to settle a debt, no cash involved.
  6. 06On maturity, if discounted or endorsed, NO entry is passed by the drawer — the new holder collects.
  7. 07Dishonour reverses the earlier entry: debit the drawee, credit whatever account was originally credited.
  8. 08Renewal = late payment + interest charged to drawee. Retiring = early payment + rebate given to drawee.
  9. 09Promissory note can NEVER be made payable to bearer (RBI Act restriction).
  10. 10Acceptor's entries are the SAME regardless of what the drawer does with the bill (retain/discount/endorse).
