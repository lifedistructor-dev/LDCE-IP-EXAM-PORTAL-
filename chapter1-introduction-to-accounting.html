1.1

Meaning of Accounting

🔴 Very Important for Exam
AICPA (1941): Accounting is the art of recording, classifying and summarising, in a significant manner and in terms of money, transactions and events which are of a financial character, and interpreting the results. American Institute of Certified Public Accountants
AAA (1966): Accounting is the process of identifying, measuring and communicating economic information to permit informed judgements and decisions by users. American Accounting Association
APB (1970): The function of accounting is to provide quantitative financial information about economic entities, useful in making economic decisions. Accounting Principles Board of AICPA

History & Development of Accounting

🟡 Conceptual Understanding
~4000 B.C.
Babylonia & Egypt — wage/tax transactions recorded on clay tablets; Egyptian treasury reports sent daily to Wazirs (PM), monthly to kings.
~2000 B.C.
China develops a sophisticated form of government accounting.
700 B.C.–400 A.D.
Greece apportions government revenue among treasuries; Rome uses a memorandum/daybook posted monthly to ledgers.
~23 centuries ago
India — Kautilya (minister to Chandragupta) writes Arthashastra, describing how accounting records should be maintained.
1494 A.D.
Luca Pacioli (Franciscan friar) publishes Summa de Arithmetica... in Venice — first book describing double-entry bookkeeping. Introduced terms Debit (Italian debito) & Credit (Italian credito).
1941
AICPA defines accounting as an art of recording, classifying, summarising.
1966
AAA redefines accounting as a process of identifying, measuring, communicating.
1970
APB (AICPA) emphasises providing quantitative financial information for decisions.

1.4

Role of Accounting

🟡 Conceptual Understanding
Five roles (Box 4):
  • As a Language — the language of business used to communicate
  • As a Historical Record — chronological record at actual amounts
  • As Current Economic Reality — measures true income / change in wealth
  • As an Information System — links accountant (source) to external users (receivers)
  • As a Commodity — a specialised information service in demand in society

Memory Tricks & Mnemonics

🟢 Quick Revision
I‑M‑R‑C
Accounting process → Identify → Measure → Record → Communicate. Say it as "I Made Rice Curry."
R‑R‑U‑C
Qualitative characteristics → Reliability, Relevance, Understandability, Comparability. "Really Reliable Users Compare."
V‑N‑F
Sub-parts of Reliability → Verifiability, Neutrality, Faithfulness. "Very Neutral Facts."
M‑P‑D‑P
Objectives of Accounting → Maintain records, calculate Profit/Loss, Depict financial position, Provide info to users.
DR owes, CR owed
Debtor owes us money (asset) · Creditor is owed by us (liability). Same first letters, opposite direction — say "D for Due-to-us."
Pacioli 1494 = "Pac-1494"
Luca Pacioli's Summa published in Venice, 1494 — first documented double-entry system. Remember "PAC-man ate 1-4-9-4 apples."
L‑H‑I‑C
Different roles of accounting → Language, Historical record, current economic reality (Income/wealth), Information system, Commodity. Think "LHIC — Language, History, Income, Commodity."

One-Page Revision Sheet

Definitions

  • AICPA 1941 — art of recording, classifying, summarising
  • AAA 1966 — process of identifying, measuring, communicating
  • APB 1970 — provides quantitative financial info

Process (IMRC)

  • Identify → Measure → Record → Communicate

Users

  • Internal: CEO, CFO, managers, supervisors
  • External: investors, creditors, tax authorities, regulators

Branches

  • Financial (past) · Cost (product cost) · Management (future decisions)

Qualitative Char. (RRUC)

  • Reliability (Verifiable, Neutral, Faithful)
  • Relevance (Timely, Predictive, Feedback)
  • Understandability · Comparability

Objectives

  • Maintain records · Calculate P&L · Depict position · Inform users

Key Pairs

  • Debtor (asset) vs Creditor (liability)
  • Trade discount (not recorded) vs Cash discount (recorded)
  • Profit (operations) vs Gain (incidental)

Milestones

  • 4000 BC Babylonia/Egypt · 1494 Pacioli · 1941 AICPA
