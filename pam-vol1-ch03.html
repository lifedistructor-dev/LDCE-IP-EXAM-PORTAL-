3.01–3.04 Accrual Accounting, CSI-SAP, PFMS 🔴 VI▾

Accrual Accounting: Move initiated post-12th Finance Commission recommendations; GASAB (Govt Accounting Standard Advisory Board, under C&AG) gave the roadmap. DoP is transitioning from Cash-Based to Accrual Based Accounting System (ABAS) via Core System Integrator (CSI) — but accounts remain on a real/cash basis; accrual is used only to monitor Receivables/Payables status.

CSI (Core System Integrator): Integrated system for centralised data/application management (Counter Ops, Delivery Ops, Financial activities, HR). Uses ERP-SAP modules: Financial Accounting, Fund Management, Business Intelligence Reporting, Payroll, Inventory Mgmt.

CSI-SAP generates 2 reports: (1) Cash-based, 15-digit CGA HOA → for CGA submission (2) Accrual-based → internal analysis/decision-making. Full head list → Appendix V.

PFMS: Web-based system by CGA, Dept of Expenditure, MoF. CGA report is pushed to e-Lekha through PFMS by PAOs.

🧠 "12FC gave birth to GASAB, GASAB gave the roadmap, CSI-SAP walks it, PFMS delivers it to e-Lekha."

3.05–3.09 Compilation of Accounts (T+3 Flow) 🔴 VI▾

HPO = primary accounting unit, provides cash to RMS/MMS/PMG office/CO/PTC/NCDDOs. In CSI, each Post Office (even Branch Office) = Profit/Cost Centre; transactions classified by GL at source.

Daily account (incl. Sundays/holidays) processed on T+3 basis
↓
GL-wise transaction summary cross-checked vs schedule (debit/credit tally, excl. accrual items)
↓
Major mistakes rectified immediately; others during month of account
↓
Monthly CGA report downloaded & cross-verified vs e-Lekha uploads; over/under-booking rectified via TE in PFMS

Post Office Balances: Physical cash = "DOP cash"; intermediary heads zeroed daily & moved to DOP cash. Previous day's closing balance = next day's opening (never altered).

Abolished HPO: Balance transfer must complete within one month's account — old HPO's balance classified under "Remittances"; new HPO's balance increased by per-contra credit to "Remittances," same month.

March Supplementary Account: Available to post non-cash TEs/error rectification against 31st March; final Journal Entries only by PAO (per Chapter VII rules).

🔴 Very Important date: Last CGA report of each PAO must be completed by 8th of the following month.

Duties of Accountants in PA Section 🔴 VI▾

22 specific checks (a–w) are prescribed. Exam-focus highlights:

CGA report on T+3, register of TE refs maintained GL vs schedule comparison, anomalies → AAO Concurrent review before month closes Every voucher enfaced "Checked" in red ink No payment on subordinate's signature Pay bills unclaimed >2 yrs (GFR 264) need PAO approval Monthly review of Receivables/Payables, Philately, Franking, COD, e-billers

6 special Notes assign day-to-day checking to specific sections: Note-1 GPF/NPS/SDBS; Note-2 Pension/Gratuity/GDS/Terminal benefits; Note-3 Loans & Advances; Note-4 DOP-Banking/RBI/IGA settlement; Note-5 MO/Cash Certificates/IPO; Note-6 IPPB/POSB/ATM/CSC/WUMT/Insurance.

Post-PFMS role of Accountant (SA/JA): clear intermediary balances by month-end; review Receivables/Payables open items; liquidate third-party recovery balances by 10th of following month; audit transactions >₹1000 (excl. registered customers); flag unusual reversals.

Monthly CGA Report, Stamp Balances, GL-to-GL & Computerisation 🟠 FAQ▾

Monthly CGA Report: Generated after month closes; GL-wise figures compared with daily e-Lekha uploads; variations corrected via TE. If Application errors cause inconsistency → Trial Balance figures adopted (deducting open accrual items) till app is fixed.

Postal Stamp & Stationery Balances: Opening inventory + receipts − closing balance = actual sales (reported as CGA sales). Reviewed & reported to DDO/Circle Head monthly.

GL-to-GL Corrections: Once month is freezed, DDO (with Divisional Head approval) sends reversal/correction request to PAO with clear reasons; PAO examines & incorporates. TE register maintained per section — no separate CTLA needed since system access itself validates.

Computerisation: DoP's CSI application (with vertical apps like CBS, McCamish) generates CGA reports pushed to e-Lekha via PFMS.

Documents cross-checked: LPC-in/out, Due-Drawn Difference statement, Pay Revision sanctions, Pay Fixation Memo, allowance sanctions, absentee statement, GDS leave sanctions.

🧠 "8 km rule": Road mileage & Daily Allowance both inadmissible within an 8-km radius of headquarters.

Classes of Contingencies (5 Types) 🔴 VI▾
Contingent Charges
  • 1. Contract Contingencies — lump-sum grant, disbursing officer's discretion
  • 2. Scale Regulated Contingencies — scales fixed by competent authority
  • 3. Special Contingencies — need special sanction each time (superior authority)
  • 4. Countersigned Contingencies — need countersignature (before/after payment)
  • 5. Fully Vouched Contingencies — no sanction/countersignature needed, disbursing officer's own authority

🧠 "C-S-S-C-F" = Contract → Scale → Special → Countersigned → Fully-vouched (decreasing control, increasing officer's own discretion).

Countersigned Contingencies — A) After Payment: Money drawn on abstract bill; approval later via monthly detailed bill (both scrutinised). Detailed bill overdue beyond Rule 118 (CGA R&P Rules 1983) period → reported to Controlling Officer → HoD → Pr. Accounts Office.
B) Before Payment: No abstract bill; entered in 'Special Charges Register' directly.

Sub-voucher cancellation: Duty of PAO (not DDO/Controlling Officer) per Rule 109 CGA(R&P) Rules 1983 & Rule 367-B P&T FHB Vol.I — cancelled by rubber stamp/hand with dated initials of Accountant; post-audit ones also attested by AAO.

Objection Registers & Write-off Powers 🔴 VI▾

3 Objection Registers: (1) HR — employee cost objections incl. GDS (2) Other than HR — non-employee charges (3) IA — Internal Audit objections at field units. Reviewed monthly by Branch Officer & Head of PAO.

Likely Q: What is Sr.DDG(PAF)'s power to forego recovery of irregular expenditure? → ₹250 (simple objection) / ₹500 (where charge itself may be genuine but under-documented).

Records, Review Cycle & Postal Stamp Depot 🟡 Concept▾

Every AAO maintains a Calendar of Returns; SA/JA fill Form DG(PA)-50 progress report, examined every Monday by AAO.

AAO/AO/Sr.AO Review: Independent random check of bills/vouchers — every DDO's every bill category checked ≥ once a year. Watch for: delay in bill submission, evading financial rules, March rush of expenditure, avoiding lapse of grant, misuse of sanctioned grant purpose. Reviewed reports due to Head of Office by 25th of the month.

Postal Stamp Depot (PSD, formerly CSD): Supplies stamps/stationery from India Security Press Nasik → HPOs (treated as DDO inventory; sold = revenue). PSD submits monthly stock statement (opening + receipts − supplied − closing).

Direct Posting: Manual transaction posting outside normal workflow — scrutinised 100%; irregularities → objection register; repeated misuse reported to Head of Region.

Loans & Advances (Head 7610) 🔴 VI▾

Long-term advances (Motor Car, Other Conveyance, Computer, House Building): Subsidiary ledger records sanction + installment-wise disbursement dates for interest calc. Monthly reconciliation with booked figures.

1st installment debit adjusted in books of first Accounts Officer; subsequent installments accumulate there too
↓
Consolidated debit (less recoveries) passed to Accounts Officer of Circle where employee currently works
↓
Recoveries before final installment → adjusted with first AO; after final installment → adjusted in circle where recovered

Transfer of balances done by Postal Accounts (Miscellaneous) Section via broadsheet under 7610 — plus credits (transferring AO) / minus credits (receiving AO); write-off of irrecoverable advances = plus credit only, never minus debit (avoids fund-availability inflation).

Motor Car/Conveyance Advance checks: Sanctioning authority certificate (Form GFR 22/23 agreement signed); comprehensive insurance ≥ outstanding advance+interest, Govt's interest noted with insurer; actual price ≥ advance amount (Rule 420 P&T FHB Vol.I notes); mortgage bond released only after No Dues Certificate.

Short-term advances (TA on transfer, Tour Advance, LTC, Medical advance — recoverable in <60 instalments): DDO maintains detailed accounts; not entered in PAO's objection book, but DDO ensures recovery + interest. Balance acceptance: non-response within 2 months = deemed accepted.

Likely Q: Which section transfers Loans & Advances balances between Circles, and how? → Postal Accounts (Miscellaneous) Section, via broadsheet with plus/minus credits under Head 7610.
