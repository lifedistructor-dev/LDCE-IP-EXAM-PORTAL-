9.2Capital vs Revenue

Very ImportantFrequently Asked
Expenditure: outlay other than settlement of an existing liability. If benefit lasts ≤1 accounting year → Revenue Expenditure. If benefit lasts >1 year → Capital Expenditure.
Is the benefit of expenditure for more than ONE accounting year?
NODay-to-day expense (salary, rent)
Revenue Expenditure
YESAcquiring/extending fixed assets
Capital Expenditure

Special case: heavy advertising benefiting >1 year → Deferred Revenue Expenditure (written off over the benefit period, same treatment as capital expenditure)

💠 Capital Expenditure

  • Increases earning capacity
  • Acquires fixed assets
  • Non-recurring
  • Benefits >1 year
  • Shown in Balance Sheet (subject to depreciation)

💠 Revenue Expenditure

  • Maintains earning capacity
  • Day-to-day conduct of business
  • Recurring
  • Benefits 1 year
  • Shown in Trading & P&L A/c
Receipts: If it creates an obligation to return money (loan, additional capital) or is sale of a fixed asset → Capital Receipt. Otherwise (sales, interest received) → Revenue Receipt.

⚠️ Effect of Wrong Classification

Revenue item wrongly treated as Capital → profit overstated, assets overstated.
Capital item wrongly treated as Revenue → profit understated, assets understated.

🎯 Probable Question: Numerical — "Rs. X of repairs wrongly debited to Machinery A/c; find correct profit." (4–6 marks — very frequent)
Remember: "F.A.R." — Fixed asset acquisition, Accounting period >1 yr, Recurs rarely = Capital Expenditure test.

9.5Operating Profit (EBIT)

Very ImportantFrequently Asked
Definition: Profit from normal business operations only — excludes purely financial items and abnormal items (e.g., loss by fire). Also called Profit Before Interest and Tax.
Sales − (Purchases + Direct Exp.) = Gross Profit
↓
Gross Profit + Other Income − Indirect Expenses = Net Profit
↓
Net Profit + Non-operating Exp. − Non-operating Income = Operating Profit
Worked Example
ItemAmount (Rs.)
Net Profit19,000
+ Non-operating expense (Interest)500
− Non-operating incomeNil
Operating Profit19,500
🎯 Probable Question: MCQ — "Correct chronological order: Gross Profit → Operating Profit → Net Profit? or Gross → Net → Operating?"
Answer: Gross Profit → Net Profit → Operating Profit is the ascertainment order shown in the account, but conceptually Operating Profit ≠ simply mid-way; always apply the formula, don't just guess order.
Remember: "GP→NP→OP, but OP = NP + Non-Op Exp − Non-Op Income" (adjust AFTER net profit).

9.6Balance Sheet

Very Important
Definition: A statement (not an account) showing the financial position of the business — assets, liabilities, and capital — as on a given date. Debit balances not transferred to Trading/P&L → Assets side; Credit balances → Liabilities side.
Format — Balance Sheet (Horizontal)
LiabilitiesAmtAssetsAmt
Capital + Profit…Furniture…
Long-term loan…Cash, Bank…
Sundry creditors…Goodwill, Debtors…
Bank overdraft, Bills payable…Land & Building, Closing Stock…
🏷 8 Key Items in Balance Sheet
1. Current Assets — cash/convertible within a year (debtors, stock, bills receivable)
2. Current Liabilities — payable within a year (bank OD, bills payable, creditors)
3. Fixed Assets — long-term, not for resale (land, plant, furniture)
4. Intangible Assets — Goodwill, Patents, Trademarks
5. Investments — shown at cost; footnote if market price is lower
6. Long-term Liabilities — payable after 1 year
7. Capital — assets minus outside liabilities
8. Drawings — deducted from Capital, not shown as an asset

📐 Order of Permanence

Most permanent asset/liability listed first (Capital → Long-term loan → Creditors; Furniture → Debtors → Bank → Cash).

💧 Order of Liquidity

Order reversed — most liquid (Cash) listed first (Creditors → Loan → Capital; Cash → Bank → Debtors → Furniture).

Marshalling = arranging assets/liabilities in a particular order (permanence or liquidity). Grouping = clubbing similar items under one heading (e.g., "Current Assets").
🎯 Probable Question: "Prepare the Balance Sheet in order of liquidity/permanence with proper grouping." (Frequently combined with the Trading & P&L numerical — 4 marks extra)

🚨 Last-Day Revision Notes

  • Wages → Trading A/c (direct); Salaries → P&L A/c (indirect) — a classic mix-up trap.
  • Deferred revenue expenditure is treated like capital expenditure — written off over its benefit period.
  • Balance Sheet is a statement, never call it an "account" in answers.
  • Drawings reduce Capital — shown by deduction, never as a separate asset.
  • Schedule III of Companies Act 2013 prescribes B/S format only for companies, not sole proprietors.
  • Always double-check: Net Profit transfers to Capital A/c via P&L A/c Dr. → To Capital A/c.
Quick Trick — "CROP": Capital stays (Balance Sheet), Revenue is One-year, P&L absorbs revenue items.
