1 Topic & Definition 🔴 VIP

Topic: Financial Statements are the end products of the accounting process, communicating a company's financial results and position to shareholders and other external parties.

Definition (AICPA): Statements prepared for the purpose of presenting a periodical review of report by management on the status of investment and results achieved, reflecting a combination of recorded facts, accounting principles, and personal judgements.

Two Core Statements: (a) Balance Sheet (position statement, as at a date) and (b) Statement of Profit and Loss (performance over a period). Cash Flow Statement is now also treated as an integral component.

2 Nature of Financial Statements 🟡 Conceptual

4 Pillars of "Nature"
  • Recorded Facts — based on historical cost, not current market price
  • Accounting Conventions — e.g. inventory at cost or market price whichever is lower; materiality for small items
  • Postulates — Going Concern, Money Measurement, Realisation
  • Personal Judgements — depreciation estimates, doubtful debt provisions (guided by Conservatism)
Mnemonic — "RAPP": Recorded facts, Accounting conventions, Postulates, Personal judgements.

4 Balance Sheet — Schedule III Format 🔴 VIP — Highest Weightage

Every company under Companies Act 2013 must prepare its Balance Sheet as per Schedule III (applicable to financial years starting on/after 1 April 2011). Vertical format is mandatory.

I. EQUITY AND LIABILITIES
▼
1. Shareholders' Funds Share Capital + Reserves & Surplus + Money received against Share Warrants
▼
2. Share Application Money Pending Allotment
▼
3. Non-Current Liabilities Long-term borrowings, Deferred tax liabilities, Other LT liabilities, Long-term provisions
▼
4. Current Liabilities Short-term borrowings, Trade payables, Other current liabilities, Short-term provisions
▼ TOTAL (must equal Assets total)
II. ASSETS
▼
1. Non-Current Assets Fixed assets (Tangible/Intangible/CWIP), Non-current investments, Deferred tax assets, LT loans & advances, Other non-current assets
▼
2. Current Assets Current investments, Inventories, Trade receivables, Cash & cash equivalents, ST loans & advances, Other current assets
Mnemonic — Equity/Liabilities order "SSNC": Shareholders' funds → Share application money pending → Non-current liabilities → Current liabilities.

5 Share Capital — Notes to Accounts Disclosures 🟠 Frequently Asked

  • Number of shares outstanding at beginning & end of the reporting period, for each class
  • Rights, preferences, restrictions attached to each class (incl. dividend & capital repayment restrictions)
  • Shares held by holding company / ultimate holding company (incl. subsidiaries/associates)
  • Shareholders holding more than 5% shares — disclosed individually with number held
  • For the preceding 5 years: shares allotted without cash payment, bonus shares issued, shares bought back
Mnemonic — "5 and 5": 5% shareholding threshold for disclosure + 5-year lookback for bonus/buyback/non-cash allotment history.

8 Important Exceptions 🟡 Conceptual

  • Schedule III does not apply to Insurance/Banking companies or companies governed by a special Act's own format.
  • A debit balance in Statement of Profit and Loss must be shown as a negative figure under "Surplus" — not hidden or netted off elsewhere.
  • 'Sundry Debtors'/'Sundry Creditors' are replaced by 'Trade Receivables'/'Trade Payables' — old terms are not acceptable in Schedule III format.
  • Rounding-off is mandatory, not optional, and depends on turnover size (see Time Limits section).

11 Uses & Importance of Financial Statements 🟠 Frequently Asked

  • Report on Stewardship function (management performance vs owner expectation)
  • Basis for fiscal policies (taxation, industrial policy)
  • Basis for granting of credit by banks/institutions
  • Guide for prospective investors (security, liquidity, profitability)
  • Guide to value of investment already made
  • Aids trade associations in developing standard ratios
  • Helps stock exchanges assess transparency & protect investors

12 Limitations of Financial Statements 🔴 VIP

Limitation

  • Do not reflect current situation
  • Assets may not realise stated value
  • Bias (personal judgement-driven)
  • Aggregate, not detailed, information
  • Vital info missing (loss of markets etc.)
  • No qualitative information
  • Only interim reports

Why

  • Based on historical cost, not market price
  • Values reflect unexpired/unamortised cost only
  • Estimates (depreciation, doubtful debts) vary by accountant
  • Users can't drill into specifics
  • Balance Sheet silent on non-monetary risks
  • No data on industrial relations, morale, etc.
  • Snapshot in time — future changes not depicted
Mnemonic — "7 Limitations = CAB-AVNI": Current situation not reflected, Assets may not realise, Bias, Aggregate info only, Vital info missing, No qualitative info, Interim reports only.
